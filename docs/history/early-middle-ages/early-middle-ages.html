Year Who? What? Mathematics Category
500 "Yativrsabha":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/yativrsabha Yativrsabha was born about 500 in India. Yativrsabha was a Jaina mathematician who gave a description of the universe which is of historical importance in understanding Jaina science and mathematics. Yativrsabha died about 570 in India./
505 "Varahamihira":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/varahamihira Varahamihira was born 505 in Kapitthaka, India. Varahamihira was an Indian astrologer whose main work was a treatise on mathematical astronomy which summarised earlier astronomical treatises. He discovered a version of Pascal's triangle and worked on magic squares. Varahamihira died in 587 in India./
580 "Wang Xiaotong":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/wang-xiaotong Wang Xiaotong was born about 580 in China. Wang Xiaotong was a Chinese mathematician and astronomer who was involved in calendar reform and the solution of cubic equations. Wang Xiaotong died about 640 in Chian./
598 "Brahmagupta":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/brahmagupta Brahmagupta was born 598 in (possibly) Ujjain, India. Brahmagupta was the foremost Indian mathematician of his time. He made advances in astronomy and most importantly in number systems including algorithms for square roots and the solution of quadratic equations. Brahmagupta died in 670 in India./
600 "Bhaskara I":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/bhaskara-i Bhaskara I was born about 600 in (possibly) Saurastra (modern Gujarat state), India. Bhaskara I was an Indian mathematicians who wrote commentaries on the work of Aryabhata I. Bhaskara I died about 680 in (possibly) Asmaka, India./
602 "Li Chunfeng":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/li-chunfeng Li Chunfeng was born 602 in Shaanxi province, China. Li Chunfeng was a Chinese mathematician who worked in astronomy and calendar reform. Li Chunfeng died in 670 in Chang'an, China./
720 "Lalla":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/lalla Lalla was born about 720 in India. Lalla was an Indian mathematician who wrote mainly on the application of mathematics to astronomy. Lalla died about 790 in India./
735 "Alcuin of York":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/alcuin-of-york Alcuin of York was born 735 in York, Yorkshire, England. Alcuin was an English mathematician who wrote elementary texts on arithmetic, geometry and astronomy. Alcuin of York died on 19 May 804 in Tours, France./
780 "Abu Ja'far Muhammad ibn Musa Al-Khwarizmi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-ja'far-muhammad-ibn-musa-al-khwarizmi/
800 "al-Abbas ibn Said Al-Jawhari":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/al-abbas-ibn-said-al-jawhari al-Abbas ibn Said Al-Jawhari was born about 800 in possibly Baghdad, Iraq. Al-Jawhari was an Islamic mathematician whose main work was a commentary on Euclid's Elements al-Abbas ibn Said Al-Jawhari died about 860 in possibly Baghdad, Iraq./
800 "Jafar Muhammad Banu Musa":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/jafar-muhammad-banu-musa Jafar Muhammad Banu Musa was born about 800 in Baghdad, Iraq. Jafar Muhammad Banu Musa was one of the three Bana Musa brothers. He wrote about conics. Jafar Muhammad Banu Musa died in after 873 in Iraq./
800 "Govindasvami":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/govindasvami Govindasvami was born about 800 in India. Govindasvami was an Indian mathematical astronomer whose most famous treatise was a commentary on work of Bhaskara I. Govindasvami died about 860 in India./
800 "Mahavira":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/mahavira Mahavira was born about 800 in possibly Mysore, India. Mahavira was an Indian mathematician who extended the mathematics of Brahmagupta. Mahavira died about 870 in India./
801 "Abu Yusuf Yaqub ibn Ishaq al-Sabbah Al-Kindi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-yusuf-yaqub-ibn-ishaq-al-sabbah-al-kindi Abu Yusuf Yaqub ibn Ishaq al-Sabbah Al-Kindi was born about 801 in Kufa, Iraq. Al-Kindi was an Islamic mathematician who wrote on the Indian mumber system as well as geometry and optics. Abu Yusuf Yaqub ibn Ishaq al-Sabbah Al-Kindi died in 873 in Baghdad, Iraq./
805 "Ahmad Banu Musa":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/ahmad-banu-musa Ahmad Banu Musa was born about 805 in Baghdad, Iraq. Ahmad Banu Musa was one of the three Bana Musa brothers. He wrote about mechanics. Ahmad Banu Musa died in after 873 in Baghdad, Iraq./
808 "Hunayn Abu Zayd ibn Ishaq al-Ibadi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/hunayn-abu-zayd-ibn-ishaq-al-ibadi Hunayn Abu Zayd ibn Ishaq al-Ibadi was born 808 in Al-Hirah (now in Iraq). Hunayn ibn Ishaq was an Islamic mathematician who is most important as a translator, making Greek works available to the Islamic mathematicians. Hunayn Abu Zayd ibn Ishaq al-Ibadi died in 873 in Baghdad (now in Iraq)./
810 "al-Hasan Banu Musa":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/al-hasan-banu-musa al-Hasan Banu Musa was born about 810 in Baghdad, Iraq. Al-Hasan Banu Musa was one of the three Bana Musa brothers. He wrote about the ellipse. al-Hasan Banu Musa died in after 873 in Baghdad, Iraq./
820 "Abu Abd Allah Muhammad ibn Isa Al-Mahani":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-abd-allah-muhammad-ibn-isa-al-mahani Abu Abd Allah Muhammad ibn Isa Al-Mahani was born about 820 in Mahan, Kerman, Persia (now Iran). Al-Mahani was an Islamic mathematician who tried to solve some of Archimedes' problems on cutting up spheres. Abu Abd Allah Muhammad ibn Isa Al-Mahani died in 880 in Baghdad, Iraq./
830 "Prthudakasvami":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/prthudakasvami Prthudakasvami was born about 830 in India. Prthudakasvami was an Indian mathematician best known for his work on solving equations. Prthudakasvami died about 890 in India./
835 "Ahmed ibn Yusuf al-Misri":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/ahmed-ibn-yusuf--al-misri/
836 "Al-Sabi Thabit ibn Qurra al-Harrani":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/al-sabi-thabit-ibn-qurra-al-harrani Al-Sabi Thabit ibn Qurra al-Harrani was born 836 in Harran, Mesopotamia (now Turkey). Thabit ibn Qurra was an important Islamic mathematician who worked on number theory, astronomy and statics. Al-Sabi Thabit ibn Qurra al-Harrani died on 18 February 901 in Baghdad, (now in Iraq)./
840 "Sankara Narayana":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/sankara-narayana Sankara Narayana was born about 840 in India. Sankara Narayana was an Indian astronomer and mathematician. He wrote a commentary on the work of Bhaskara I. Sankara Narayana died about 900 in India./
850 "Abu Abdallah Mohammad ibn Jabir Al-Battani":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-abdallah-mohammad-ibn-jabir-al-battani Abu Abdallah Mohammad ibn Jabir Al-Battani was born about 850 in Harran (near Urfa), Mesopotamia (now Turkey). Al-Battani or Albategnius was an Islamic astronomer and mathematician. He made important accurate measurements of the stars, moon and planets. His measurements and methods were used by later astronomers. Abu Abdallah Mohammad ibn Jabir Al-Battani died in 929 in Qasr al-Jiss, Iraq./
850 "Abu Kamil Shuja ibn Aslam ibn Muhammad ibn Shuja":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-kamil-shuja-ibn-aslam-ibn-muhammad-ibn-shuja Abu Kamil Shuja ibn Aslam ibn Muhammad ibn Shuja was born about 850 in (possibly) Egypt. Abu Kamil Shuja was an Islamic mathematician. He was one of Al-Khwarismi's successors and applied algebraic methods to geometric problems. Abu Kamil Shuja ibn Aslam ibn Muhammad ibn Shuja died about 930./
865 "Abu'l Abbas al-Fadl ibn Hatim Al-Nayrizi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu'l-abbas-al-fadl-ibn-hatim-al-nayrizi/
870 "Sridhara":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/sridhara Sridhara was born about 870 in possibly Bengal, India. Sridhara was an Indian mathematician who wrote on practical applications of algebra and was one of the first to give a formula for solving quadratic equations. Sridhara died about 930 in India./
880 "Abu Said Sinan ibn Thabit ibn Qurra":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-said-sinan-ibn-thabit-ibn-qurra Abu Said Sinan ibn Thabit ibn Qurra was born about 880. Sinan ibn Thabit was the son and father of other Islamic mathematicians. He is more important for his contributions to medicine than to mathematics. Abu Said Sinan ibn Thabit ibn Qurra died in 943 in Baghdad, (now in Iraq)./
900 "Abu Jafar Muhammad ibn al-Hasan Al-Khazin":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-jafar-muhammad-ibn-al-hasan-al-khazin Abu Jafar Muhammad ibn al-Hasan Al-Khazin was born about 900 in Khurasan (eastern Iran). Al-Khazin was an Islamic mathematician who worked on number theory and astronomy. Abu Jafar Muhammad ibn al-Hasan Al-Khazin died about 971 in possibly Rayy./
920 "Abu'l Hasan Ahmad ibn Ibrahim Al-Uqlidisi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu'l-hasan-ahmad-ibn-ibrahim-al-uqlidisi/
920 "Aryabhata II":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/aryabhata-ii Aryabhata II was born about 920 in India. Aryabhata II was an Indian mathematician who wrote about astronomy as well as geometry. He constructed tables of sines accurate up to about 5 figures. Aryabhata II died about 1000 in India./
940 "Mohammad Abu'l-Wafa Al-Buzjani":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/mohammad-abu'l-wafa-al-buzjani/
940 "Abu Mahmud Hamid ibn al-Khidr Al-Khujandi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-mahmud-hamid-ibn-al-khidr-al-khujandi Abu Mahmud Hamid ibn al-Khidr Al-Khujandi was born about 940 in Khudzhand, Tajikistan. Al-Khujandi was an Islamic astronomer and mathematician. He made accurate astronomical observations and may have made some advances in trigonometry. Abu Mahmud Hamid ibn al-Khidr Al-Khujandi died in 1000./
940 "Abu Sahl Waijan ibn Rustam al-Quhi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-sahl-waijan-ibn-rustam-al-quhi Abu Sahl Waijan ibn Rustam al-Quhi was born about 940 in Tabaristan (now Mazanderan), Persia (now Iran). Al-Quhi was an Islamic mathematician who was a leading figure in the revival and continuation of Greek geometry. Abu Sahl Waijan ibn Rustam al-Quhi died about 1000./
940 "Vijayanandi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/vijayanandi Vijayanandi was born about 940 in Benares (now Varanasi), India. Vijayanandi was an Indian mathematician and astronomer who made some contributions to trigonometry. Vijayanandi died about 1010 in India./
945 "Abu Said Ahmad ibn Muhammad Al-Sijzi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-said-ahmad-ibn-muhammad-al-sijzi Abu Said Ahmad ibn Muhammad Al-Sijzi was born about 945 in Sijistan, Persia (now Iran). Al-Sijzi was an Islamic astronomer and mathematician who wrote on the geometry of spheres. Abu Said Ahmad ibn Muhammad Al-Sijzi died about 1020./
946 "Gerbert of Aurillac":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/gerbert-of-aurillac Gerbert of Aurillac was born 946 in Belliac, Auvergne, France. Gerbert of Aurillac or Pope Syvester II was a talented French mathematics teacher who popularised the Indo-Arabic numerals. Gerbert of Aurillac died on 12 May 1003 in Rome, Italy./
950 "Abu'l-Hasan Ali ibn Abd al-Rahman ibn Yunus":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu'l-hasan-ali-ibn-abd-al-rahman-ibn-yunus/
953 "Abu Bekr ibn Muhammad ibn al-Husayn Al-Karaji":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-bekr-ibn-muhammad-ibn-al-husayn-al-karaji Abu Bekr ibn Muhammad ibn al-Husayn Al-Karaji was born on 13 April 953 in Baghdad (now in Iraq). Al-Karaji was an Islamic mathematician who wrote about the work of earlier mathematicians and who can be regarded as the first person to free algebra from geometrical operations and replace them with the type of operations which are at the core of algebra today. Abu Bekr ibn Muhammad ibn al-Husayn Al-Karaji died about 1029./
965 "Abu Ali al-Hasan ibn al-Haytham":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-ali-al-hasan-ibn-al-haytham Abu Ali al-Hasan ibn al-Haytham was born 965 in (possibly) Basra, Persia (now Iraq). Al-Haytham is also known as Alhazen. He was an Islamic mathematician who wrote early works on optics as well as geometry and number theory. Abu Ali al-Hasan ibn al-Haytham died in 1040 in (possibly) Cairo, Egypt./
970 "Abu Nasr ibn Ali ibn Iraq Mansur":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-nasr-ibn-ali-ibn-iraq-mansur-/
971 "Kushyar ibn Labban":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/kushyar-ibn-labban Kushyar ibn Labban was born 971 in Gilan (now in Iran). Kushyar ibn Labban was a Persian astronomer who wrote an early work on Hindu numerals. Kushyar ibn Labban died in 1029 in Baghdad (now in Iraq)./
973 "Abu Arrayhan Muhammad ibn Ahmad al-Biruni":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-arrayhan-muhammad-ibn-ahmad-al-biruni Abu Arrayhan Muhammad ibn Ahmad al-Biruni was born on 15 September 973 in Kath, Khwarazm (now Kara-Kalpakskaya, Uzbekistan). Al-Biruni is one of the major figures of Islamic mathematics. He contributed to astronomy, mathematics, physics, medicine and history. Abu Arrayhan Muhammad ibn Ahmad al-Biruni died on 13 December 1048 in Ghazna (now Ghazni, Afganistan)./
980 "Abu Mansur ibn Tahir Al-Baghdadi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-mansur-ibn-tahir-al-baghdadi Abu Mansur ibn Tahir Al-Baghdadi was born about 980 in Baghdad, Iraq. Al-Baghdadi was an Islamic mathematician who wrote about different systems of arithmetic in a work of great importance in the history of mathematics. Abu Mansur ibn Tahir Al-Baghdadi died in 1037./
980 "Abu Ali al-Husain ibn Abdallah ibn Sina Avicenna":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-ali-al-husain-ibn-abdallah-ibn-sina-(avicenna)/
989 "Abu Abd Allah Muhammad ibn Muadh Al-Jayyani":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-abd-allah-muhammad-ibn-muadh-al-jayyani Abu Abd Allah Muhammad ibn Muadh Al-Jayyani was born 989 in Cordoba, Andalusia (now Spain). Al-Jayyani was an Islamic mathematician working in Spain. He wrote important commentaries on Euclid's Elements and he wrote the first treatise on spherical trigonometry. Abu Abd Allah Muhammad ibn Muadh Al-Jayyani died in after 1079 in possibly Jaén, Andalusia (now Spain)./
