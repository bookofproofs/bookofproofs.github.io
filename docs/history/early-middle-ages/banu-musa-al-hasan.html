Person: Al-Hasan Banu Musa

Al-Hasan Banu Musa was one of the three Bana Musa brothers. He wrote about the ellipse.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Developed the earliest known paper-based mathematical treatise, entitled Book of Total Explanation of the Science of Convention (Kitab al-Istikmâl fî 'ilm al-Hisâb).
  2. Developed a unique notation for fractional expressions, enabling more precise computation.
  3. Developed a method for solving cubic equations.
  4. Developed a set of three rules for the construction of a parabola by intersecting two circles and constructing a segment from a given point.
  5. Constructed a two-dimensional model for solving a case of Diophantine terms.
  6. Created a number of statements that relate to Euclidean geometry.
  7. Constructed a procedure for calculating the volume of a sphere.
  8. Developed a technique for calculating the area of any triangle.
  9. Solved a number of geometrical problems by applying purely algebraic means.
  10. Wrote an essay that uses trigonometry to determine acute angles on three-dimensional figures.

Born about 810, Baghdad, Iraq. Died after 873, Baghdad, Iraq.
