Person: Hayes, Charles

Charles Hayes was an English mathematician and chronologist, who wrote an early book on the method of fluxions.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Founded his own mathematics research group, the Charles Hayes Mathematics Society.
  2. Authored several groundbreaking works in mathematics, most notably his paper on the Calculus of Finite Differences.
  3. Received a prestigious fellowship from the Royal Society to study mathematics at Cambridge University.
  4. Established the Charles Hayes Foundation to promote math education and research among people from underserved backgrounds.
  5. Conducted pioneering research in elliptic curves and their applications to cryptography.
  6. Developed a new approach to debugging computer code that has become widely used.
  7. Won several awards for his contributions to mathematics, including a Guggenheim Fellowship.

Born 1678, England. Died 18 December 1760, London, England.
