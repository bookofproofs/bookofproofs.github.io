Person: Praeger, Cheryl Elisabeth

Cheryl Praeger is an Australian mathematician who has applied the ideas of symmetry to a wide variety of mathematical problems.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. First female professor of Mathematics in Australia, appointed in 1992 at the University of Western Australia.
  2. First female President (1996–1998) of the Australian Mathematical Society.
  3. In 2001, initiated the Cherilyn Praeger Visiting Professors Program to bring international mathematicians to Australia.
  4. In 2003, was appointed a Companion of the Order of Australia, one of the highest civil honors.
  5. In 2005, was awarded the Australia Prize, one of the highest scientific awards in Australia.
  6. In 2011, became the first female mathematician to be elected as an honorary member of the Royal Society of Western Australia.
  7. In 2015, became the first Australian woman to be elected an honorary member of the London Mathematical Society.
  8. In 2018, was honored with a special lecture in her name, called ‘The Cheryl E.P. Praeger Lecture on Finite Geometry’ at the British Mathematical Colloquium, held at Cardiff University.
  9. In 2019, was inducted into the WA Women’s Hall of Fame, becoming only the second mathematician to be inducted.

Born 7 September 1948, Toowoomba, Queensland, Australia.
