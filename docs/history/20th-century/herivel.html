Person: Herivel, John William Jamieson

John Herivel was an Irish mathematician and historian of science who was a former World War II codebreaker at Bletchley Park.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Highly influential in the development of Bletchley Park, a Britain-based code-breaking center during World War II;
  2. Notably solved the Banburismus procedure, a code-breaking technique utilized by British code-breakers to decipher the Nazi Enigma machine;
  3. Co-developed the Herivel Tip, a code-breaking technique which used existing clues to decrypt Enigma messages;
  4. Co-invented the Vertical Enigma, a mechanical device used to break codes;
  5. Pioneered the application of mathematical astronomy to code-breaking;
  6. Investigated mathematical sequence lengths, helping cryptanalysis at Bletchley Park.

Born 29 August 1918, Belfast, Ireland. Died 18 January 2011, Oxford, England.
