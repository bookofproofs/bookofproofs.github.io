Person: Sadosky (2), Cora

Cora Sadosky was born in Argentina and became Professor of Mathematics at Howard University in the United States. She wrote over fifty papers in harmonic analysis and operator theory. She promoted women in mathematics as well encouraging greater participation of African-Americans in mathematics.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Founding member and first Executive Director of the Argentine Association of Women Scientists and Technologists (AMCYT).
  2. Being selected by the UNESCO/Keizo Obuchi Research Fellowships Programme as one of the two South American researchers for 1997-1998.
  3. Being awarded the Konex Prize in Mathematics in 2017.
  4. Becoming the first woman professor of mathematics of the University of Buenos Aires in 2010.
  5. Being the first Argentinian to win the international Emil Artin Mathematical Prize in 1990.
  6. Being elected to the National Academy of Exact, Physical and Natural Sciences of Argentina in 1995.
  7. Presenting numerous scientific papers in International Conferences and Journals.
  8. Participating in the International Congress of Mathematicians in 1958 as the only woman in the South American delegation.
  9. Receiving an Honorary Doctorate from the University of Buenos Aires in 1999.
  10. Appointed by the Inter-American Development Bank to lead team of experts working on the set-up of the Women in Science and Technology Program in Latin America and the Caribbean in 1994.

Born 23 May1940, Buenos Aires, Argentina. Died 3 December 2010, Long Beach, California, USA.
