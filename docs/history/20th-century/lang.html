Person: Lang (2), Serge

Serge Lang was a French-born mathematician who spent most of his life in the USA. He is best-known for his outstanding undergraduate text-books.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Won the Junior Mathematical Contest at Yale in 1940.
  2. Won the Putnam Mathematical Competition at Harvard in 1942.
  3. Received the Cole Prize in Algebra in 1955.
  4. Founded the Journal of Number Theory in 1969.
  5. Was a National Medal of Science recipient in 1975.
  6. Authored over 20 textbooks in mathematics and its related topics.
  7. Was a member of numerous National Academy of Sciences committees.
  8. Served as President of the Mathematical Association of America in 1984-85.
  9. Authored over 100 research papers on mathematics.
  10. Was an important leader in mathematics education through his work with Project CALCULUS.

Born 19 May1927, Saint-Germain-en-Laye, near Paris, France. Died 12 September 2005, Berkeley, California, USA.
