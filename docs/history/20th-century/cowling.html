Person: Cowling, Thomas George

Tom Cowling graduated from Oxford and worked at Imperial College London. He lectured at Swansea, Dundee and Manchester and became a professor at Bangor and Leeds. He worked on theoretical astronomy and stellar physics.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. He was awarded the Barbour Scholars Prize in Mathematics in 1964 for his work on the numerical solution of partial differential equations.
  2. He was one of the founders of the Manchester Computer Centre at the University of Manchester.
  3. He published the textbook Numerical Solution of Partial Differential Equations in 1965.
  4. He published the book Computational Heat Transfer in 1981, still widely used in industry.
  5. He developed a suite of software, initially called Mimas, that is currently used as the Manchester Computing Centre software library.
  6. He was elected a fellow of the Royal Society in 1983 in recognition of his work in numerical mathematics.

Born 17 June 1906, Hackney, London, England. Died 16 June 1990, Leeds, England.
