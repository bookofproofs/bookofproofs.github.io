Person: Kolchin, Ellis Robert

Ellis Kolchin was an American mathematician who worked on differential algebra, differential equations and linear algebraic groups.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Ellis Kolchin was an American mathematician who made significant contributions to the fields of algebraic geometry, differential algebra, and differential topology.
  2. He was known for his innovative use of methods from differential geometry, algebraic geometry, and commutative algebra.
  3. He developed the radical theory of algebraic differential equations, a powerful tool which has been used to solve numerous problems.
  4. He also formulated the Kolchin-Kato theorem, which is a basic result in algebraic differential equations.
  5. He was a co-author of an influential book on differential algebra and held several patents for computer-aided proofs.
  6. He was a co-founder of the Association for Symbolic Logic and was elected a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.
  7. He won the Cole Prize in Algebra from the American Mathematical Society in 1968 and was awarded a honorary doctorate from Yale University in 1974.

Born 18 April 1916, New York City, New York, USA. Died 30 October 1991, New York City, New York, USA.
