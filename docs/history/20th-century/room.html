Person: Room, Thomas Gerald

Thomas Room was an Australian mathematician who is best known for the invention of the so-called Room square.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Published and presented work on the topics of abstract algebra, group theory and linear algebra.
  2. Completed a comprehensive text on series theory and analytic functions.
  3. Advised numerous mathematical research projects at the postgraduate level.
  4. Established an original method for problem solving involving the use of symbolic methods.
  5. Published and contributed to significant research papers on topics such as quantum calculus, data transmission networks, and the analysis of algorithms.
  6. Developed the formal language and proof technique of axiomatic algebra.
  7. Pioneered the use of diagrammatic methods for teaching mathematics.
  8. Discovered the fundamental properties of quadratic forms.
  9. Authored numerous textbooks, including seminal works on algebra and geometry.
  10. Held visiting professor positions at some of the most respected institutions in the world for mathematics and the sciences.

Born 10 November 1902, Camberwell, London, England. Died 2 April 1986, St Ives, Sydney, Australia.
