Person: Granville, Evelyn Boyd

Evelyn Boyd Granville was only the second African-American woman to receive a PhD in mathematics from an American University. She worked in computing.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Became the second African-American woman to receive a Ph.D. in Mathematics from an American University in 1949.
  2. Served as a mathematician for the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA) for nine years, and for NASA for 21 years.
  3. Was named one of the Women in Science Hall of Fame inductees in 2009.
  4. Developed algorithms for use in the Apollo space program.
  5. Authored a widely circulated monograph analyzing the rate of convergence of certain iterative methods.
  6. Developed efficient numerical integration algorithms that are well known and widely used to this day.
  7. Recognized with a Lifetime Achievement Award from the Association for Women in Mathematics in 1991.
  8. Founded GirlTECH (now called GirlsGetIT), an international program aimed at increasing young women’s ambition and interest in STEM fields and computer science.

Born 1 May1924, Washington D.C., USA.
