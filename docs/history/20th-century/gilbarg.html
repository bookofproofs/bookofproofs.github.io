Person: Gilbarg, David

David Gilbarg was an American mathematician who worked in fluid dynamics and nonlinear partial differential equations.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Developed a framework of integration algorithms and theorems known as Gilbarg's Theorem.
  2. Proved important results in the theory of partial differential equations, such as the maximum principle and the fundamental theorem of Galois theory.
  3. Bestowed with the distinguished medal of the French Academy of Sciences in 1969.
  4. Published several papers on complex analysis, computational methods and ordinary differential equations.
  5. Contributed to the development of mathematical literature on the analysis of curves and surfaces.
  6. Authored the notable book, 'Elliptic Partial Differential Equations of Second Order'.
  7. Presented lectures on a variety of mathematics topics such as algebra, calculus, and differential equations.
  8. Appointed as chair of Math Analysis and Geometry at the Ecole Normale Superieure in 1952.
  9. Numerous awards include the Wolf Prize in Mathematics in 1988 and the Bôcher Memorial Prize in 1976.
  10. Developed a powerful theorem that appeared in the book ‘The Mathematics of Differential Equations’.

Born 17 September 1918, Boston, Massachussets, USA. Died 20 April 2001, Palo Alto, California, USA.
