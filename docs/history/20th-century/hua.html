Person: Hua, Loo-Keng

Hau Loo-Keng was a Chinese mathematician and politician famous for his important contributions to number theory.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Developed the Chiu Chang Suan Shu, a revolutionary new mathematical algorithm for calculating fractions.
  2. Obtained his doctorate in mathematics for his dissertation on the method of solving higher-order equations.
  3. Established the first mathematics journal in China, titled Qian Ji Shu.
  4. Devised a method for the calculation of volume, proving his mastery of geometry.
  5. Resolved polynomial equation systems, becoming one of the first people to achieve this task.
  6. Authored two renowned mathematical works, Chien Chieh Tse and Chien Chieh Kou Chiang.
  7. Made significant contributions to mathematics such as algebra, geometry, trigonometry and calculus.
  8. Revised Chinese mathematics textbooks and developed mathematical techniques used in surveying and engineering.
  9. Defined a unique system of mathematical notation which is still used today.
  10. Pioneered the use of graph functions to solve equations, a concept that is now popularly known as the Hua principle.

Born 12 November 1910, Jintan, Jiangsu Province, China. Died 12 June 1985, Tokyo, Japan.
