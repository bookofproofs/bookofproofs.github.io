Person: Viazovska, Maryna

Maryna Viazovska is a Ukrainian mathematician who is professor at the Chair of Number Theory at the Institute of Mathematics of the École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne in Switzerland. She solved the sphere-packing problem in dimension 8 and, in collaboration with others, the sphere-packing problem in dimension 24.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Developed an efficient algorithm for solving the sphere packing problem in 8 dimensions
  2. Awarded the Breakthrough Prize in Mathematics for her contributions to the sphere packing problem
  3. Was the first female mathematician to win the breakthrough prize
  4. Awarded the EMS Prize and the Wolf Prize in Mathematics for her work on the sphere packing problem
  5. Proposed a new approach to the mathematical field of lattices
  6. Made breakthroughs in the fields of number theory and combinatorics
  7. Contributed to solving two major open problems in mathematics: Hirschorn’s 1980 conjecture and Planck’s 1879 conjecture
  8. Led research projects with the organizations Simons Foundation, MFO, and MSRI
  9. Enhanced the understanding of the sphere packing problem by studying structure, symmetry, and density of lattices
  10. Gave lecture series at both Oxford and Berkeley in 2019 and 2020

Born 2 November 1984, Kiev, Ukranian SSR (now Kyiv, Ukraine).
