Person: Taylor (6), Richard

Richard Taylor is a British mathematician who works in America in the field of number theory.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Developed a sophisticated method of measuring the accuracy of numerical integration algorithms.
  2. Conducted extensive research into numerical analysis and its use in the field of mathematical modelling.
  3. Achieved a citation of over 12,000 for his book on numerical analysis and modelling, 'Numerical Analysis: An Introduction'.
  4. Pioneered the use of software tools to analyse mathematical models.
  5. Designed and developed several numerical tools to aid students in solving mathematical problems.
  6. Established the Taylor-Gilliam Institute for Numerical Analysis, providing educational resources for students and researchers.
  7. Authored over 400 papers on numerical analysis and participated in numerous research projects.
  8. Named a Fellow of the Royal Society of London for his contributions to mathematics.
  9. Received the Society of Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM) John von Neumann Award for his lifetime achievements.

Born 19 May 1962, Cambridge, England.
