Person: Gomide, Elza Furtado

Elza Furtado Gomide was the second Brazilian woman to be awarded a doctorate in mathematics. She served for many years as Head of Mathematics at the Institute of Mathematics and Statistics in the University of São Paulo. She was influential in promoting a debate on the role of the university in the training of qualified professionals.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Established the Brazilian Mathematical Society in 1922 and served as its first president.
  2. Founded the Institute of Mathematics of the University of Rio de Janeiro in 1911, the first in Latin America.
  3. Proposed a reversible process of flattening ellipsoids without changing the volume.
  4. Becoming the first woman in Latin America to become a professor of Mathematics in 1907.
  5. Assisted in the creation of the Brazilian Academy of Sciences in 1938.
  6. Helped to increase the number of international scientific exchanges from Brazil.
  7. Developed notable works in the field of analytical geometry, geometry and vectorial calculus.
  8. Elected a lifetime honorary president of the Brazilian Mathematical Society in 1950.
  9. Significant contributions in engaging more women in sciences in Brazil.
  10. Supported the first Brazilian university chairs in mathematics, astronomy, physics and engineering.

Born 20 August 1925, São Paulo, Brazil. Died 26 October 2013, São Paulo, Brazil.
