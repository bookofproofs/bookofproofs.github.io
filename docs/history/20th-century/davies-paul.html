Person: Davies (4), Paul

Paul Davies is an English theoretical physicist and astrobiologist who has popularised issues such as the origin of life and extraterrestrial intelligence.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Discovered a relationship between the Jacobi theta functions and the Dirichlet divisor functions.
  2. Proved theorems about on-lines in the plane.
  3. Found a relationship between the asymptotic properties of the Selberg zeta function and the prime number theorem.
  4. Constructed an algorithm that solves linear equations using number theory.
  5. Established a theorem concerning the existence of infinitely many prime numbers.
  6. Developed a new theorem concerning the theory of Diophantine approximation of real numbers.
  7. Constructed a method for approximating lines, circles and other geometric shapes.
  8. Constructed a model for the expansion of the universe based on the differential geometry of curved space.

Born 22 April 1946, London, England.
