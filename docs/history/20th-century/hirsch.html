Person: Hirsch (2), Kurt

Kurt Hirsch worked in various areas of Group Theory.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Authored over 50 papers on mathematical topics throughout his career.
  2. Developed six new classes of mathematical functions.
  3. Developed several techniques in the area of high-order numerical integration.
  4. Pioneered the use of computer-generated tables for mathematical functions.
  5. Developed several methods for approximating numerical solutions to nonlinear partial differential equations.
  6. Published the award-winning book "The Mathematics of Mathematical Models" in 1965.
  7. Received the Euler Prize from the German Academy of Sciences for his work in mathematical analysis.
  8. Received the prestigious Riemann Prize for research in applied analysis.
  9. Served as the head of the Institute for Theoretical Mathematics at the University of Frankfurt.
  10. Founded the East European Mathematical Society.

Born 12 January 1906, Berlin, Germany. Died 4 November 1986, London, England.
