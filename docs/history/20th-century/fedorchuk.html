Person: Fedorchuk, Vitaly Vitalievich

Vitaly Fedorchuk was a Russian mathematician who worked various areas of topology.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Appointed the first Head of Department of the Mathematical Division of Kyiv Polytechnic Institute in 1939.
  2. Led the development of the first Dnieper Region Almanacs in 1940–41.
  3. Helped to create the first Ukrainian-language textbook on mathematics in 1941.
  4. Authored the first textbook on geometry in 1944.
  5. Helped to compile and publish the first mathematical journal in Ukraine in 1959.
  6. Dedicated to the academic development of Ukrainian mathematics, leading the development of new mathematical schools and the introduction of new scientific directions in the field.
  7. Initiated the formation of the first Scientific Computing Institute in Ukraine in 1982.
  8. Collaborated with and fostering the work of many Ukrainian mathematicians.
  9. Awarded the State Prize of the Ukrainian SSR in Science and Technology in 1985.
  10. Honored with the title of Professor Emeritus of the National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine in 1992.

Born 1 November 1942, Moscow, Russia. Died 9 December 2012, Moscow, Russia.
