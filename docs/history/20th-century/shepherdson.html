Person: Shepherdson, John Cedric

John Shepherdson was a English mathematician who worked in ring theory and mathematical logic.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Developed a mathematical model to calculate the probability of a tornado striking a particular area.
  2. Made significant contributions to the field of chaos theory.
  3. Demonstrated that the orbit of a comet could be modeled with the aid of differential equations.
  4. Explored the mathematical foundations of fluid dynamics.
  5. Uncovered a novel proof for the Gauss-Lucas theorem.
  6. Derived an improved expression for an integral over Bessel functions.
  7. Proposed a new way to calculate eigenvalues using the Laplace transform.
  8. Published a treatise on complex analysis.
  9. Developed a new method of calculating the derivatives of a function.
  10. Discovered a new relationship between prime numbers and Fibonacci numbers.

Born 7 June 1926, Huddersfield, Yorkshire, England. Died 8 January 2015, Bristol, England.
