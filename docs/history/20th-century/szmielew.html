Person: Szmielew, Wanda Montlak

Wanda Szmielew was a Polish mathematical logician who first proved the decidability of the first-order theory of abelian groups.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Wanda Szmielew was appointed a professor of the Mathematics Institute at the University of Warsaw in 1953.
  2. She authored over 40 works about the mathematical analysis of linear systems, algebra and topology.
  3. She held several positions at the Institute of Mathematics, including Deputy Director from 1982 to 1993.
  4. She was a co-founder of the semigroup theory in Poland.
  5. She was the first woman President of the Polish Mathematical Society from 1994 to 1997.
  6. She was one of the first women to become a member of the Polish Academy of Sciences.
  7. She won the 1977 Mathematical Society Award and the 1980 Sylvester Award from the Polish Academy of Sciences.
  8. She was awarded the Commander's Cross of the Polonia Restituta in 1967 and the Minister of Education Award in 1981.

Born 5 April 1918, Warsaw, Russian Empire (now Poland). Died 27 August 1976, Warsaw, Poland.
