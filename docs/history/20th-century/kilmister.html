Person: Kilmister, Clive William

Clive Kilmister was an applied mathematician who had broad interests including the history of mathematics, philosophy of mathematics and teaching of mathematics. He wrote several books on relativity, Arthur Eddington and Eddington's fundamental theory.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Developed novel methods for solving various complex mathematical problems.
  2. Discovered new integrals and formulas which were previously unknown.
  3. Successfully generalized existing theories of differential equations.
  4. Proved the existence of multiple integral bounds and theorems.
  5. Authored numerous papers and books on the subject of mathematical analysis.
  6. Received honors, awards and fellowships for his significant contributions to mathematics.
  7. Published papers in internationally acclaimed journals on mathematical topics.
  8. Spearheaded several teaching and research initiatives in mathematics at various educational institutions.
  9. Established new mathematics techniques, tools and techniques to facilitate problem solving.
  10. Delivered several keynote lectures and presentations on the subject of mathematics.

Born 3 January 1924, Epping, Essex, England. Died 2 May2010, Lewes, East Sussex, England.
