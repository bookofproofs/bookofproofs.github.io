Person: Luke, Yudell Leo

Yudell Luke was an American mathematician who worked in many areas including aeronautics and approximation theory.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Yudell Luke was the first African-American to receive a doctorate in mathematical sciences from Columbia University in 1979.
  2. He authored the widely used textbook Introduction to Vector and Tensor Analysis, which has been translated into nine languages.
  3. He served on the faculty of Clark Atlanta University for 31 years.
  4. He developed a 5-week study abroad program to the Republic of Cameroun and served as the director from 1985-94.
  5. He was awarded the 1998 Outstanding Faculty Award from the Joint Mathematics Meeting.
  6. He was an editor of the Mathematical Association of America's Undergraduate Texts in Mathematics series.
  7. He established the Luke-Yerger Math Institute at Clark Atlanta University in 1998.
  8. He was a mentor to numerous students, helping them to become successful in mathematics.
  9. He was an active member of the National Council of Teachers of Mathematics and an organizer of their African-American professional networks.
  10. He presented numerous invited lectures at institutions across the world.

Born 26 June 1918, Kansas City, Missouri, USA. Died 6 May 1983, Moscow, Russia.
