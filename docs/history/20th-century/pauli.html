Person: Pauli, Wolfgang Ernst

Wolfgang Pauli won a Nobel prize for his work on quantum mechanics.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Developed the exclusion principle, which lies at the heart of quantum mechanics and explains the characteristic properties of atoms.
  2. Derived the Thomas-Fermi theory that is used to predict the physical properties of atoms.
  3. Proposed the Pauli exclusion principle, which governs the behaviour of elementary particles.
  4. Proposed the Pauli-Villars regularization, a regularization method for divergent integrals in quantum field theory.
  5. Put forward the Pauli-Lubanski vector, a mathematical tool for describing the motion of a single particle in quantum mechanics.
  6. Co-discovered the spin-statistics theorem, which explains the relationship between the spin of particles and their quantum statistics.
  7. Co-derived the nuclear shell model, which explains the structure of atomic nuclei.

Born 25 April 1900, Vienna, Austria. Died 15 December 1958, Zürich, Switzerland.
