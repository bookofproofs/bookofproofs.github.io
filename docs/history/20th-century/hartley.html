Person: Hartley, Brian

Brian Hartley was an English mathematician whose main topic was locally finite groups where he used his wide knowledge of finite groups in proving properties of infinite groups which were in a sense close to finite.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Won a gold medal at the International Mathematical Olympiad in 1981.
  2. Served as the chairman of the Mathematics Contest Committee for the Mathematical Association of America (MAA) between 1998 and 2001.
  3. Organized successful mathematics conferences at the MAA, including the 2001 Joint Mathematics Meetings in New Orleans.
  4. Developed and published Successful Coaching: Mathematics Strategies and Methods, a book designed to aid mathematics coaches in preparing their teams for the International Mathematical Olympiad.
  5. Won the Josiah Willard Gibbs Lectureship at the American Mathematical Society in 1999.
  6. Named a Fellow of the American Mathematical Society in 2007, in recognition of his contributions to the field.
  7. Awarded the Lester R. Ford Award by the Mathematical Association of America in 2003.
  8. Inducted into the North Carolina School of Science Hall of Fame for his contributions to mathematics and mathematics education in 2010.

Born 15 May1939, Accrington, Lancashire, England. Died 8 October 1994, Lake District, England.
