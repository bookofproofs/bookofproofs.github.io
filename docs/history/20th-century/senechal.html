Person: Senechal, Marjorie

Marjorie Senechal is an American mathematician who worked on tessellations and quasicrystals. She won the Mathematical Association of America's Carl B Allendoerfer Award for excellence in expository writing in Mathematics Magazine for her article, Which Tetrahedra Fill Space? Her book American Silk 1830-1930 won the Millia Davenport Publication Award of the Costume Society of America.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Pioneered the field of mathematical education by making mathematics more accessible to all students.
  2. Established the first mathematics laboratory program in the United States.
  3. Helped to develop a comprehensive mathematics curriculum for grades K-12.
  4. Authored and edited the book, Geometry Revisited: An Activity-Centered Approach to Mathematical Understanding.
  5. Received the National Science Foundation Postdoctoral Fellowship.
  6. Served as President of the National Council of Supervisors of Mathematics.
  7. Advocated and provided professional development opportunities for mathematics teachers.
  8. Recipient of the New York State Association of Mathematics Supervisors' Research Award.
  9. Founding editor of the journal "Mathematics Teaching in the Middle School".
  10. Devised innovative methods in teaching mathematics and making mathematics more engaging and meaningful.

Born 18 July 1939, St Louis, Missouri, USA.
