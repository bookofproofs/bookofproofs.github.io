Person: Ouedraogo, Marie Françoise

Marie Françoise Ouedraogo became the first female doctorate from Burkina Faso. She is President of the African Women Mathematicians Association, and a member of IMU Women in Mathematics Advisory Group.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Developed a novel method for solving complex partial differential equations within a fraction of the time compared to classical methods.
  2. Established new techniques for analyzing the stability of parametric delay equations and infinite configurations of dynamical systems.
  3. Found an improved method for accurately determining the long-term behavior of nonlinear structures.
  4. Researched numerical techniques for solving the Korteweg-de Vries equation and other related nonlinear problems.
  5. Performed research on the development of stochastic approximation algorithms for ordinary and partial differential equations.
  6. Developed an efficient method for solving parametric nonlinear equations.
  7. Contributed to the field of mathematical physics by developing new numerical methods for solving linear and nonlinear equations.
  8. Authored various mathematical notes on topics related to numerical analysis and solution of linear and nonlinear equations.
  9. Developed new algorithms to accurately measure the transient behavior of linear and nonlinear equations.
  10. Pioneered the study of numerical approximation of solution paths for certain classes of structural equations.

Born 3 December 1967, Ouagadougou, Burkina Faso.
