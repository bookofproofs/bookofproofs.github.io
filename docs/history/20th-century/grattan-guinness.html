Person: Grattan-Guinness, Ivor

Ivor Grattan-Guinness was an English historian of mathematics and logic. He was a pioneer in expanding the role of the history of mathematics in education.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Founded the Institute for the History of Mathematical Sciences (IHMS) at the University of Oxford in 1982.
  2. Authored a series of influential books and articles on the history of mathematics and its applications.
  3. Developed and implemented innovative ways of analyzing and interpreting the mathematical works of some of history’s most influential thinkers.
  4. Served as Editor-in-Chief of the journal Historia Mathematica for three decades.
  5. Received numerous awards for his scholarly work, such as the Pfafman Prize and the Carl Friedrich Gauss and Henry Augustus Rowland prizes.
  6. Assisted in the establishment of the European Mathematical Society and became its first President in 1990.
  7. Played a leading role in organizing and developing the International Commission on the History of Mathematics.
  8. Recruited an impressive team of scholars to support the IHMS and its activities.
  9. Presented a range of public lectures and seminars on the history of mathematics throughout Europe and the United States.
  10. Helped to establish attractive fellowships for young researchers in this field of study.

Born 23 June 1941, Bakewell, Derbyshire, England. Died 12 December 2014, Bengeo, Hertford, England.
