Person: Uhlenbeck (2), Karen

Karen Uhlenbeckis an American mathematician who is a leading expert on partial differential equations.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. The first woman to win the Abel Prize for Mathematics (2019).
  2. The first woman to receive the National Medal of Science in Mathematics (2003).
  3. She was the first female professor at Princeton University's Institute for Advanced Study (1973).
  4. She mentored many notable mathematicians, including the first female winner of the Fields Medal.
  5. She has made groundbreaking contributions to mathematics in areas such as calculus of variations, gauge theory, and geometric analysis.
  6. She served on the National Science Board for 12 years, became president of the American Mathematical Society, and she was the first woman to receive the American Mathematical Society's Steele Prize for Mathematical Exposition (2015).
  7. She was elected Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences (1981).
  8. She was elected to the United States National Academy of Sciences (2004).
  9. She was elected to the American Philosophical Society (2004).
  10. She was the first woman to be inducted into the Academy of Europe (1997).

Born 24 August 1942, Cleveland, Ohio, USA.
