Person: Birkhoff (2), Garrett

Garrett Birkhoff was an American mathematician best known for the algebra book he wrote with Saunders Mac Lane.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Developed the algebraic method of characterizing lattices in 1935.
  2. Developed the theorems of Birkhoff-von Neumann and Birkhoff-Rothschild in 1955 and 1951, respectively.
  3. Co-authored Von Neumann's text 'Theory of Games and Economic Behavior' in 1944.
  4. Founded the Department of Applied Mathematics at Harvard University in 1948.
  5. Founded the Institute of Lattice Theory at Harvard University in 1961.
  6. Proved the Bron–Kerbosch theorem related to graph theory in 1973.
  7. Became president of the American Mathematical Association in 1956.
  8. Won the National Medal of Science in 1989.
  9. Received the Order of the Rising Sun in 1995.

Born 19 January 1911, Princeton, New Jersey, USA. Died 22 November 1996, Water Mill, New York, USA.
