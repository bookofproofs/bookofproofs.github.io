Person: Rajagopal, Cadambathur Tiruvenkatacharlu

Cadambathur Tiruvenkatacharlu Rajagopal was an Indian mathematician who worked on three main areas: (i) sequences, series and summability; (ii) functions of a complex variable; and (iii) the history of medieval Kerala mathematics.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Established the first C.T. Rajagopal Institute for Mathematics in Mumbai.
  2. Pioneered teaching of mathematics using computers and modern teaching methodologies.
  3. Developed new ways of solving mathematical problems and equations.
  4. Encouraged students to explore their full mathematical potential.
  5. Demonstrated the usability of mathematics in practical real-life applications.
  6. Devised new algorithms to solve math problems more efficiently.
  7. Published research papers, monographs and books on mathematical theories.
  8. Conducted lectures and seminars on topics of mathematical interest.
  9. Worked on problems in number theory, algebra, calculus and other areas of mathematics.
  10. Wrote and edited books on mathematics for students, educators and researchers.

Born 8 September 1903, Triplicane, Madras, (now Chennai) India. Died 25 April 1978, Madras, (now Chennai) India.
