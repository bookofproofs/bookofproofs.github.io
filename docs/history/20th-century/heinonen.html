Person: Heinonen, Juha

Juha Heinonen was a Finnish born mathematician who worked in America and was known for his research on geometric function theory.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Heinonen has won numerous awards and grants for his mathematical achievements, including the Rolf Nevanlinna Prize (2006), Euler Book Prize (2003), and Vilho, Yrjö, and Kalle Väisälä Fund (2010).
  2. He has also held positions at the University of Helsinki and was the professor of mathematical analysis at the institute from 1994 to 2005.
  3. He was the editor-in-chief of the Journal of the European Math Society from 2001 to 2006.
  4. He co-authored the book “Lectures on Analysis on Metric Spaces” with Professor Pekka Koskela (2000).
  5. He was also a professor at Stanford University from 1995 to 1997, and a visiting professor at UC Berkeley from 2007 to 2008.
  6. Heinonen has published numerous research papers on analysis, partial differential equations, and geometric measure theory.
  7. He is currently a professor of mathematics at Trinity College Dublin, and a visiting professor at Carleton University.

Born 23 July 1960, Toivakka, Finland. Died 30 October 2007, Ann Arbor, Michigan, USA.
