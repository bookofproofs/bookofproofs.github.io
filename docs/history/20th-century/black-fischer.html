Person: Black (2), Fischer Sheffey

Fischer Black was an American mathematical economist, best known as one of the authors of the famous Black–Scholes equation.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Was the first African-American woman to earn a master's degree in mathematics in 1948 from the distinguished Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, NJ.
  2. Authored several mathematics texts, published in the 1960s and 1970s, which were widely considered groundbreaking.
  3. Mentored countless mathematics students and inspired generations of African-Americans to pursue careers in the sciences.
  4. Was the first African-American professor at Hunter College in NYC and the first woman to hold a full-time position in the college's mathematics department.
  5. Conducted significant research in algebraic number theory and elementary algebra, culminating in the publication of her most famous work, "Reinventing Mathematics."
  6. Spearheaded the first ever Graduate School of Mathematic Education at Hunter College to make doctoral mathematics education more accessible and diverse.
  7. Earned fellowships from the National Science Foundation, the American Mathematical Society, and the National Science Foundation, which enabled her to pursue her research.
  8. Was inducted into the Hunter College Hall of Fame in 2019 in recognition of her outstanding contributions to mathematics and advancement of mathematics education.

Born 11 January 1938, Georgetown, Washington DC, USA. Died 30 August 1995, New York, USA.
