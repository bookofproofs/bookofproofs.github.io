Person: Apostol, Tom

Tom Apostol was an American mathematician who specialised in analytic number theory and wrote one of the best-known books on analysis.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Published 9 books on calculus, which have become a standard reference in high-level education.
  2. Developed the modern geometric interpretation of the Multiplication Theorem of Calculus.
  3. Ratified theorems related to the Euler-Maclaurin Formula.
  4. Originated the notion of principal kinematic pairs.
  5. Co-authored five other mathematical texts, including "Theory and Problems of Calculus" and "Mathematics: Calculator-Based Laboratory Manual".
  6. Authored the "Apostol Calculus" column for the Mathematical Association of America.
  7. Created a series of lecture notes and seminars on advanced mathematics.
  8. Developed the field of applied mathematical analysis.
  9. Received the Mathematical Association of America's Gungoll-Morgan-Smith Awards for Excellence in Teaching.

Born 20 August 1923, Helper, Carbon County, Utah, USA. Died 8 May 2016, Pasadena, California, USA.
