Person: Householder, Alston Scott

Alston Householder was an American mathematician who worked in mathematical biology and numerical analysis.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Established the Geometry Lab at RPI and created the ‘Householder Algorithm’ for numerical linear algebra
  2. Pioneered in the areas of numerical linear algebra, matrix theory, and matrix calculation
  3. Authored two books on numerical linear algebra, Matrix Theory and Matrix Computations
  4. Developed a fast inversion algorithm for computing matrix-vector products
  5. Played a major role in producing new and improved algorithms in eigenvalue calculations
  6. Constructively contributed to advancements in linear matrix equation solvers
  7. Pioneered a new branch of numerical linear algebra theory, 'Markov Chain Analysis'
  8. Conducted research in matrix polynomials, matrix equations and functions, and numerical matrix factorizations
  9. Developed techniques for computing inverses and determinants of large sparse matrices
  10. Developed solid methods for solving linear least squares equations in matrix form

Born 5 May 1904, Rockford, Illinois, USA. Died 4 July 1993, Malibu, California, USA.
