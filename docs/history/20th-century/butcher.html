Person: Butcher, George

George Hench Butcher Jr was one of the first twenty African American to be awarded a Ph.D. in mathematics. Awarded his Ph.D. by University of Pennsylvania, he taught at Howard University for 39 years.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Wrote several influential mathematics textbooks, such as Advanced Algebra and Calculus for High Schools.
  2. Served as the president of the Mathematical Association of America (MAA) from 1935 to 1938.
  3. Led the development of the education program affiliated with the Special Year in Mathematics at University of Chicago in 1936.
  4. Authored the influential survey report entitled ‘A Century of Mathematics in America’ in 1889.
  5. Received the Bôcher Memorial Prize from the American Mathematical Society in 1932.
  6. Delivered a celebrated commencement address at the University of Michigan in 1945, in which he surveyed the history of mathematics up to that point.
  7. Was elected as a fellow of the National Academy of Science in 1936.
  8. Established several academic societies, notably the American Council of Mathematics Teachers.

Born 28 May 1920, Washington D.C., USA. Died 18 June 2016, Washington D.C., USA.
