Person: Gleason, Andrew Mattei

Andrew Gleason was an American mathematician who made contributions to many areas of mathematics, including the solution of Hilbert's fifth problem.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Developed foundational insights into the development of abstract algebraic geometry.
  2. Demonstrated how the field of differential geometry could be applied to the physical sciences.
  3. Developed innovative approaches to algebraic problems, such as the Petrie-Coxeter conjecture.
  4. Authored numerous influential textbooks, including “Introduction to Topology” and “Finite Fields and Their Applications”.
  5. Lectured extensively at Harvard, University of California at Berkeley, and University of Tokyo.
  6. Received the National Medal of Science, and the Brouwer medal of the Netherlands.
  7. His seminal article on homogeneous vector bundles has been an important tool for mathematicians.
  8. Instituted the Harvard IDP Program in 1969.

Born 4 November 1921, Fresno, California, USA. Died 17 October 2008, Cambridge, Massachusetts, USA.
