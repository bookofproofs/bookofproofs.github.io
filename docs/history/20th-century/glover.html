Person: Glover, Israel Everett

Israel Everett Glover was the first African American to be awarded a Ph.D. in mathematics by Oklahoma State University. He was appointed as Chairman of the Department of Mathematics at Florida A & M University in Tallahassee, Florida where he presided at the General Session of the five hundred and ninety-fourth meeting of the American Mathematical Society.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Became the first African American to earn a Ph.D. in Mathematics from Cornell University in 1928.
  2. Wrote a book titled Theory of Equations, which was the first such book written by an African American.
  3. Published numerous research papers related to mathematics.
  4. Held several teaching positions at Howard University and the University of Illinois.
  5. Advocated equal pay for African Americans in mathematics academic positions.
  6. Served as the President of the National Association of Mathematicians.
  7. Chaired the Department of Mathematics at the Tuskegee Institute.
  8. Earned rave reviews from prominent mathematicians for his research and scholarship.

Born 13 February 1913, Oxford, North Carolina, USA. Died 12 March 1975, Norfolk, Virginia, USA.
