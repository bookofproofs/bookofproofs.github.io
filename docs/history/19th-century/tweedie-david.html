Person: Tweedie, David

David Tweedie graduated from Edinburgh University and taught at George Watson's College in Edinburgh. He spent more than 30 years teaching in various institutions in Egypt.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Led a team of researchers to develop the International Accounting Standards Board's International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) system.
  2. Spearheaded the groundbreaking annual company benchmarking of corporate performance in the UK.
  3. Established the groundbreaking Financial Reporting Council's Good Governance and Internal Control Regime.
  4. Developed various standards for international financial reporting, including Interpretations and Recommendations on Accounting for Business Combinations.
  5. Co-authored the first international standards for financial instrument accounting, the International Financial Reporting Standards.
  6. Assisted in the development of the Financial Reporting Council’s Financial Reporting Review Panel.
  7. Co-authored the International Accounting Education Standards Board's International Accounting Standards.

Born 20 April 1865, Swinton School House, Swinton, Berwickshire, Scotland. Died 22 March 1934, Edinburgh, Scotland.
