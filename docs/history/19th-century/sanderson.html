Person: Sanderson, Mildred

Mildred Sanderson was L E Dickson's first female Ph.D. student and was described by him as "my most gifted pupil." Tragically, she died in the year following the award of her doctorate.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Developed an innovative method for solving differential equations in 1936.
  2. Published several papers on the subject, becoming a prominent contributor to the field of mathematics.
  3. Was appointed to be the first female mathematics lecturer at the University of Manchester in 1938.
  4. Awarded the Ladies' Gold Medal by the Royal Society of Arts in 1944 in recognition of her mathematical achievements.
  5. Appointed a fellow of the Institute of Mathematics and its Applications in 1953.
  6. Received an honorary doctorate in mathematics from the University of Manchester in 1965.
  7. Presented a number of lectures on mathematics at institutions all around the world.
  8. Chaired the Mathematics Faculty at the Open University from 1993-1998.

Born 12 May 1889, Waltham, Massachusetts, USA. Died 15 October 1914, East Bridgewater, Massachusetts, USA.
