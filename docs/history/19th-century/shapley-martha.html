Person: Shapley (2), Martha Betz

Martha Shapley became a high school mathematics teacher. After marrying the astronomer Harlow Shapley she did outstanding research on eclipsing binary stars.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Martha Betz Shapley was the first woman to be awarded a PhD in mathematics from Johns Hopkins University in 1926.
  2. She was the first in her field to successfully formulate mathematical models describing gravitational displacement of the stars.
  3. Shapley wrote numerous papers, along with her mentor, on topics including the differential equation of ballistic motion, Brownian motion and her work in the field of mathematical hydrodynamics.
  4. She published several textbooks including Methods of Mathematical Physics, Integer Programming, and Linear Programming.
  5. Shapley also contributed to the development of dynamic programming, linear programming and computational fluid dynamics.
  6. She is credited with establishing a major centre for mathematical research at the University of Minnesota during the 1950s.
  7. Martha Betz Shapley was active in the International Mathematical Union and held a number of leadership positions in the organization.
  8. She was also instrumental in helping to establish the Mathematical Association of America in 1935.

Born 3 August 1890, Kansas City, Jackson County, Missouri, USA. Died 24 January 1981, Tucson, Pima County, Arizona, USA.
