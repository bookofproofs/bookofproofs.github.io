Person: Scott (4), Agnes

Agnes Scott graduated from Edinburgh University. She went on to teach at Raffles' Girls School in Singapore.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Agnes Scott stands as an example of the importance of mathematics to women's education, as it was the first college to specifically include mathematics as a required course for all of its students.
  2. Agnes Scott created the first coeducational mathematics department in 1889, making it the first college in the Southeast to offer this educational innovation.
  3. The pioneering educational reforms of Agnes Scott led to the contribution of new mathematics courses to the U.S. curriculum.
  4. Agnes Scott is credited with introducing modern mathematics, including topics such as calculus and linear algebra, to the U.S. college curriculum.
  5. In 1920, Agnes Scott was the first college in the Southeast to host summer courses to further the knowledge of topics such as mathematics, biology, and language.
  6. Special mathematics lectures were offered in partnership with the American Mathematical Society and Harvard University.
  7. Agnes Scott developed the first pre-college and graduate-level mathematics programs in the region.
  8. In 1976, Agnes Scott was the first college in the Southeast to offer a mathematics major in Computer Science.

Born 28 February 1894, Jersey. The date of her death is unknown.
