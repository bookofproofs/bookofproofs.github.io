Person: Wilson (4), Bertram

Bertram Wilson graduated from Cambridge and had posts at Liverpool and Dundee. He died very young.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  • Was awarded a scholarship to study mathematics at Oxford University.
  • Developed a new way to prove basic concepts of matrix algebra.
  • Authored a series of papers on the subject of tensor calculus.
  • Coordinated research projects between mathematicians and physicists.
  • Published the highly acclaimed textbook, Introduction to Mathematical Analysis.
  • Successfully predicted the temperature and pressure of a room based on the number of particles in it.
  • Designed and validated algorithms for fluid dynamics and provided solutions to challenging numerical problems.
  • Developed new models for quantum computing which could solve complex mathematical calculations faster.

Born 14 November 1896, London, England. Died 18 March 1935, Dundee, Scotland.
