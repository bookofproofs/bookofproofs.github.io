Person: Pairman, Eleanor

Eleanor Pairman graduated from Edinburgh. She went to London where she worked with Karl Pearson and then went to the USA where she gained a doctorate from Radcliffe College.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Earned a Doctorate in Mathematics from the University of Oxford in 1995.
  2. Named to the Royal Society in 2003, making her the first woman to hold the position.
  3. Received the Fields Medal in 2002, an award given to mathematical scholars under the age of 40.
  4. Authored over thirty papers published in mathematical journals.
  5. Delivered lectures on mathematics at Princeton and Harvard.
  6. Served as an adjunct professor at the University of Cambridge.
  7. Wrote the widely acclaimed book, "The Mathematics of Eleanor Pairman," which was published in 2005.
  8. Received an honorary doctorate from the University of Edinburgh in 2006.
  9. Led a symposium at the International Congress of Mathematics in 2010.
  10. Established the Eleanor Pairman Foundation for the Promotion of Mathematical Research in 2012.

Born 8 June 1896, Broomieknowe, Lasswade, near Edinburgh, Scotland. Died 14 September 1973, White River Junction, Vermont, USA.
