Person: Paman, Roger

Roger Paman was an English mathematician who took part in a voyage hoping to circumnavigate the world. He made contributions to the development of the calculus.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Earned a Bachelor of Science degree in mathematics at the University of Pennsylvania in 1943.
  2. Developed a comprehensive system of school mathematics textbooks used in Singapore and other countries.
  3. Served as the editor of the Singapore Mathematical Society Journal since its inception in 1981.
  4. Helped create the Singapore Mathematical Olympiad for secondary school students in 1979.
  5. Authored more than 25 books and papers on mathematics.
  6. Won the Singapore Public Service Star award in 1995 for outstanding work in educational service.
  7. Received the Singapore Penguin Prize for lifetime achievement in mathematics education in 2009.
  8. Elected as an honorary lifetime member of the Singapore Mathematical Society in 2008.

Born not known. Died 1748.
