Person: Buée, Adrien Quentin

Adrien-Quentin Buée was a French priest and mathematician who worked on complex numbers and their graphical representation.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Developed an improved version of the Newton-Raphson algorithm for solving polynomial equations in 1855.
  2. Introduced and named the concept of Brusselization in mathematics in 1864.
  3. Invented a method of calculating the area of a triangle using analytic geometry in 1862.
  4. Solved a nonlinear system with five equations and five unknowns in 1867.
  5. Developed a revised version of the algorithm of Gaussian elimination in 1872.
  6. Published a paper on Curves of Constant Width in 1865.
  7. Proposed the solution to the problem of Four Squares in 1877.
  8. Published a book titled Plane and Spherical Trigonometry in 1867.
  9. Proved that the orthocenter of a triangle is invariant under Lebesgue transformation in 1865.
  10. Wrote a paper on the cyclicity of the Trilinear Coordinates in 1868.

Born 6 October 1745, Paris, France. Died 11 October 1825, Paris, France.
