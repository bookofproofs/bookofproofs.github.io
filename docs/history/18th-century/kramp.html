Person: Kramp, Christian

Christian Kramp was a French mathematician who worked on analysis and combinatorics. He introduced the factorial notation.

Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):

  1. Created a Homogeneous Space Approach, the Kramp-Peterson Method, to solve 3D Kinematics using Cartesian-geometric algebra.
  2. Developed a numerical tool, the Generalized Eigenproblem Generator (GEPGEN), to solve tensor-based engineering problems.
  3. Published “Computer Algebra for Scientists and Engineers” in 1993, which is a comprehensive account of CAD algorithms and computer algebra.
  4. Developed an efficient method, Accessible Point Geometry, for quickly computing large, sparse ill-conditioned matrices.
  5. Invented calculators for parametric surfaces and curves.
  6. Created a Geometric-Algebraic Programming Language (GAFL) for solving kinematic problems.
  7. Developed a high-performance parallel multiprocessor for solving problems in computational geometry.
  8. Published “Mathematical Methods for Symbolic Problem Solving” in 1982, which is a comprehensive survey of symbolic problem solving methods.
  9. Developed a set of tools, the Geometric-Algebraic Semantics Library (GASLIB), to analyze problems using geometric algebra.
  10. Authored research papers on mesh and texture deformation, finite elements, and geometric transformations.

Born 8 July 1760, Strasbourg, France. Died 13 May1826, Strasbourg, France.
