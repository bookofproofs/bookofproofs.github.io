Definition: Congruent, Residue

Let $m > 0$ be a positive integer $a$ is called congruent to $b$ (or $a$ is called a residue of $b$) modulo $m$, written $$a\equiv b \mod m$$ or shorter $$a\equiv b (m),$$ if $m\mid a-b,$ i.e. if $m$ is a divisor of the difference $a-b.$
