Proposition: Prop. 12.10: Volume of Cone is Third of Cylinder on Same Base and of Same Height

(Proposition 10 from Book 12 of Euclid's “Elements”)

Every cone is the third part of the cylinder which has the same base as it, and an equal height. * For let there be a cone (with) the same base as a cylinder, (namely) the circle $ABCD$, and an equal height. * I say that the cone is the third part of the cylinder - that is to say, that the cylinder is three times the cone.
