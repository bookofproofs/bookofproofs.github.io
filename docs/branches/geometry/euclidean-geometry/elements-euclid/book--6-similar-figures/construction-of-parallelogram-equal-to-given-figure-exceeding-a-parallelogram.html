Proposition: 6.29: Construction of Parallelogram Equal to Given Figure Exceeding a Parallelogram

(Proposition 9 from Book 6 of Euclid's “Elements”)

To apply a parallelogram, equal to a given rectilinear figure, to a given straight line, (the applied parallelogram) overshooting by a parallelogrammic figure similar to a given (parallelogram). * Let $AB$ be the given straight line, and $C$ the given rectilinear figure to which the (parallelogram) applied to $AB$ is required (to be) equal, and $D$ the (parallelogram) to which the excess is required (to be) similar. * So it is required to apply a parallelogram, equal to the given rectilinear figure $C$, to the given straight line $AB$, overshooting by a parallelogrammic figure similar to $D$.

Modern Formulation

This proposition is a geometric solution of the quadratic equation1 \[x^2 + \alpha\,x -\beta = 0.\]
