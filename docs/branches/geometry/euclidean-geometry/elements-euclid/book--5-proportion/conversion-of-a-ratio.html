Definition: 5.16: Conversion of a Ratio

A conversion of a ratio is a taking of the (ratio of the) leading (magnitude) to the excess by which the leading (magnitude) exceeds the following.

Modern Formulation

In other words, given a ratio of positive real numbers \(\alpha,\beta\) with \(\alpha > \beta\),

\[\frac\alpha\beta,\]

the conversion of the ratio is given by

\[\frac\alpha{\alpha-\beta}.\]
