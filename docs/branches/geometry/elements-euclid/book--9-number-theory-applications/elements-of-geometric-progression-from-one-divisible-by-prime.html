Proposition: Prop. 9.12: Elements of Geometric Progression from One Divisible by Prime

Euclid's Formulation

If any multitude whatsoever of numbers is in continued proportion, (starting) from a unit, then however many prime numbers the last (number) is measured by, the (number) next to the unit will also be measured by the same (prime numbers).

  • Let any multitude whatsoever of numbers, $A$, $B$, $C$, $D$, be in (continued) proportion, (starting) from a unit.
  • I say that however many prime numbers $D$ is measured by, $A$ will also be measured by the same (prime numbers).
