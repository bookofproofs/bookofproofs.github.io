Proposition: Group of Units

The units of an integral domain $(R, + ,\cdot)$ form with respect to the multiplication operation $[\cdot"$ a "group]bookofproofs$671, called the group of units of $R,$ and denoted by $(R^\ast,\cdot).$

Examples

  • $\mathbb Z^\ast=\{1,-1\}.$
  • $\mathbb Q[X]^\ast=\mathbb Q\setminus\{0\}.$
