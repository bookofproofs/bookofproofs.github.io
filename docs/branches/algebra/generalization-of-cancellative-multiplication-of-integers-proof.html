Proof: 1592

(related to Proposition: Generalization of Cancellative Multiplication of Integers)

$"\Rightarrow"$

  • Assume, $ac=bc.$
  • Because of the distributivity law in rings, it follows $(a-b)c=0.$
  • By hypothesis, $(R, + ,\cdot)$ is an integral domain and $c\neq 0.$
  • Therefore $a-b=0.$
  • This is equivalent to $a=b.$

$"\Leftarrow"$

  • Assume, $a=b.$
  • Multiplication by $c$ gives us $ac=bc.$
