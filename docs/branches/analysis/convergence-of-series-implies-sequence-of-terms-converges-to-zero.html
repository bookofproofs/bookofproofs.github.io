The following proposition shows that the convergence of terms in the sequence is a necessary condition for the series itself to converge.

Proposition: Convergence of Series Implies Sequence of Terms Converges to Zero

A necessary, but not a sufficient1 condition for an infinite series $\sum_{k=0}^\infty x_k$ for being convergent is that the sequence of the summands \((x_n)_{n\in\mathbb N}\) converges to zero, formally $$\sum_{k=0}^\infty x_k\text{ convergent}\Longrightarrow\lim_{n\to\infty} x_n=0,$$ $$\lim_{n\to\infty} x_n= 0\not\Longrightarrow\sum_{k=0}^\infty x_k\text{ convergent}$$

$$\lim_{n\to\infty} x_n\neq 0\Longrightarrow\sum_{k=0}^\infty x_k\text{ divergent}$$
