Definition: Complex Conjugate

Let \(z=x + yi\in\mathbb C\) be a complex number. The complex conjugate of \(z\), denoted by \(z^* \), is the complex number with the same real part and the negative imaginary part, i.e.

\[z^* :=\Re(z) - \Im (z) i=x - y i.\]
