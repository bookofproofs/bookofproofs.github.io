Lemma: The Proving Principle by Contradiction

Let \(p\) be a proposition and \(\neg p\) its negation. By showing that: \[\neg p \Rightarrow 0\] it follows that \[p = 1.\]

This proving method is called by contradiction or reductio ad absurdum.

Formally, reductio ad absordum is the following logical argument: $$\begin{array}{rll} \neg p\Rightarrow 0&\text{premise}&\text{e.g. It is false that the sun is not shining.}\\ \hline p&\text{conclusion}&\text{e.g. Therefore, the sun is shining.}\\ \end{array} $$
