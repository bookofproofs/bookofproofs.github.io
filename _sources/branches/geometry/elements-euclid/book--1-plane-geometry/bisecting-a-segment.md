### (Proposition 10 from Book 1 of Euclid's “Elements”)

> To cut a given finite [straight line][bookofproofs$645] in half.

* Let `$AB$` be the given finite [straight line][bookofproofs$645].
* So it is required to cut the finite [straight line][bookofproofs$645] `$AB$` in half.


![fig10e](https://github.com/bookofproofs/bookofproofs.github.io/blob/main/_sources/_assets/images/euclid/Book01/fig10e.png?raw=true)


### Modern Formulation

It is possible to bisect an arbitrary segment; that is, it is possible to locate the midpoint of a segment.
