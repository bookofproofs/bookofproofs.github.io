### (Definition 3 from Book 6 of Euclid's “Elements”)

> The **height** of any [figure][bookofproofs$687] is the ([straight line][bookofproofs$645]) drawn from the vertex [perpendicular][bookofproofs$653] to the base.

### Modern Formulation

The **height** of a [figure][bookofproofs$687] is the longest [segment][bookofproofs$645] which can be drawn [perpendicularly][bookofproofs$653] to the base.
