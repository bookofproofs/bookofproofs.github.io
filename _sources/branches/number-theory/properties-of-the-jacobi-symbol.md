Many of the properties of the [Legendre symbol][bookofproofs$8198] can be generalized for the [Jacobi symbol][bookofproofs$8214].
---

Let `$n,m$` be [odd][bookofproofs$8130] [positive integers][bookofproofs$1075] and let `$a,b$` be any [integers][bookofproofs$844]. The [Jacobi symbol][bookofproofs$8214] fulfills the following properties:

1. Jacobi symbol for equal residues: If `$a(n)\equiv b(n),$` then `$\left(\frac an\right)=\left(\frac bn\right).$`
1. Multiplicativity: `$\left(\frac {ab}n\right)=\left(\frac an\right)\cdot \left(\frac bn\right).$`
1. Reciprocal Multiplicativity: `$\left(\frac {a}{nm}\right)=\left(\frac an\right)\cdot \left(\frac am\right).$`
1. Reciprocity Law: If `$n$` and `$m$` are [co-prime][bookofproofs$8093], then `$$\left(\frac mn\right)\left(\frac nm\right)=(-1)^{\frac{n-1}{2}\frac{m-1}{2}}.$$`
1. First supplementary law: `$$\left(\frac {-1}n\right)=(-1)^{\frac{n-1}{2}}.$$`
1. First supplementary law: `$$\left(\frac {-1}n\right)=(-1)^{\frac{n-1}{2}}.$$`
