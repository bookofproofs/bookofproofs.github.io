Let `\(A\)` be an alphabet with `\(k\)` letters `\[A=\{l_1,\ldots,l_k\}.\]` 
The number of different [strings][bookofproofs$708] of length `\(n\)` over this alphabet (i.e. `\(s_i\in A\)`) `\[s_1s_2\ldots s_n\]` 
equals `\[k^n.\]`
