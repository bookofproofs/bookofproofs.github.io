Let `\(V\)` be a [vector space][bookofproofs$560] over the [field of real numbers][bookofproofs$6194] `\(\mathbb R\)`. Then the [scalar product][bookofproofs$1049] `\(\langle \cdot,\cdot\rangle\)` on `\(V\)` fulfills the following properties:

1. **linear in the first argument**: `\(\left\langle \lambda _{1}v+\lambda _{2}w,y\right\rangle =\lambda _{1}\left\langle v,y\right\rangle +\lambda _{2}\left\langle w,y\right\rangle \,\)` for all `\(\lambda_{1},\lambda _{2}\in \mathbb {R} \)`, `\(v,w,y\in V\)`,
1. **linear in the second argument**: `\(\left\langle x, \lambda _{1}v+\lambda _{2}w\right\rangle =\lambda _{1}\left\langle x, v\right\rangle +\lambda _{2}\left\langle x, w\right\rangle \,\)` for all `\(\lambda _{1},\lambda _{2}\in \mathbb {R} \)`, `\(x, v,w\in V\)`,
1. **symmetric**: `\(\left\langle v,w\right\rangle =\left\langle w,v\right\rangle \,\)` for all `\(v,w\in V\)`.
1. **symmetric**: `\(\left\langle v,w\right\rangle =\left\langle w,v\right\rangle \,\)` for all `\(v,w\in V\)`.
