Let `\((G,\ast)\)` be a finite [cyclic group][bookofproofs$807], i.e. `\(|G|=n < \infty\)` and let `\(d\mid n\)` be a divisor of `\(n\)`. Then there exists exactly one [subgroup][bookofproofs$554] `\(H\subseteq G\)` with `\(|H|=d\)`.
