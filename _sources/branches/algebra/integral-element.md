Let `\(S\)` be an [algebra over the ring ][bookofproofs$6213] `\(R\)`. An element `\(x\in S\)` is called *integral over `\(R\)`*, if there is a [monic polynomial][bookofproofs$6257] `\(p\in R[X]\)` with `\(p\neq 0\)` and `\(p(x)=0\)`, i.e. `\(x\)` is a root of the monic polynomial.
