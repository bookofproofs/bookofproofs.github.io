Let `\(A=(a,b)\)`, `\(I=(c,d)\)` be [bounded real intervals][bookofproofs$1153] with `\(A\subseteq I\)` and the lengths `\(L(A):=|b-a|\)`, `\(L(I):=|d-c|\)`. A function `\(p:A\mapsto [0,1]\)` defined by 
`\[p(A)=\frac{L(A)}{L(I)}\]`
is called a **geometric probability**.
