Let `$p:\mathbb R\to\mathbb R$` be a [real polynomial][bookofproofs$199] with an [odd][bookofproofs$2318] degree `$n$`, i.e. `$$p(x):=a_nx^n + \ldots + a_1x + a_0.$$` Then there are is at least one real number `$x$` such that `$p(x)=0.$`
