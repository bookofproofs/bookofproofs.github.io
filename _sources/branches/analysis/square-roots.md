Let `\(a > 0\)` be a positive [real number][bookofproofs$1105]. The equation
`\[x^2=a\]`
has two real solutions `\(x_1,x_2\in\mathbb R\)`, called the **square roots** of the number `\(a\)` and denoted by `\(x_1:=\sqrt a\)` and `\(x_2:=- \sqrt a\)`.
