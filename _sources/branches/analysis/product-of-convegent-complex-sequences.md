Let `\((a_n)_{n\in\mathbb N}\)` and `\((b_n)_{n\in\mathbb N}\)` be [complex sequences convergent][bookofproofs$1700] to the limits `\(\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} a_n=a\)` and `\(\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} b_n=b\)`. Consider the complex sequence `\((c_n)_{n\in\mathbb N}\)` with `\(c_n:=a_n \cdot b_n\)`. Then `\((c_n)_{n\in\mathbb N}\)` is also convergent and its limit equals `\(\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} c_n=a \cdot b\)`.

This proposition can be expressed in the short form:

`\[\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} (a_n \cdot b_n)=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} a_n \cdot \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} b_n.\]`
