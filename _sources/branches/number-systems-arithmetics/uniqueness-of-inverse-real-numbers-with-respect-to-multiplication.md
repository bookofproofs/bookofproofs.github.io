For every [real number][bookofproofs$1105] `\(x\neq 0\)` there is only one real number, denoted by `\(x^{-1}\)`, such that `\(x\cdot x^{-1}=x^{-1}\cdot x=1\)` for all `\(x\in\mathbb R\)`.
