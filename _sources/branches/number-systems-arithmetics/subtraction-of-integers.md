The  **subtraction of integers**, written `\(x-y\)`, is defined as the [addition][bookofproofs$890] of the first integer `\(x\)` with the [inverse of the second integer with respect to addition][bookofproofs$1511] `\((-y)\)`, formally
`\[x-y=[a,b]-[c,d]:=[a,b]+[d,c]=[a+d,b+c]=x+(-y).\]`
