For every [rational number][bookofproofs$1033] `\(x\neq 0\)` there is only one rational number, denoted by `\(x^{-1}\)`, such that `\(x\cdot x^{-1}=x^{-1}\cdot x=1\)` for all `\(x\in\mathbb Q\)`.
