Let `\(0 < b < 1\)` be a [real number][bookofproofs$1105]. Then, for every (arbitrarily small) `\(\epsilon > 0\)` there is [natural number][bookofproofs$718] `\(n\)`, for which `\(b^n < \epsilon\)`.
