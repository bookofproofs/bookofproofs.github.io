Given two natural numbers `\(x,y\)`, only one of the following cases can be true:

1. Either `\(x=y\)`, 
1. or there exists exactly one natural number `\(u\neq 0\)` with `\(x=y+u\)`,
1. or there exists exactly one natural number `\(u\neq 0\)` with `\(x=y+u\)`,
