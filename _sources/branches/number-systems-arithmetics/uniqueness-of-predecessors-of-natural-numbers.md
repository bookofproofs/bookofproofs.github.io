Every [natural number][bookofproofs$718] `\(x\neq 0\)` has a unique predecessor `\(u\)`, i.e. there is exactly one natural number `\(u\)` such that `\(x\)` is its [successor][bookofproofs$504] `\(x=u^+\)`.
