Since `\(d\)` is a distance on `\(X\)`, it follows by the definition of its [three properties][bookofproofs$614] that 
`\[0=d(x,x)\le d(x,y)+d(y,x)=2d(x,y).\]`
Consequently, it must be `\(0\le d(x,y)\)` for all `\(x,y\in X\)`.
