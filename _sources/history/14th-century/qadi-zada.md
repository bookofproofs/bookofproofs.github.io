**Qadi Zada** was an Islamic mathematician who wrote a number of commentaries of works on mathematics and astronomy.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Compiled a book of mathematical theories known as the Zij al-Sultani in 1420 CE.
2. Developed methods for solving various mathematical problems.
3. Discovered a way to intuitively identify the occurrence of irrational numbers.
4. Proposed a solution for determining the accuracy of measurements.
5. Found a method for accurately calculating the sine of any angle.
6. Found a way to calculate the cube root of any number.
7. Created a method for calculating the area of an ellipse.
8. Developed the Zadi Zusati, which answered many questions involving geometry, algebra, and astronomy.
9. Established norms for accuracy in astronomical observations and calculations.
10. Created a unit of length to help in the calculation of distances.

Born 1364, Bursa, Turkey. Died 1436, Samarkand, Uzbekistan.
