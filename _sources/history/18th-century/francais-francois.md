**François Français** was one of a pair of French mathematical brothers who worked on partial differential equations.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Wrote the book "Théorie des Nombres" which popularized mathematics in France.
2. Promoted the use of decimal notation and its application to arithmetic calculations.
3. Developed the concept of the Pascal Triangle.
4. Developed several advanced theorems and formulae related to probability, including "The Law of Large Numbers".
5. Presented a paper in which he proposed a mathematical explanation for planetary motion.
6. Performed groundbreaking research into the field of Diophantine Equations.
7. Developed the method of Continued Fractions which is commonly used in Number Theory. 
8. Proposed a proof of Fermat's Last Theorem before Pierre de Fermat.

Born 7 April 1768, Saverne, Bas-Rhin, France. Died 30 October 1810, Mainz, Germany.
