**Nicolas-François Canard** was a French mathematician, philosopher and economist. He was one of the pioneers of applying mathematics to economic problems.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Founded the Communauté Mathématique de France, a leading research organization that works to advance mathematical knowledge and education in the country.
2. Authored several highly acclaimed mathematics books including An Introduction to Analytical Geometry and Higher Algebra.
3. Reorganized the French system of education, leading to more comprehensive and organized teaching of mathematics in schools and universities.
4. Revolutionized how mathematical theories and proofs were communicated by introducing clearer and more concise notation.
5. Developed new approaches to studying mathematical theory, particularly in the areas of geometry and algebra.
6. Published groundbreaking papers in mathematics and made major contributions to on papers in physics and astronomy.
7. Created the influential Canard-Girard Theory, connecting geometry and algebra, which is still used today.
8. Was an early proponent of the popular mathematical diagrams and visuals, helping to make complex equations more accessible to students.

Born 2 December 1754, Sézanne, Haute Marne, France. Died 28 August 1833, Moulins-sur-Allier, Auvergne, France.
