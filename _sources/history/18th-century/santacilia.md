**Jorge Juan** was a mathematician and seaman who took part in the famous Franco-Spanish expedition to South America to measure a degree of meridian at the Equator. He introduced the study of Newton to Spain, supervised the modernisation of the Spanish Navy and set the basis for the future Spanish Academy of Sciences.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Founded the Spanish Royal Academy of Mathematics in 1782.
2. Led a survey of the Spanish coast which resulted in the publication of the guidebook Relaciones y derroteros de las costas de Espa√±a.
3. Used his expertise in mathematics with his knowledge of astronomy, geography, and navigation to make large chart of the Balearic Islands.
4. Calculated the curvature of the earth using a survey on the coast of Cadiz.
5. Constructed the first mechanical calculator to calculate tables of logarithms.

Born 5th January, 1713, Novelda, Alicante, Spain. Died June 21, 1773, Madrid, Spain.
