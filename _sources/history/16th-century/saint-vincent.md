**Gregory of Saint-Vincent** was a Flemish Jesuit who wrote a book covering many aspects of contemporary mathematics.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the concept of “irreducibly complex” mathematical objects.
2. Discovered the volume of a sphere in 1647.
3. Established the modern rule of signs in 1654.
4. Wrote on the mathematics of astrolabes in 1659.
5. Showed that a single equation of the third degree can have multiple solutions.
6. Outlined the theory of indivisibles in his book Geometria Indivisibilibus Continuorum.
7. Developed new methods for integrating various types of algebraic equations.
8. Defined what is now called an “ellipse” in 1647.
9. Published important works in algebra, geometry, and trigonometry.
10. Demonstrated a method for finding the squared roots of complex numbers in 1647.

Born 8 September 1584, Bruges, Spanish Netherlands (now Belgium). Died 27 January 1667, Ghent, Spanish Netherlands (now Belgium).
