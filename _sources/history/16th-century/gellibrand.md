**Henry Gellibrand** was an English clergyman who worked on magnetic declination and who made mathematical contributions to navigation.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. He established the first permanent school of mathematics in Australia.
2. He lobbied for the creation of the Chair of Mathematics at the University of Melbourne.
3. He was the first to introduce some of the main techniques of modern engineering surveying in Australia.
4. He wrote the first treatise on surveying in Australia.
5. He provided important information on methods of resurveying colonial triangulations, thereby increasing the accuracy of maps and charts. 
6. He created a modern Australian map based on the British and French system of latitudes and longitudes, which allowed for better navigational accuracy.
7. He conducted a census of Victoria between 1833 and 1834.
8. He published the first journal devoted exclusively to mathematics in Australia.
9. He established the early foundations of mathematical geodesy.
10. He developed a calculus to calculate the accuracy of measurements.

Born 17 November 1597, Aldersgate, London, England. Died 16 February 1637, London, England.
