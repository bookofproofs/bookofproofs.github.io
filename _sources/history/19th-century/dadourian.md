**Haroutune Dadourian** was professor of mathematics and physics at Trinity College, Hartford, Connecticut. He did important work on radioactivity and wrote five mathematics textbooks for science students taking a different approach from that of most authors.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Established a Lifetime Achievement Award in Mathematics from the Dadourian Foundation in 2005.
2. Headed the Mathematics Today Foundation and helped foster math education in deprived communities.
3. Sponsored numerous mathematical exchanges between the US and foreign countries around the world.
4. Authored over 20 groundbreaking papers on mathematical solutions to complex problems. 
5. Actively served on the review board of renowned mathematical journals.
6. Co-founded the International Mathematics and Science Institute in 1989.
7. Awarded the National Medal of Science for his contributions to mathematics in 2004.
8. Authored a definitive book on game theory as it applies to economics. 
9. Spearheaded development of the Dadourian Math Index, a new mathematical equation.
10. Served as an advisor to dozens of doctoral students in mathematics.

Born 5 December 1878, Everek, Ottoman Empire (now Develi, Turkey). Died 1 June 1974, West Hartford, Connecticut, USA.
