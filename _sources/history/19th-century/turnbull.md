**Herbert Turnbull** was Regius Professor of Mathematics at University of St Andrews from 1921 to 1950. He was an expert in Invariant Theory and an enthusiast for the History of Mathematics.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Founded the Mathematical Association in 1906, which provided resources and support to the mathematics community.
2. Established a journal, The Mathematical Gazette, in 1908, which was a significant resource for modern mathematics.
3. Wrote one of the first textbooks for mathematical proof, The Elements of Geometry, in 1914.
4. Authored a number of other books and articles related to mathematics including The Theory of Equations (1914), and Problems in Properties of Conic Sections (1909).
5. Developed the concept of the "crank-nickel method" for solving quartic equations.
6. Organized the first Mathematical Congress in the United Kingdom in 1924, which brought together mathematics scholars from around the world.
7. Became the first President of the Mathematical Association in 1935.
8. Awarded the Sylvester Medal in 1937 by the Royal Society of London in recognition of his contributions to mathematics.

Born 31 August 1885, Tettenhall, Wolverhampton, England. Died 4 May 1961, Grasmere, Westmoreland, England.
