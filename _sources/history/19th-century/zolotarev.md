**Egor Zolotarev** was a Russian mathematician who produced fundamental work on analysis and number theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed several methods for solving linear and integral equations in finite difference and interpolation.
2. Proposed a general method for solving nonlinear equations of various kinds.
3. Developed methods for the numerical solution of differential equations.
4. Published a number of theoretical works on the theory of probability, the theory of constants, and the theory of transforms.
5. Served as a professor of mathematics at the University of Moscow.
6. Co-author of numerical methods textbook.
7. Developed efficient algorithms for solving systems of linear equations.
8. Set up mathematical models of meteorological processes using integral equations. 
9. Co-author of several papers on the computational theory of ideal fluids.
 10. Developed numerical methods for studying the dynamics of incompressible and compressible fluids.

Born 12 April 1847, St Petersburg, Russia. Died 19 July 1878, St Petersburg, Russia.
