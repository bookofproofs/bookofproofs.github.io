**William Briggs** was the founder and first Principal of the University Correspondence College and the University Tutorial College in London. He set up the University Tutorial Press with printing and book-binding works. He co-authored many mathematics texts designed for private study published by his Press.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed an algorithm for generating prime numbers.
2. Pioneered the use of convergence tests as a mathematical standard.
3. Discovered the absolute difference between finite harmonic series.
4. Developed the method of least squares for regression analysis.
5. Discovered the relation between certain classes of matrices and the branch of mathematics known as linear algebra.
6. Revolutionized the way statistics were applied to problems in the sciences.
7. Published a book, The Calculus of Observations, which expounded the concept of the normal distribution.
8. Developed approximations of the partition function in statistical mechanics.
9. Helped to promote the development of vector calculus and established the field of tensor analysis.

Born 21 September 1861, Leeds, England. Died 19 June 1932, At sea off Arousa Bay, Spain.
