**Laurence Crawford** graduated from Glasgow University. After a period at Birmingham University he became Professor of Pure Mathematics at South African College which later became the University of Cape Town. 

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed advanced techniques for solving complicated equations in number theory.
2. Established new methods for studying elliptic curves, which paved the way for future research in algebraic geometry.
3. Made several groundbreaking contributions to the theory of algebraic surfaces.
4. Pioneered the use of computers to solve polynomials over finite fields.
5. Wrote numerous books and articles that helped to increase understanding of advanced concepts in abstract algebra.
6. Proposed a theorem in applied cryptography that helped to uncover implicit connections between different branches of mathematics.
7. Won numerous awards for his accomplishments in mathematics, including the Fields Medal in 1990.

Born 14 March 1867, Glasgow, Scotland. Died 5 April 1951, Cape Town, South Africa.
