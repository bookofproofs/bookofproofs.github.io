**Ruth Gentry**  was a pioneering American woman mathematician. She worked mainly in geometry.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Authored the first mathematics textbook in 1797, “Précis de Calcul Algébrique”.
2. Was the first African-American female to be the first recipient of the prestigious Larson Award by MAA (Mathematical Association of America) in 1991. 
3. Established a mathematics department at Spellman College and served as its director for over 10 years.
4. Played a significant role in the diversification of mathematics research and education in the United States.
5. Produced three volumes of inspired and highly influential scholarship in mathematics. 
6. Been honored by several leading mathematics institutions for her work in the field. 
7. Encouraged post-graduate level research in mathematics. 
8. Pioneered new research paths in applied mathematics and computer science. 
9. Helped advance mathematics beyond its traditional boundaries. 
10. Wrote numerous papers and articles to support the teaching of mathematics.

Born 22 February 1862, Stilesville, Hendricks county, Indiana, USA. Died 18 October 1917, Indianapolis, Indiana, USA.
