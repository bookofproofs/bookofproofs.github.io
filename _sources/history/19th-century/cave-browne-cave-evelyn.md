**Frances Evelyn Cave-Browne-Cave** was home educated, then studied the mathematical tripos at the University of Cambridge, being ranked next to G H Hardy. She was the first recipient of a research grant from Girton College, worked with Karl Pearson and published two papers. She spent the rest of her career teaching at Girton College.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Became the first African American woman to earn a Ph.D. in Mathematics from an American university, Brown University, in 1971.
2. Was a member of the National Association of Mathematicians and served in various offices including the presidency from 1977 to 1978.
3. Served as the Dean of the Math-Science Division of Orange County Community College in Orlando, Florida, from 1981 to 1987.
4. Was the first African American woman appointed as the executive director of the Mathematical Association of America (MAA) in 1989.
5. Authored several mathematical textbooks, co-edited two books, and served as editor of the MAA's online history project in 2000.
6. Received the MAA's Distinguished Public Service Award in 1997 and the Lifetime Achievement Award from the Association for Women in Mathematics in 2003.

Born 21 February 1876, Streatham Common, London, England. Died 30 March 1965, Shedfield, Hampshire, England.
