**Ethel Elderton** was a statistician who worked for Francis Galton and Karl Pearson. Although her work was an important contribution applying statistics to social problems, much of it makes difficult reading today because it is written with a eugenic perspective.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Became a Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh, making her the first woman to become a Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh for mathematical achievements.
2. Gave lectures on various mathematical subjects at the University of Edinburgh.
3. Published several mathematical papers, including one on the theory of transformation groups.
4. Was a consultant to the Royal Observatory at the University of Edinburgh.
5. Became a professor and the first woman to teach applied mathematics at the University of St. Andrews.
6. Published a book on mathematical theory, entitled Arithmatic and Algebraic Transformations.
7. Contributed to the development of tensor analysis and differential geometry.
8. Was awarded the Keith Medal of the Royal Society of Edinburgh for her significant mathematical achievements.

Born 31 December 1878, Fulham, London, England. Died 5 May 1954, Stanborough Park, near Watford, Hertfordshire, England.
