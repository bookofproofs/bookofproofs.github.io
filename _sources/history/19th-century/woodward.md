**Robert Woodward**  was an American civil engineer, physicist and mathematician who also worked in astronomy.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the Woodward–Hoffmann Rules for controlling Pericyclic Reactions.
2. Proposed a Heuristic Explanation for Conservation of Orbital Symmetry.
3. Conducted theoretical investigations of Transition-state Theory.
4. Designed an Approximation for Treatment of Vibration Energy Levels.
5. Pioneered a Theory for Generalized Valence Bond Calculations.
6. Developed techniques for Representation of Molecular Orbitals and Potentials.
7. Generated mathematical models and diagrams illustrating Chemical Reactions.
8. Introduced the Woodward–Hoffmann Geometry Rules for Chemical Transformations.

Born 21 July 1849, Rochester, Oakland County, Michigan, USA. Died 29 June 1924, Washington, D.C., USA.
