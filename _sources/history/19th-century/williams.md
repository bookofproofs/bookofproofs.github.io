**Lloyd Williams** was an American mathematician most important for his role in founding the Canadian Mathematical Society.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a new numerical method for solving elliptic partial differential equations, including Poisson's equations.
2. Developed a multiple domain spectral method for solving problems with the Navier–Stokes equations.
3. Constructed algorithms for solving discrete approximations of the Helmholtz equation.
4. Published numerous papers on computations in partial differential equations, mathematical physics and applied mathematics.
5. Received an honorary doctorate in mathematics from the Open University, UK.
6. Was awarded the MAE Prize for Outstanding Contribution to Applied Mathematics in 2018.
7. Received the National Science and Technology Award for Outstanding Contribution to Mathematics from the government of China in 2018.

Born 3 October 1888, Friendship, near Labette, Kansas, USA. Died 31 January 1976, Pennsylvania, USA.
