**Victor Puiseux** worked on elliptic functions and studied computational methods in astronomy.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Published three important mathematical works in the field of algebraic analysis in the 1830s and 1840s. 
2. Published important results on differential equations in 1847. 
3. Pioneered modern methods for handling infinite series. 
4. Derived new techniques for extracting roots of algebraic equations. 
5. Demonstrated the use of non-positive integral index in calculus. 
6. Defined the concept of Puiseux series. 
7. Introduced the concept of virtual functions into algebraic analysis. 
8. Researched irrational and transcendental functions. 
9. Described ways to construct functions with specific properties. 
10. Provided a detailed analysis of the theory of differential equations.

Born 16 April 1820, Argenteuil, Val-d'Oise, France. Died 9 September 1883, Frontenay, Jura, France.
