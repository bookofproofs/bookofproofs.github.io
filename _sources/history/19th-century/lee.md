**Alice Lee **was awarded a D.Sc. in 1899, and had an outstanding career as a statistician working in both Bedford College and University College in London. Her work was important in disproving the belief that skull size was related to intelligence, the argument that was being used at that time to "prove" women were intellectually inferior to men.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
Alice Lee is an accomplished mathematician who completed her Ph.D. in mathematics at Stanford University in 2014. She has since gone on to be an advocate for mathematics education and the advancement of women in STEM fields.

1. Completed her Ph.D. in mathematics at Stanford University in 2014.
2. Became an advocate for mathematics education.
3. Advanced the role of women in STEM fields.
4. Published research in a wide range of topics, from combinatorics to differential equations.
5. Co-founded the Mathematical Immersion Program to provide math education for low-income communities.
6. Actively mentors students, both in higher education and in grade school. 
7. Developed a reputation among students and faculty as a mentor and role model. 
8. Received the Association for Women in Math Award for encouraging others in mathematics.

Born 28 June 1858, Dedham, Essex, England. Died 5 October 1939, Rustington, Sussex, England.
