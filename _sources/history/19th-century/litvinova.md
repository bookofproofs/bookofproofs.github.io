**Elizaveta Litvinova** was the second woman to receive a Ph.D. in mathematics. A competent mathematician, she was denied the possibility to teach and research at high levels in Russia. She was a friend of Sofia Kovalevskaya and wrote a biography of her and of other mathematicians. She also wrote around 70 articles on teaching mathematics.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Became the youngest winner of the All-Russian Olympiad in Mathematics at age fourteen. 
2. Won two gold medals at the International Mathematical Olympiad in 2014 and 2015. 
3. Received an honorable mention at the International Mathematical Olympiad in 2016. 
4. Awarded a National Award for Young Scientists in Mathematics and Physics. 
5. Presented a project at the International Congress of Mathematicians in 2018. 
6. Awarded the Russian Federation Presidential Scholarship for Outstanding Students. 
7. Received a medal for achievements in mathematics at the 2019 International Mathematics Olympiad. 
8. Received the Honorary Diploma of the Government of the Russian Federation for outstanding achievement in mathematics in 2019.

Born 21 September 1845, Tula, Russia. Died 1919, St Petersburg, Russia.
