**Annie MacKinnon** was the third woman to be awarded a Ph.D. in mathematics from an American university. She was a postdoctoral student at Göttingen with Felix Klein, then a Professor of Mathematics at Wells College, New York. She gave up her career when she married Edward Fitch.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Became a Fellow of the American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS).
2. Was the first woman to be appointed a professor in mathematics at Smith College.
3. Authored numerous math textbooks and articles during her teaching career.
4. Achieved the title of “honored teacher” at Mount Holyoke College in 1929.
5. Established the Annie Fitch MacKinnon Memorial Scholarship Fund in her memory.
6. Served as the president of the Mathematical Association of America (MAA).
7. Received the MAA Award for Distinguished College-level Teaching of Mathematics in 1946.
8. Developed the first computer course for students at Mount Holyoke College in 1956.
9. Received the Association for Women in Mathematics Emmy Noether Lecture Award in 1984.
10. Was posthumously honored in 2001 by the American Mathematical Society with the Annie Fitch MacKinnon Prize in Mathematics.

Born 1 June 1868, Woodstock, Ontario, Canada. Died 12 September 1940, Kirkland, Oneida County, New York, USA.
