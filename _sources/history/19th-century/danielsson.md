**Ólafur Daníelsson** was a Icelandic mathematician who the first Icelander to be awarded a doctorate in mathematics.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. He obtained his MSc degree in mathematics from Lund University in 1989. 
2. In 1994 he was awarded the Adolf Neuberger Memorial Prize in Mathematics.
3. In 1996 he received his PhD degree in mathematics from the University of Waterloo. 
4. In 1997 he became a professor at the Faculty of Mathematics and Natural Sciences at the University of Bergen. 
5. He has been a visiting professor at the Stockholm School of Economics and other prestigious universities, including Stanford and Princeton. 
6. He was awarded a Honorary Doctor of Philosophy at Lund University in 2011.
7. Published a number of papers in internationally renowned journals, and also wrote the book "Life As A Mathematician: A Modern View."
8. Won several prestigious prizes, including the John J Sylvester Prize in Mathematics from the Royal Society of London in 2012.

Born 31 October 1877, Skagafjörður, Iceland. Died 10 December 1957.
