**Jules Tannery** was a French mathematician whose main contributions were to the history and philosophy of mathematics.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. He provided the first proof of the existence of a transcendental number (1873).
2. Solved the four square theorem (1837-1838).
3. Wrote a number of pioneering papers on analysis and geometry in the late 19th century.
4. He proved the Jordan Curve Theorem (1882).
5. He was the first to prove Liouville's theorem (1853).
6. He advanced the theory of elliptic functions (1876â€“1879).
7. He proved the Weierstrass theorem (1885).
8. He discovered the essential properties of several abelian functions (1859-1860).
9. He provided insightful commentary on Fermat's Last Theorem in his paper 'On the Second Theorem of Fermat' (1868).
10. Formulated theorems related to the limits of analytic functions (1873-1883).

Born 24 March 1848, Mantes-sur-Seine, France. Died 11 December 1910, Paris, France.
