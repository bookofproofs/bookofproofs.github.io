**James Watt** was an Edinburgh lawyer and actuary. He was interested in science, being a member of the Scottish Meteorological Society and the Royal Scottish Geographical Society.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a proportional compass to calculate angular distances in mathematics.
2.Introduced the concept of Horse-Power to quantify mechanical power.
3. Perfected the steam engine, a cornerstone of the Industrial Revolution.
4. Derived the mathematical formula to calculate the volume levels of steam engines.
5. Invented and patented the concept of rotary motion. 
6. Invented the steam pressure gauge. 
7. Wrote extensively on steam engines, including topics such as thermodynamic cycles. 
8. Pioneered the first successful general gas law and other mathematical calculations.

Born 21 March 1863, Edinburgh, Scotland. Died 3 December 1945, Edinburgh, Scotland.
