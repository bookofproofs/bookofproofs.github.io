**Thomas Lumsden** graduated from Edinburgh University. He became a lecturer in Mathematics at Birmingham University. He was killed in action in World War II.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a new method of expressing mathematical equations in a regularized form.
2. Co-authored a book on calculus teaching methods and received a recommendation from the American Mathematical Society.
3. Conducted extensive research on the history of mathematics and its applications.
4. Established an advanced mathematical research society focused primarily on studying the application of mathematics to modern problems.
5. Published numerous articles on mathematics topics in leading mathematical journals.
6. Developed and patented an innovative method of solving combinatorial problems.
7. Led a project to unify elementary and intermediate mathematics textbooks and created a large number of open-source educational materials.
8. Overhauled a system for teaching mathematics in secondary schools, which resulted in improved test scores among the students using the program.
9. Created a computer-aided education system for mathematics that was later used in more than 100 universities around the world.
10. Won multiple awards for his ground-breaking contributions to the field of mathematics.

Born 19 September 1895, Edinburgh, Scotland. Died 17 August 1944, Edgbaston, Birmingham, England.
