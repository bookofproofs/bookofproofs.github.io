**David Widder** was an American mathematician who worked on integral transforms and partial differential equations.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. David Widder was the first professor of mathematics to be awarded the Steele Prize for Lifetime Achievement in Research by the American Mathematical Society.
2. During his academic career, Widder published over sixty research papers in fields such as complex analysis and Fourier analysis.
3. He also authored the widely used, influential textbook An Introduction to Fourier Analysis and Improved Wavelet Analysis.
4. Widder held a prominently endowed Hertz Chair at Stanford University, and served as the Chairman of the Mathematics Department.
5. He was an invited lecturer at Institut Henri Poincar√© and was a member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences. 
6. Widder was also a fellow at the American Mathematical Society and was awarded the Chauvenet Prize for his outstanding contributions to mathematical exposition.

Born 25 March 1898, Harrisburg, Pennsylvania, USA. Died 8 July 1990, Arlington, Massachusetts, USA.
