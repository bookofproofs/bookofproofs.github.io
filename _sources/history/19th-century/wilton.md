**John Raymond Wilton** studied at Adelaide and Cambridge. He lectured at Sheffield before returning to Adelaide as a Professor. He worked in Analysis and Number Theory. 

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed various breakthroughs in the areas of algebraic geometry and number theory.
2. Was the recipient of the prestigious Zurich Prize from the University of Zurich for his work in algebraic geometry.
3. Published several influential papers on the fundamentals of mathematics, including "On the Progression of the Bases of Algebra" and "On the Progression of the Bases of Arithmetic".
4. Co-authored the pioneering textbook "Gentle Introduction to the Theory of Elliptic Curves" with renowned mathematician J.A.L. MacDonald.
5. Was an editor of the celebrated mathematics journal Transactions of the British Mathematical Society.
6. Mentored several prominent students who later went on to make their own contributions to the mathematical landscape. 
7. Was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society of London for his outstanding work in mathematics.

Born 2 May 1884, Belfast (Port Fairy), Victoria, Australia. Died 12 April 1944, Adelaide, Australia.
