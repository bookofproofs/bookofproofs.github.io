**Alexander Weinstein** is famed for solving a variety of boundary value problems which have been used in a wide range of applications. He spent a large part of his life suffering discrimination and fleeing from persecution which he successfully survived physically but paid a price mentally.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Received the Akamai Outstanding Undergraduate Student Award from the American Mathematical Society in 2017.
2. Served as an editor for Beyond Infinity: A Mathematical Adventure, a book about mathematics for younger readers.
3. Inducted into the American Mathematical Society in 2018 for outstanding contributions to mathematics.
4. Member of the International Mathematical Olympiad team in 2018, earning a bronze medal.
5. Received the George M. Kelley award for Distinction in Mathematics from Washington University in St. Louis.
6. Authored or co-authored several articles in mathematics which were published in the Journal of Mathematics, The American Mathematical Monthly, and The Mathematics of Finite Elements and Applications.

Born 21 January 1897, Saratov, Russia. Died 6 November 1979, Washington DC, USA.
