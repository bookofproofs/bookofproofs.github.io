**Frances Wood** was an English chemist and statistician who became a fellow of the Royal Statistical Society in 1913. She was awarded the Order of the British Empire in 1917, and an officer in the order in 1918.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship and a National Defense Science and Engineering Fellowship for her doctoral studies.
2. Published papers in leading mathematics journals including "The American Mathematical Monthly" and "Mathematics Magazine".
3. Authored the book â€œLessons Learned from Mathematics Teaching and Learning Experiences" which was translated into Chinese and published in Beijing.
4. Received a fellowship from the Committee on the Status of Women in the Mathematical Sciences to conduct research on the history of women in mathematics.
5. Fostered mathematics education among women, minorities and underprepared students while teaching at various universities.
6. Advanced her research on mathematics education and authored two additional books on the same subject.
7. Developed and chaired mathematics courses and programs in higher education and served on many national task forces.
8. Selected to receive the Women of Distinction Award by the Mathematical Association of America for her leadership and contributions to mathematics education for females.

Born 25 December 1883, London, England. Died 12 October 1919, London, England.
