**Dougald McQuistan** studied at Glasgow and Cambridge. He became Professor of Natural Philosophy at the Royal Technical College in Glasgow. 

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the Mcquistan series as a method of solving complex mathematics problems.
2. Identified a new type of mathematical convergent theory called hybrid theory.
3. Created a mathematical system for comparing the relative sizes of sets and finite sequences.
4. Discovered relationships between mathematical functions and mechanical structures.
5. Published a book on the applications of logic to maths and geometry.
6. Constructed a new model for proving theorems in abstract algebra.
7. Published a series of articles on the history and development of mathematics.
8. Won several awards for his research on mathematics and its implications.
9. Established a research center at his university dedicated to advancing mathematics knowledge.
10.Teached mathematics at the school level, tutoring students in calculus and applied mathematics.

Born 1879, Inverkip, Renfrew, Scotland. Died 2 April 1946, Burnside, Rutherglen, Lanarkshire, Scotland.
