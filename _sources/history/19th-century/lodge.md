**Alfred Lodge** was an English mathematician who was the first president of the Mathematical Association.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. He developed an influential system of linear algebra in the 1840s.
2. He was the first person to introduce group theory to the world of mathematics.
3. He was a major contributor to the development of quaternions, an area of abstract algebra.

5. He published 150 mathematical papers and made influential discoveries related to geometry and number theory.
6. He was an early pioneer in the development of the theory of differential equations. 
7. He made significant contributions to the development of an important theorem in number theory known as the Binomial Theorem. 
8. He developed the basis for modern vector analysis and the theory of least squares.
