**Sierpiński**'s most important work is in the area of set theory, point set topology and number theory. In set theory he made important contributions to the axiom of choice and to the continuum hypothesis.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Discovered the Sierpiński Triangle, a fractal known as the Sierpiński gasket.
2. Established several important theories related to set theory and cardinal arithmetic.
3. Introduced the Sierpiński–Nagumo theorem.
4. Developed the Sierpiński curve and space filling Sierpiński curve.
5. Researched the problem of Steinhaus–Moser and its variations.
6. Proved the Calculus of Finite Differences Equations.
7. Founded Surreal Numbers theory.
8. Discovered the prime-representing functions.
9. Established fixed point theorems.
10. Developed the Sierpiński Number theory.

Born 14 March 1882, Warsaw, Russian Empire (now Poland). Died 21 October 1969, Warsaw, Poland.
