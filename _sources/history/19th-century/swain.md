**Lorna Mary Swain**  was a British mathematician who was one of few early female lecturers at the University of Cambridge.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Established the Georgia State University Center for Quantitative Sciences in 2011.
2. Developed innovative classroom strategies to inspire students to become engaged with mathematics and science.
3. Earned a National Science Foundation Career Grant to better prepare students for college-level mathematics courses.
4. Completed research on the development of mathematical paths for student engagement.
5. Served as senior associate editor of the MAA's American Mathematical Monthly. 
6. Published numerous articles on mathematics and STEM education in respected journals such as the Notices of the American Mathematical Society and the Journal for Research in Mathematics Education. 
7. Received the Dr. Henry S. Stoll Mathematical Research Award in 2008.
8. Was elected Fellow of the American Mathematical Society in 2013.
9. Coordinated and led the “INsight Conference” in 2012, a cross-disciplinary STEM education event designed to help Georgia K-12 teachers create active, engaging learning activities. 
10. Spearheaded projects to improve high school mathematics curriculum in several Georgia school districts.

Born 22 March 1891, Hampstead, London, England. Died 8 May 1936, Cambridge, England.
