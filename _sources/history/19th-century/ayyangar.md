**Krishnaswami Ayyangar** was an Indian mathematician who worked in Mysore. He produced important work on the history of Hindu mathematics. 

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Founded the Indian Mathematical Society and served as its first General Secretary. 
2. Assisted in the formation of the All-India Mathematics Congregation in 1922. 
3. Directorate of Public Instruction in Madras from 1930 to 1933.
4. Played an important role in the development of mathematical education in India.
5. Developed the post-graduate syllabus in mathematics at the University of Madras. 
6. Contributed to the compilation of the Test-Books in Mathematics for secondary schools in India. 
7. Assisted the Indian Mathematical Society in organizing numerous mathematics conferences in India.
8. Authored several publications in mathematics, including textbooks in algebra and trigonometry.

Born 1 December 1892, Attipattu, Chingleput district, Tamil Nadu, India. Died June 1953, Mysore, India.
