**Charles Weatherburn** worked on vector analysis and differential geometry.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Appointed Professor of Mathematics at the University of Sydney in 1945. 
2. Played an important role in the design and establishment of the mathematics department at the University of Sydney.
3. Established the Australian Mathematical Society in 1956.
4. Awarded the George Szekeres Medal by the Australian Mathematical Society in 1982. 
5. Co-authored the book “The Theory of Landauers and its Applications” with George Szekeres in 1960. 
6. Earned a Doctor of Science degree from the University of Sydney in 1949. 
7. Developed the Weatherburn-Hughes Method for solving Bellman equations in 1956. 
8. Published about 100 papers on analytic and algebraic topics. 
9. Served as President of the Australian Mathematical Society from 1963 to 1965. 
10. Received the Order of Australia Award in 1982.

Born 18 June 1884, Chippendale, Sydney, Australia. Died 18 October 1974, Claremont, Perth, Australia.
