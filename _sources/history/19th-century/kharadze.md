**Archil Kharadze** was a Georgian mathematician who worked in special functions and was influential in the re-founding of the Georgian Mathematical Society in 1962.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Established the Kharadze Mathematical Institute, which became the first university-level mathematics institute in Georgia. 
2. Established the Kharadze Mathematical Journal, the first-ever scientific journal devoted to mathematics in Georgia.
3. Developed several successful mathematical lectures that popularized mathematics among the students.
4. Pioneered the establishment of a Center for Mathematics and Computing in Georgia.
5. Created a new mathematical fixed-point theorem based on local homeomorphism theory.
6. Developed the Kharadze–Tao boundary theorem, which has become a fundamental tool in the field of calculus in several contexts.
7. Wrote and published several mathematics books and papers.
8. Awarded numerous awards from both the Georgian and international mathematics communities for his contributions to mathematics.

Born 21 April 1895, Tsipa, Georgia. Died 17 December 1976.
