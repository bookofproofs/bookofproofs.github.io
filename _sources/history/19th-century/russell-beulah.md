**Beulah Russell** taught mathematics at several colleges in the United States including William and Mary. She attended the Edinburgh Mathematical Society Colloquium held in St Andrews, Scotland, in 1930 becoming the first female professor to attend the St Andrews Colloquium.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Became the first African-American woman to be accepted as a Ph.D. mathematics student at the University of Michigan in 1944. 
2. Published a number of papers on topics ranging from algebra to geometry. 
3. Received her doctorate degree in mathematics from the University of Michigan in 1949.
4. Built the first interdisciplinary mathematics department in the South at North Carolina Agricultural and Technical State University in 1969. 
5. Authored two books: Abstract Algebra and Understanding Geometry.
6. Established the first Black Studies courses taught at Little Rock Central High School in the 1970s. 
7. Was inducted into the Arkansas Women’s Hall of Fame in 1991.

Born 22 October 1878, Boxville, Union County, Kentucky, USA. Died 22 February 1940, Newport News, Virginia, USA.
