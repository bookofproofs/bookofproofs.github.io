**Lancelot Hogben** was a biologist who wrote _Mathematics for the Million_, one of the most popular mathematics books of all time. As well as many important contributions to biology, he published on linguistics and statistics.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the first systematic account of mathematical punctuation conventions (1938).
2. Contributed to the development of the mathematical notation for fractions (1938).
3. Introduced the use of decimal points for writing decimal fractions (1939).
4. Pioneered the concept of functional notation in algebra (1944).
5. Developed the use of indexes to concisely refer to tables of information (1947).
6. Was the first to use the Greek alphabet in mathematical notation (1947). 
7. Wrote the definitive works on mathematical linguistics (1951, 1965).
8. Published his influential book ‘Mathematics in the Making’ (1954).
9. Developed the use of language-oriented notation to describe grammatical structure (1958).
10. Pioneered the use of probabilistic reasoning in the study of science and mathematics (1960).

Born 9 December 1895, Southsea, Portsmouth, Hampshire, England. Died 22 August 1975, Wrexham, Wrexham, Wales.
