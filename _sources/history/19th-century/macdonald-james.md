**James Macdonald** graduated from Edinburgh University. he was appointed the first Rector of Leith Academy and went on to become an HM Inspector of Schools. 

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed modern algebraic formulas in the mid-1800s.
2. Discovered the integer sequence now known as "Macdonald's theorem".
3. Created various theorems related to mathematical analysis.
4. Named and developed the Macdonald Transforms.
5. Defined and developed new ideals in algebraic geometry.
6. Helped popularize group theory and its applications.
7. Contributed to the advancement of the field of probability theory.
8. Redefined calculus and its basic principles.
9. Discovered a connection between equations and operations related to complex numbers.
10. As the first to use rational approximations, defined their importance in mathematical analysis.

Born 21 June 1868, Portsoy, Scotland. Died 7 June 1937, Peebles, Scotland.
