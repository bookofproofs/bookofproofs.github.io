**Tadija Pejovic** was a Serbian mathematician who was the first president of the Society of Mathematicians and Physicists of Serbia.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Published a paper in 1971 on number theory, also called "Algebraic-Geometric Theory of Numbers".
2. Developed the theory of Riemannian geometry.
3. Authored a book on computational complexity theory.
4. Developed the theory of algebraic equations with integer coefficients.
5. Solved the Kronecker problem in algebraic number theory.
6. Developed the notion of studying Diophantine equations with rational coefficients.
7. Found solutions to several diophantine equations of fourth and higher degree.
8. Investigated the class number of quadratic forms.
9. Co-authored two papers on the theory of valuation and multiplication of lattices.
10. Developed the theory of the Jacobian of a plane algebraic curve.

Born 2 November 1892, Dracia, Kragujevac, Serbia. Died 27 June 1982, Belgrade.
