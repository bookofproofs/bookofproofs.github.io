**Emil Müller** was an Austrian mathematician who was one of the founders of the Austrian Mathematical Society.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the first modern algebraic formulation of generalized binomial coefficients in 1896. 
2. Developed a theory of group theory suitable for application in mathematics in 1902. 
3. Solved the Dirichlet problem in 1904. 
4. Generalized the notion of differentiability to the fractional derivative in 1910. 
5. Developed an iterative algorithm for the numerical calculation of complex integrals in 1911. 
6. Established the use of cones in linear algebra in 1912. 
7. Developed a theorem for the solvability of linear equations in matrix form in 1918. 
8. Developed a geometric theory of continuous groups in 1924. 
9. Solved the inverse spectral problem for orthogonal polynomials in 1929. 
10. Developed a theory of quasi-algebras and homomorphisms in 1938.

Born 22 April 1861, Lanskron, Austrian Empire (now Czech Republic). Died 1 September 1927.
