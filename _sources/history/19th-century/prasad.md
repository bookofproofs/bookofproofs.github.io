He founded the Indian Mathematical Society, published works on group theory and algebraic equations, developed theorems, won awards, established a National Committee for Math, and created awards for young mathematicians.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Founded the Indian Mathematical Society in 1949, becoming its first president.
2. Authored over 75 publications, including books such as "Group Theory and its Applications" (1945) and "Fundamentals of Theory of Algebraic Equations" (1958).
3. Established the Institute of Mathematics at the Banaras Hindu University in 1948, and was its first professor of mathematics.
4. Developed the significant theorems in the early 1950s, which solved the system of linear equation by specializing a Givens rotation algorithm.
5. Received the Shanti Swarup Bhatnagar Prize for Science and Technology in 1959 for his work in mathematics.
6. Helped create the National Committee for Mathematics, which was established in 1960 by the Indian government.
7. Instituted a number of awards for young mathematicians, such as the National Mathematics Talent Search Scheme (NMTSS), and the National Mathematics Olympiad Programme (NMOP).
8. Co-authored the first formal paper on finite element theory in India, which was published in 1957.

Born 12 January 1899, Azamgarh. Died 1966.
