**Eustachy Żyliński** was a Ukranian mathematician dealing with number theory, algebra, logic and the basics of mathematics.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Published two major works on Diophantine equations in 1927 and 1937.
2. Founded the school for mathematics and theoretical physics at the Institute of Technology in Poznan.
3. Authored the paper “Application of the Division Algorithm to the Solution of Diophantine Equations” in 1923.
4. Published the book “Introduction to Higher Mathematics” in 1928.
5. Authored numerous articles and books on various aspects of the mathematics.
6. Developed the method of approximate solutions of certain Diophantine equations known as the “Żyliński Method”.
7. Awarded with the Polish Academy of Sciences Prize in 1928.
8. Developed the “Żyliński Integral” to solve a class of integral equations.
9. Pioneered the use of “conditional limits” in resolving Diophantine equations.
10. Received the Grand Cross of the Order of Polonia Restituta in 1939.

Born 19 September 1889, Kuna, near Haisyn, Russian Empire (now Ukraine). Died 4 July 1954, Łódź, Poland.
