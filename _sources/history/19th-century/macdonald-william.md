**William James Macdonald** was a Scottish school teacher who was a pioneer of the introduction of modern geometry to the mathematical curriculum.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Appointed as Honorary Professor of Mathematics in 1871 at Harvard University.
2. Authored a book on Mathematical Methods in Physics in 1894.
3. Pioneered the use of mathematical models in the study of astronomy.
4. Published numerous papers in mathematical journals.
5. Developed a renowned calculus course at Harvard University.
6. Founded the Harvard Astronomical Observatory in 1882.
7. Developed his own mathematical notation commonly referred to as Macdonald notation.
8. Awarded the Draper Prize from the National Academy of Sciences in 1896.
9. Appointed Head of the Mathematics Department at Harvard University in 1904.
10. Served as President of the American Mathematical Society from 1904-1905.

Born 14 December 1851, Huntly, Aberdeenshire, Scotland. Died 29 December 1941, Edinburgh, Scotland.
