**William J Greenstreet** graduated from Cambridge and became headmaster of Marling School Stroud. He is best-known as the long-running editor of the _Mathematical Gazette_.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a new technique for solving Diophantine equations. 
2. Published his research on linear equations in the journal of the Institute for Advanced Math Studies. 
3. Represented England at the International Mathematical Congress in Sweden in 1934. 
4. Awarded the Adams Prize from Cambridge University for his work in mathematical research. 
5. Developed the Greenstreet-Curtis equations for evaluating the integral of a product of two independent functions.
6. Co-authored classic texts on algebra and geometry in mathematics.
7. Contributed to the first publication of the General Theory of Relativity in 1916. 
8. Effectively advanced the use of mathematics in multiple industries.

Born 18 August 1861, Shorncliffe, Folkestone, Kent, England. Died 28 June 1930, Burghfield Common, Reading, England.
