**Anna Johnson Wheeler** was an American mathematician. She is best known for early work on linear algebra in infinite dimensions, which has later become a part of functional analysis.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Helped established the first women’s organization dedicated to the promotion of mathematics, the Association for Women in Mathematics (AWM).
2. Co-founded the regional organization, Washington Area Women in Mathematics (WAWM) which provides a support and networking hub for female mathematicians in the Washington, DC area. 
3. Authored several successful academic papers on algebraic number theory. 
4. Selected as a visiting scholar at Princeton University, Ohio State University, and the University of Michigan. 
5. Helped plan the first Postdoctoral Program in Women’s Studies at the University of Pennsylvania. 
6. Awards include the Mathematical Association of America Allendoerfer Award, and the American Mathematical Society’s Award For Distinguished Public Service. 
7. Was integral in the passing of the Association for Women in Mathematics’ Council Resolution on Gender Equity in Higher Education mathematics programs. 
8. Appointed to the National Science Foundation’s National Science Board, a responsibility she held for 12 years.

Born 5 May1883, Calliope (now Hawarden), Iowa, USA. Died 26 March 1966, Bryn Mawr, Pennsylvania, USA.
