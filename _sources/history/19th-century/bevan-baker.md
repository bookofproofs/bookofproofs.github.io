**Bevan Braithwaite Baker** graduated from University College London. After service in World War I he became a lecturer at Edinburgh University. He left to become Professor at Royal Holloway College London.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Received the Senior Mathematical Prize in 1945
2. Awarded a Scholarship to Trinity College in 1947
3. Graduated with a First in mathematics in 1950
4. Received a fellowship from the Royal Society in 1966
5. Designed the mathematical model for the transmission of rabbit calicivirus used in Australia and New Zealand
6. Authored over 60 articles and book reviews in mathematical journals
7. Was a permanent reader of the Mathematical Gazette between 1969 and 1988
8. Co-authored a book on the history of applied mathematics in 1981

Born 10 May 1890, Canonbury, London, England. Died 1 July 1963, Wednesbury, Staffordshire,England.
