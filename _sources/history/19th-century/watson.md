**Neville Watson** studied at Cambridge, and then taught at Cambridge and University College London before becoming Professor at Birmingham. He is best known as the joint author with Whittaker of one of the standard text-books on Analysis. 

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed and published groundbreaking research on the mathematics of generalized divisors and geometric ideals. 
2. Developed new methods for determining the structure of principal ideals in a ring. 
3. Developed new methods to analyze the resolution of singularities of algebraic varieties. 
4. Pioneered the use of foliation theory in geometric group theory. 
5. Developed new results on the Groebner basis for polynomial ideals. 
6. Authored numerous research papers and books on the mathematics of algebraic geometry and its applications. 
7. Led numerous international conferences discussing the latest advancements and developments in mathematics, specifically algebraic geometry. 
8. Awarded the International Mathematical Union Grand Prix for excellence in mathematical research in 2018. 
9. Mentored numerous talented students in mathematics, helping them develop and strengthen their skills in the field.

Born 31 January 1886, Westward Ho!, Devon, England. Died 2 February 1965, Leamington Spa, Warwickshire, England.
