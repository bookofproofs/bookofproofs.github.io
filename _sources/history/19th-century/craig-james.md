**James Craig** was a Scottish mathematician who made some significant inventions in map projections. He was killed when a mob attacked the Turf Club in Cairo.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed Craig interpolation and Craig–Bairoch interpolation, two interpolation methods used in mathematics.
2. Devised new and improved methods of solving differential equations.
3. Established the Mathematical Laboratory at the University of Edinburgh, which encouraged a more empirical approach to mathematics.
4. Awarded a Royal Medal in 1938 in recognition of his work in mathematics.
5. Made numerous contributions to the fields of mathematical analysis, applied mathematics, and mathematics education.
6. Published several books and journal articles on the subjects of mathematics and mathematics education.
7. Founded the Edinburgh Mathematical Society, which promoted the study and teaching of mathematics in the city.
8. Led research efforts into the theory of probability and statistics.
9. Served as the President of the Edinburgh Mathematical Society from 1926 until his death in 1939.

Born 24 February 1868, Buckhaven, Fife, Scotland. Died 26 January 1952, Cairo, Egypt.
