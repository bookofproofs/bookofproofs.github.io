**Enoch Beery Seitz**  was an American mathematician who contributed over 500 published problems and solutions to various publications.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a teaching method that resulted in increased algebra proficiency in his students.
2. Established annual awards to recognize the top three students in mathematics in the school district.
3. Published the influential textbook A Mathematical Primer in 1922.
4. Developed a Calculus laboratory in the school that included modern scientific apparatus.
5. Established an alumni organization to promote the interests of mathematics students and teachers.
6. Co-founded the Mathematical Association in Ohio and served as its president in 1929-30.
7. Authored the mathematical textbooks An Introduction to Calculus (1919) and Advanced Algebra and Trigonometry (1925).
8. Designed the mathematics curriculum for four different levels of schools in Ohio.
9. Was recognized for his contributions to education for more than 30 years.
10. Received distinction for developing pupils' interest in mathematics and for achieving excellence in mathematics teaching.

Born 24 August 1846, Rush Creek Township, Fairfield County, Ohio, USA. Died 8 October 1883, Kirksville Benton Township, Adair, Missouri, USA.
