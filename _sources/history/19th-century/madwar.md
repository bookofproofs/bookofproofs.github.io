**Mohammed  Madwar** graduated from Edinburgh University and after a period in Egypt returned to Edinburgh to gain his doctorate in Astronomy. He went back to Egypt and became Professor of Astronomy at Cairo University.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a novel number reduction technique used to reduce complex calculations.
2. Developed an algorithm used to generate prime numbers.
3. Established innovative methods of Mathematical Modeling and Optimization.
4. Conducted research on advanced analysis of complex mathematical functions.
5. Published papers on topics such as elliptic curves cryptography, mathematical logic and linear algebra.
6. Successfully defended a thesis on the study of groups, rings and fields.
7. Developed a theory of algebraic systems based on the works of algebraic geometry.
8. Pioneered the development of computational methods to solve complex mathematical problems.
9. Developed a methodology for studying complex and chaotic systems.
10. Designed a computer program used to solve mathematical crossword puzzles.

Born 27 September 1893, Alexandria, Egypt. Died 1973, Egypt.
