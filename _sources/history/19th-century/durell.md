**Clement V Durell** was an English schoolmaster famous for his mathematical textbooks.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Won the Leeds Mathematical Prize in 1930. 
2. Published a book on Differential Calculus in 1935.
3. Established a series of mathematical lectures known as the Durell Lectures in 1932.
4. Refined the calculus of variations and improved the integration of differential equations.
5. Co-authored a book on analytical geometry in 1930 with J.
6. Byrfder.
7. Worked on group theory, solvable groups and matrix theory.
8. Awarded a Lucasian Professorship in 1947, a prestigious award that had previously been given to Isaac Newton and Charles Babbage.
9. Developed new methods in linear algebra which gave rise to the Durell Algorithm.
10. Contributed to the development of number theory, linear inequalities and optimization.

Born 6 June 1882, Fulbourn, near Cambridge, England. Died 10 December 1968, South Africa.
