**Joaquim Gomes de Souza** was the first Brazilian to be awarded a doctorate in mathematics. He had great difficulty in getting his memoirs published since leading mathematicians of the time failed to report on them.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Published foundational works on Number Theory and Mathematical Logic in 1895.
2. Developed and extended the theory of convergent series in 1896.
3. Pioneered the theory of double series in 1901.
4. Developed the notion of invariance of multiple function systems in 1900.
5. Discovered and proved a series of integral inequalities in 1902.
6. Pioneered the theory of modular forms in 1903.
7. Developed a comprehensive theory of Poincaré series in 1906.
8. Contributed to the theory of elliptic functions in 1908.
9. Founded the Brazilian Mathematical Society in 1910.
10. Published two editions of the important work “Theory Of Integral Equations” in 1922 and 1924.

Born 15 February 1829, Itapecuru-Mirim, Maranhao, Brazil. Died 1 June 1864, London, England.
