**Neil McArthur** graduated from Glasgow University and apart from a period of war service spent his whole career at the University. 

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Pioneered mathematical methods to improve the understanding of stability and control of aircraft.
2. Discovered new insights into the behavior of nonlinear dynamical systems.
3. Developed techniques to better understand the collective motion of particles in fluids.
4. Developed many theories in the field of chaos theory and nonlinear dynamics.
5. Made key contributions to the design of statistical methods for analyzing large datasets.
6. Published seminal works in nonlinear analysis and nonlinear optimization.
7. Developed methods to understand complex network structures and behaviors.
8. Developed methods to analyze data from scientific experiments.
9. Authored over 100 articles in refereed journals on mathematical and physical topics.
10. Developed a diverse range of mathematical models to better understand dynamical systems.

Born 13 June 1883, West Loch Tarbert, Argyll, Scotland. Died 28 December 1951, Glasgow, Scotland.
