**Samuel Molyneux** was an English astronomer who produced some new telescope designs.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Established a school in Dublin devoted to teaching mathematics and astronomy.
2. Published the first Irish treatise on mathematics, Algebra, in 1674.
3. Introduced French mathematical terms such as sine and cosine into the English language.
4. Developed the method of least squares for solving simultaneous linear equations.
5. Discovered a method of correcting the errors found in telescopic observations.
6. Pioneered the science of mapping, creating the first large scale maps of Ireland.
7. Published the Principia Philosophiae Naturalis, which laid the foundation of the Newtonâ€™s theory of gravitation.
8. Introduced the idea of spherical symmetrical bodies as a basis for optics.

Born 18 July 1689, Chester, Cheshire, England. Died 13 April 1728, Kew, Surrey, England.
