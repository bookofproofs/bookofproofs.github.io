 1100|"Jabir al-Ishbili Abu Muhammad ibn Aflah":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/jabir-al-ishbili-abu-muhammad-ibn-aflah Jabir al-Ishbili Abu Muhammad ibn Aflah was born about 1100 in possibly Seville, Spain. Jabir ibn Aflah or Geber was an Spanish Islamic mathematician whose works were translated into Latin and so became available to European mathematicians. Jabir al-Ishbili Abu Muhammad ibn Aflah died about 1160./||
 1114|"Bhaskara":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/bhaskara Bhaskara was born 1114 in Vijayapura, India. Bhaskara II or Bhaskaracharya was an Indian mathematician and astronomer who extended Brahmagupta's work on number systems. Bhaskara died in 1185 in Ujjain, India./||
 1114|"Gherard of Cremona":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/gherard-of-cremona Gherard of Cremona was born 1114 in Cremona, Italy. Gherard of Cremona was an Italian mathematician who worked in Spain and translated mathematical works from Arabic into Latin. Gherard of Cremona died in 1187 in Toledo, Spain./||
 1130|"Ibn Yahya al-Maghribi Al-Samawal":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/ibn-yahya-al-maghribi-al-samawal Ibn Yahya al-Maghribi Al-Samawal was born about 1130 in Baghdad, Iraq. Al-Samawal was an Islamic mathematician who was able to extend the arithmetic operations to handle polynomials. He used an early form of induction. Ibn Yahya al-Maghribi Al-Samawal died about 1180 in Maragha, Iran./||
 1135|"Sharaf al-Din al-Muzaffar al-Tusi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/sharaf-al-din-al-muzaffar-al-tusi Sharaf al-Din al-Muzaffar al-Tusi was born about 1135 in Tus, Khorasan (now Iran). Sharaf al-Tusi was an Islamic mathematician who wrote a treatise on cubic equations. Sharaf al-Din al-Muzaffar al-Tusi died in 1213 in Iran./||
 1140|"Reinher of Paderborn":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/reinher-of-paderborn Reinher of Paderborn was born about 1140 in Paderborn, Roman Empire, now Germany. Reinher of Paderborn was a German mathematician who worked on the mathematics of the calendar and in particular on the calculation of Easter. Reinher of Paderborn died about 1190 in Paderborn, Roman Empire, now Germany./||
 1168|"Robert Grosseteste":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/robert-grosseteste Robert Grosseteste was born 1168 in Suffolk, England. Robert Grosseteste was an English bishop who worked on geometry, optics and astronomy and made Latin translations of many Greek and Arabic scientific writings. Robert Grosseteste died on 9 October 1253 in Buckden, Buckinghamshire, England./||
 1170|"Leonardo Pisano Fibonacci":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/leonardo-pisano-fibonacci Leonardo Pisano Fibonacci was born 1170 in (probably) Pisa (now in Italy). Leonard of Pisa or Fibonacci played an important role in reviving ancient mathematics and made significant contributions of his own. Liber abaci introduced the Hindu-Arabic place-valued decimal system and the use of Arabic numerals into Europe. Leonardo Pisano Fibonacci died in 1250 in (possibly) Pisa (now in Italy)./||
 1175|"Michael Scot":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/michael-scot Michael Scot was born about 1175 in (probably) Northern England/Southern Scotland. Michael Scot (or Scotus) helped in the transmission of Islamic mathematics from Spain to the rest of Europe. Michael Scot died about 1235 in (possibly) Northern England/Southern Scotland./||
 1192|"Li Zhi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/li-zhi Li Zhi was born 1192 in Ta-hsing (now Peking), China. Li Zhi was a Chinese mathematician who described methods for solving equations. Li Zhi died in 1279 in Hopeh province, China./||
 1195|"Johannes de Sacrobosco":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/johannes-de-sacrobosco Johannes de Sacrobosco was born about 1195 in (possibly) Halifax, Yorkshire, England. John of Holywood or Johannes de Sacrobosco was an English scholar who wrote an important text on astronomy and promoted Arabic methods of arithmetic and algebra. Johannes de Sacrobosco died in 1256 in Paris, France./||
