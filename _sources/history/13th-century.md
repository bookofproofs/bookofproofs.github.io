 1200|"Saint Albertus Magnus":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/saint-albertus-magnus Saint Albertus Magnus was born about 1200 in Lauingen an der Donau, Swabia (now Germany). Albert (or Albertus Magnus) was a German Dominican who wrote a commentary on Euclid's Elements. He was made a Saint in 1931 and, in 1941, was made patron of natural scientists. Saint Albertus Magnus died on 15 November 1280 in Cologne, Prussia (now Germany)./||
 1201|"Nasir al-Din al-Tusi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/nasir-al-din-al-tusi Nasir al-Din al-Tusi was born on 18 February 1201 in Tus, Khorasan (now Iran). Nasir al-Tusi was an Islamic astronomer and mathematician who joined the Mongols who conquered Baghdad. He made important contributions to astronomy and wrote many commentaries on Greek texts. Nasir al-Din al-Tusi died on 26 June 1274 in Kadhimain (near Baghdad now in Iraq)./||
 1202|"Qin Jiushao":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/qin-jiushao Qin Jiushao was born 1202 in Puzhou (Anyue), Szechwan province, China. Qin Jiushao was a Chinese mathematician who wrote an important study Shushu Jiuzang on equations, including the [Chinese Remainder Theorem][bookofproofs$8182]. Qin Jiushao died in 1261 in Meizhou (now Meixian), Guangtong province, China./|[Chinese Remainder Theorem][bookofproofs$8182]|Chinese
 1214|"Roger Bacon":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/roger-bacon Roger Bacon was born 1214 in Ilchester, Somerset, England. Roger Bacon was an English mathematician. His most important mathematical contribution is the application of geometry to optics. Roger Bacon died on June 1292 in Oxford, England./||
 1220|"Muhyi l'din al-Maghribi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/muhyi-l'din-al-maghribi/||
 1220|"Campanus of Novara":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/campanus-of-novara Campanus of Novara was born 1220 in Novara, Italy. Johannes Campanus was an Italian mathematician who published a Latin edition of Euclid's Elements. He also wrote on astronomy. Campanus of Novara died on 13 September 1296 in Viterbo, Italy./||
 1231|"Guo Shoujing":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/guo-shoujing Guo Shoujing was born 1231 in Xingtai, Hebei province, China. Guo Shoujing was a Chinese astronomer who worked on spherical trigonometry and the calendar. Guo Shoujing died in 1316 in China./||
 1235|"Ramon Llull":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/ramon-llull Ramon Llull was born 1235 in Majorca, Spain. Ramon Llull was a Majorcan philosopher whose logical methods influenced Leibniz. Ramon Llull died in 1316 in (probably) Tunis, Tunisia./||
 1236|"Jacob ben Machir ibn Tibbon":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/jacob-ben-machir-ibn-tibbon Jacob ben Machir ibn Tibbon was born 1236 in Marseilles, Spain (now France). Jacob ben Tibbon was a French Jewish medical doctor who translated Arabic versions of Greek mathematics into Hebrew. Jacob ben Machir ibn Tibbon died in 1305 in Montpellier, France./||
 1238|"Yang Hui":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/yang-hui Yang Hui was born about 1238 in Qiantang (now Hangzhou), Chekiang province, China. Yang Hui was a Chinese mathematician who wrote several outstanding mathematical texts. These contained solutions of quadratic equations as well as Pascal's triangle and magic squares. Yang Hui died about 1298 in China./||
 1250|"Shams al-Din ibn Ashraf Al-Samarqandi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/shams-al-din-ibn-ashraf-al-samarqandi Shams al-Din ibn Ashraf Al-Samarqandi was born about 1250 in Samarqand, Uzbekistan. Shams al-Samarqandi was an Islamic mathematician who wrote works on theology, logic, philosophy, mathematics and astronomy. Shams al-Din ibn Ashraf Al-Samarqandi died about 1310./||
 1257|"al-Marrakushi ibn Al-Banna":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/al-marrakushi-ibn-al-banna al-Marrakushi ibn Al-Banna was born on 29 December 1256 in Marrakesh, Morocco. Ibn al-Banna was an Islamic mathematician who wrote a large number of works including an introduction to Euclid's Elements, an algebra text and various works on astronomy. al-Marrakushi ibn Al-Banna died in 1321 in Marrakesh, Morocco./||
 1260|"Kamal al-Din Abu'l Hasan Muhammad Al-Farisi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/kamal-al-din-abu'l-hasan-muhammad-al-farisi/||
 1260|"Zhu Shijie":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/zhu-shijie Zhu Shijie was born about 1260 in Yan-shan, near Peking, China. Zhu Shijie or Chu Shih-Chieh was a Chinese mathematician who developed methods for handling simultaneous equations. Zhu Shijie died about 1320 in Not known./||
 1271|"Zhao Youqin":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/zhao-youqin Zhao Youqin was born on 26 July 1271 in Poyang, now Jiangxi province, China. Zhao Youqin was a Chinese mathematician and astronomer who published work on the structure of the universe. He described how to calculate $\pi$. Zhao Youqin died about 1335 in Longyou Mountains, Zhejiang province, China./||
 1288|"Rabbi Levi Ben Gerson":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/rabbi-levi-ben-gerson Rabbi Levi Ben Gerson was born 1288 in Bagnols now Bagnols sur Cèze, Provence, France. Rabbi Levi Ben Gerson, also known as Gersonides, was a French scholar who wrote on trigonometry as well as astronomy and philosophy. Rabbi Levi Ben Gerson died on 20 April 1344 in Avignon, France./||
 1288|"William of Ockham":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/william-of-ockham William of Ockham was born about 1288 in Ockham (near Ripley, Surrey), England. William of Ockham was an English mathematician and philosopher best known for Ockham's razor, one version of which is: It is vain to do with more what can be done with less. William of Ockham died on 9 April 1348 in Munich, Bavaria (now Germany)./||
 1295|"Thomas Bradwardine":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/thomas-bradwardine Thomas Bradwardine was born about 1295 in Chichester, England. Thomas Bradwardine was an English mathematician and theologian who examined Aristotle's theories of dynamics. Thomas Bradwardine died on 26 August 1349 in Lambeth, London, England./||
