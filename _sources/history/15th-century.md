 1400|"Abu Abdallah Yaish ibn Ibrahim Al-Umawi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu-abdallah-yaish-ibn-ibrahim-al-umawi Abu Abdallah Yaish ibn Ibrahim Al-Umawi was born about 1400 in possibly Andalusia, Spain. Al-Umawi was a Spanish-born Islamic mathematician who wrote works on mensuration and arithmetic. Abu Abdallah Yaish ibn Ibrahim Al-Umawi died in 1489 in possibly Damascus, Syria./||
 1401|"Nicholas of Cusa":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/nicholas-of-cusa Nicholas of Cusa was born 1401 in Kues, Trier (now Germany). Nicholas of Cusa was a German philosopher and bishop. He was interested in geometry and logic as well as in philosophy and astronomy. Nicholas of Cusa died on 11 August 1464 in Todi, Papal States (now Italy)./||
 1404|"Leone Battista Alberti":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/leone-battista-alberti Leone Battista Alberti was born on 18 February 1404 in Genoa, French Empire (now Italy). Leone Alberti was an Italian mathematician who wrote the first general treatise on the laws of perspective and also wrote a book on cryptography containing the first example of a frequency table. Leone Battista Alberti died on 3 April 1472 in Rome, Papal States (now Italy)./||
 1412|"Abu'l Hasan ibn Ali al Qalasadi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/abu'l-hasan-ibn-ali-al-qalasadi/||
 1420|"Piero della Francesca":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/piero-della-francesca Piero della Francesca was born on June 1420 in Borgo San Sepolcro (now Sansepolcro), Italy. Piero della Francesca was an Italian artist who pioneered the use of perspective in Renaissance art and went on to write several mathematical treatises. Piero della Francesca died on 12 October 1492 in Borgo San Sepolcro (now Sansepolcro), Italy./||
 1423|"Georg Peurbach":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/georg-peurbach Georg Peurbach was born on 30 May 1423 in Peuerbach, Austria. Georg Peurbach was an Austrian astronomer who published observations as well as a textbook on trigonometric calculation. Georg Peurbach died on 8 April 1461 in Vienna, Austria./||
 1424|"Piero Borgi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/piero-borgi Piero Borgi was born about 1424 in Venice, Venetian States (now Italy). Piero Borgi was the author of the best known 15th Century Italian arithmetic books. Piero Borgi died about 1494 in Italy./||
 1436|"Johann Müller Regiomontanus":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/johann-mueller-regiomontanus Johann Müller Regiomontanus was born on 6 June 1436 in Unfinden (near Königsberg), Lower Franconia (now in Bayern, Germany). Regiomontanus or Johann Müller was a German scholar who made important contributions to trigonometry and astronomy. Johann Müller Regiomontanus died on 6 July 1476 in Rome, Italy./||
 1444|"Nilakantha Somayaji":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/nilakantha-somayaji Nilakantha Somayaji was born on 14 June 1444 in Trkkantiyur (near Tirur), Kerala, India. Nilakantha was a mathematician and astronomer from South India who wrote texts on both astronomy and infinite series. Nilakantha Somayaji died in 1544 in India./||
 1445|"Nicolas Chuquet":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/nicolas-chuquet Nicolas Chuquet was born 1445. Nicolas Chuquet was a French mathematician who wrote the earliest French algebra book./||
 1445|"Luca Pacioli":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/luca-pacioli Luca Pacioli was born 1445 in Sansepolcro, Italy. Luca Pacioli was an Italian mathematician who published the influential book Suma in 1494 giving a summary of all the mathematics known at that time. Luca Pacioli died in 1517 in Sansepolcro, Italy./||
 1452|"Leonardo da Vinci":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/leonardo-da-vinci Leonardo da Vinci was born on 15 April 1452 in Vinci (near Empolia), Italy. Leonardo da Vinci was an Italian artist and scholar who had many talents in addition to his painting. He worked on mechanics, though geometry was his main love. He was involved in hydrodynamics, anatomy, mechanics, mathematics and optics. Leonardo da Vinci died on 2 May 1519 in Cloux, Amboise, France./||
 1462|"Johannes Widman":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/johannes-widman Johannes Widman was born 1462 in Eger, Bohemia (now Cheb, Czech Republic). Johannes Widman was a German mathematician who is best remembered for an early arithmetic book which contains the first appearance of + and - signs. Johannes Widman died in 1498 in Leipzig, Germany./||
 1465|"Scipione del Ferro":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/scipione-del-ferro Scipione del Ferro was born on 6 February 1465 in Bologna (now Italy). Scipione del Ferro was an Italian mathematician who is famous for being the first to find a formula to solve a cubic equation. Scipione del Ferro died on 5 November 1526 in Bologna, Papal States (now Italy)./||
 1468|"Johann Werner":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/johann-werner Johann Werner was born on 14 February 1468 in Nuremberg, Germany. Johann Werner was a German mathematician whose main work was on astronomy, mathematics and geography. Johann Werner died on May 1522 in Nuremberg, Germany./||
 1469|"John Maior":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/john-maior John Maior was born 1469 in Gleghornie (near Haddington), Scotland. John Maior was a Scottish mathematician who worked in Paris and St Andrews where he taught logic and theology. John Maior died in 1550 in St Andrews, Fife, Scotland./||
 1470|"Estienne de La Roche":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/estienne-de-la-roche Estienne de La Roche was born about 1470 in Lyon, France. Estienne de la Roche as a French mathematician who published an arithmetic book with good notation for powers and roots. Estienne de La Roche died about 1530 in France./||
 1471|"Charles de Bouvelles":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/charles-de-bouvelles Charles de Bouvelles was born 1471 in Soyecourt, near Amiens, Picardy, France. Charles de Bouvelles was a French priest who wrote works on geometry and philosophy. Charles de Bouvelles died in 1553 in Noyon, Picardy, France./||
 1471|"Albrecht Dürer":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/albrecht-duerer Albrecht Dürer was born on 21 May 1471. Dürer was a German artist who is known for his work as an engraver. The foundations of descriptive geometry are laid in Dürer's treatise on human proportions published in Nuremberg after his death in 1528./||
 1473|"Nicolaus Copernicus":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/nicolaus-copernicus Nicolaus Copernicus was born on 19 February 1473 in Toruń, Poland. Copernicus was a Polish astronomer and mathematician who was a proponent of the view of an Earth in daily motion about its axis and in yearly motion around a stationary sun. This theory profoundly altered later workers' view of the universe, but was rejected by the Catholic church. Nicolaus Copernicus died on 24 May 1543 in Frauenburg (now Frombork), Poland./||
 1474|"Cuthbert Tunstall":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/cuthbert-tunstall Cuthbert Tunstall was born 1474 in Hackforth, Yorkshire, England. Cuthbert Tunstall wrote the first printed work published in England devoted exclusively to mathematics. Cuthbert Tunstall died on 18 December 1559 in Lambeth, London, England./||
 1480|"Juan de Ortega":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/juan-de-ortega Juan de Ortega was born 1480 in Palencia, Spain. Juan de Ortega was a Spanish mathematician who devised an original method of extracting square roots. Juan de Ortega died in 1568./||
 1487|"Gaspar Lax":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/gaspar-lax Gaspar Lax was born 1487 in Sarinena, Aragon, Spain. Gaspar Lax was a Spanish scholar who published several mathematics books based on the work of earlier mathematicians. Gaspar Lax died on 23 February 1560 in Zaragoza, Spain./||
 1487|"Michael Stifel":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/michael-stifel Michael Stifel was born 1487 in Esslingen, Germany. Michael Stifel was a German mathematician who invented logarithms independently of Napier using a totally different approach. Michael Stifel died on 19 April 1567 in Jena, Germany./||
 1493|"Adam Ries":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/adam-ries Adam Ries was born on 23 December 1492 in Staffelstein (near Bamberg), Upper Franconia (now Germany). Adam Ries was a German mathematician who published one of the first widely available arithmetic texts. Adam Ries died on 30 March 1559 in Annaberg, Saxony (now Annaberg-Buchholz, Germany)./||
 1494|"Oronce Fine":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/oronce-fine Oronce Fine was born on 20 December 1494 in Briançon, France. Oronce Fine was a French mathematician who published a major work on mathematics and astronomy. Oronce Fine died on 8 August 1555 in Paris, France./||
 1494|"Francesco Maurolico":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/francesco-maurolico Francesco Maurolico was born on 16 September 1494 in Messina, Kingdom of Sicily (now Italy). Francisco Maurolico was an Italian Benedictine who wrote important books on Greek mathematics. He also worked on geometry, the theory of numbers, optics, conics and mechanics. Francesco Maurolico died on 22 July 1575 in Messina, Kingdom of Sicily (now Italy)./||
 1495|"Petrus Apianus":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/petrus-apianus Petrus Apianus was born on 16 April 1495 in Leisnig, Saxony (now Germany). Petrus Apianus was a German mathematician who published important popular works on astronomy and geography. Petrus Apianus died on 21 April 1552 in Ingolstadt, Bavaria, Germany./||
 1499|"Christoff Rudolff":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/christoff-rudolff Christoff Rudolff was born 1499 in Jauer, Silesia (now Jawor, Poland). Christoff Rudolff was a German mathematician whose book Coss is the first German algebra book. Christoff Rudolff died in 1543 in Vienna, Austria./||
