One of the most important Greek philosophers. He founded the Academy in Athens, an institution devoted to research and instruction in philosophy and the sciences. His works on philosophy, politics and mathematics were very influential and laid the foundations for Euclid's systematic approach to mathematics.  

Main contributions are in philosophy, mathematics, and science.  

Plato's name is attached to the Platonic solids representing the "elements" i.e. cube (=earth), tetrahedron (=fire), octahedron (=air), icosahedron (=water).  

Plato associated the dodecahedron with the whole universe.

Born 427 BC Athens, Greece. Died 347 BC Athens, Greece.
