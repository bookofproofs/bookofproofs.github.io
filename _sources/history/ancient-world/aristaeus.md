**Aristaeus the Elder** was a Greek mathematician who worked on conic sections.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
Aristaeus was a mathematician and scientist of ancient Greece. He is credited with discovering many important mathematical theorems, most notably the theory of proportionality, which is fundamental to mathematics. He also proposed a canonical form of equations and the use of mathematical induction to prove theorems.
Key Achievements of Aristaeus related to mathematics: 
1. Discovery of the theory of proportionality 
2. Proposal of a canonical form of equations 
3. Use of mathematical induction to prove theorems

Born about 370BC, Greece. Died about 300 BC.
