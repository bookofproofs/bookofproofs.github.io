**Hippias Of Elis** was a Greek contemporary of Socrates whose only contribution to mathematics seems to be the quadratrix - a curve he may have used for squaring the circle and trisecting angles.  

Probably, the inventor of "quadratrix" which may have been used by him for trisecting an angle and squaring the circle.

Born about 460 BC Elis, Peloponnese, Greece. Died about 400 BC.
