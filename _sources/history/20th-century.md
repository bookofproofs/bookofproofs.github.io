## Chronology
### 1900 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Plessner/">Abraham Ezechiel Plessner</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Ingham/">Albert Ingham</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Zygmund/">Antoni Szczepan Zygmund</a>
: Zygmund's work in harmonic analysis has application in the theory of waves and vibrations. He also did major work in Fourier analysis and its application to partial differential equations.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Payne-Gaposchkin/">Cecilia Payne-Gaposchkin</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Tanner/">Cecily Tanner</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Dantzig/">David van Dantzig</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Collingwood/">Edward Foyle Collingwood</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Bath/">Frederick Bath</a>
: Frederick Bath graduated from Bristol and Cambridge and held posts at King's College London, University College Dundee, St Andrews and Edinburgh. He worked in Geometry.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Uhlenbeck/">George Eugene Uhlenbeck</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Cox/">Gertrude Mary Cox</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Vranceanu/">Gheorghe Vrănceanu</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Curry/">Haskell Brooks Curry</a>
: Haskell Curry worked in mathematical logic with particular interest in the theory of formal systems and processes.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Kloosterman/">Hendrik Douwe Kloosterman</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Aiken/">Howard Hathaway Aiken</a>
: A pioneer designer of early computers.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Rhodes/">Ida Rhodes</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Burkill/">John Charles Burkill</a>
: An English mathematician who worked on analysis and introduced the Burkill integral. He is best-known for his analysis textbooks.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Redei/">László Rédei</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Cartwright/">Dame Mary Lucy Cartwright</a>
: The first woman mathematician to be elected to the Royal Society. She became Mistress of Girton College, Cambridge.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Lavrentev/">Mikhail Alekseevich Lavrentev</a>
: Mikhail Lavrent'ev is remembered for an outstanding book on conformal mappings and he made many important contributions to that topic.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Puig_Adam/">Pedro Puig Adam</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Cauer/">Wilhelm Cauer</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Youden/">William John Youden</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Pauli/">Wolfgang Ernst Pauli</a>
: Wolfgang Pauli won a Nobel prize for his work on quantum mechanics.

### 1901 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Tarski/">Alfred Tarski</a>
: Tarski made important contributions in many areas of mathematics, including metamathematics, set theory, measure theory, model theory, and general algebra.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Caraca/">Bento Caraça</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Zeckendorf/">Édouard Zeckendorf</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Copson/">Edward Thomas Copson</a>
: An expert on Complex Analysis and was Regius Professor of Mathematics at St Andrews from 1950 until 1969.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Fermi/">Enrico Fermi</a>
: An Italian physicist who created the world's first nuclear reactor. He made important contributions to the development of quantum theory, nuclear and particle physics and to statistical mechanics.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Schmidt_F-K/">Friedrich Karl Schmidt</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Temple/">George Frederick James Temple</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Petrovsky/">Ivan Georgievich Petrovsky</a>
: A Russian mathematician who worked in the field of partial differential equations.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Williamson/">John Williamson</a>
: John Williamson graduated from Edinburgh University and then had an appointment at St Andrews. He spent some time in Chicago and eventually moved to the USA to a post at Johns Hopkins University in Baltimore. His most important work was in Algebra.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Oka/">Kiyoshi Oka</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Friedrichs/">Kurt Otto Friedrichs</a>
: A German American mathematician who worked on many aspects of partial differential equations. He was the co-founder of the Courant Institute.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Gokieli/">Levan Gokieli</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Fantappie/">Luigi Fantappiè</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Euwe/">Machgielis Euwe</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Simpson_Mary/">Mary Simpson</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Akhiezer/">Naum Il'ich Akhiezer</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Kochin/">Nikolai Evgrafovich Kochin</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Bari/">Nina Karlovna Bari</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Bottema/">Oene Bottema</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Schreier/">Otto Schreier</a>
: Schreier worked in combinatorial group theory, particularly on subgroups of free groups and on knot groups.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Novikov/">Petr Sergeevich Novikov</a>
: Petr Sergeevich Novikov is a Russian mathematician known for his work on combinatorial problems in group theory including the word problem for groups and the Burnside problem.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Bose_Raj/">Raj Chandra Bose</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Brauer/">Richard Dagobert Brauer</a>
: Brauer became interested in work on group characters. He formulated a method to classify all finite simple groups and spent the rest of his life working on this problem.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Gwilt/">Richard Lloyd Gwilt</a>
: An actuary who worked for various Edinburgh insurance companies. He was a Fellow of the Faculty of Actuaries and of the Institute of Actuaries.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Robb/">Richard Alexander Robb</a>
: Richard Robb studied at Glasgow and Michigan universities. Most of his career was spent at Glasgow University lecturing on Mathematics and Statistics.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Gershgorin/">Semyon Aranovich Gershgorin</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Vajda/">Steven Vajda</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Morin_Ugo/">Ugo Morin</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Heisenberg/">Werner Karl Heisenberg</a>
: Werner Heisenberg did important work in Quantum Mechanics as well as nuclear physics.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Whyburn_William/">William Whyburn</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Deans/">Winifred Margaret Deans</a>
: Winifred Deans graduated from Aberdeen and Cambridge. After a period in teaching she joined a Scottish publishing company and translated many important German scientific texts for them. After World War II she worked at the Commonwealth Bureau of Animal Nutrition in Aberdeen.

### 1902 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Wald/">Abraham Wald</a>
: A mathematician born in what is now Romania who worked on decision theory, geometry and econometrics.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Eisele/">Carolyn Eisele</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Wigner/">Eugene Paul Wigner</a>
: A Hungarian-American theoretical physicist and mathematician who won a Nobel prize for his contributions to the theory of the atomic nucleus and elementary particles.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Yates/">Frank Yates</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Frewin/">Gilbert Leslie Frewin</a>
: Leslie Frewin graduated from the University of Edinburgh and taught at George Watson's College before moving to Inverness.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Calugareanu/">Gheorghe  Calugăreănu</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Petersson/">Hans Petersson</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Schwerdtfeger/">Hans Wilhelm Eduard Schwerdtfeger</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Pierce_Joseph/">Joseph Pierce</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Karamata/">Jovan Karamata</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Menger/">Karl Menger</a>
: An Austrian-American mathematician who worked on algebras, geometries, curve and dimension theory. He also contributed to game theory and social sciences.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Zarankiewicz/">Zarankiewicz, Kazimierz</a>
: A Polish mathematician who worked in topology.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Shoda/">Kenjiro Shoda</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Jordan_Pascual/">Pascual Jordan</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Dirac/">Paul Adrien Maurice Dirac</a>
: Dirac is famous as the creator of the complete theoretical formulation of quantum mechanics.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Baer/">Reinhold Baer</a>
: Wide ranging; topology, abelian groups and geometry. His most important work, however, was in group theory, on the extension problem for groups, finiteness conditions, soluble and nilpotent groups.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/McDaniel/">Reuben R McDaniel</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Golab/">Stanislaw Golab</a>

### 1903 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Broadbent/">Thomas Arthur Alan Broadbent</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Oppenheim/">Alexander Victor Oppenheim</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Church/">Alonzo Church</a>
: Alonzo Church made important contributions to mathematical logic and theoretical computer science.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Kolmogorov/">Andrey Nikolaevich Kolmogorov</a>
: One of the developers of probability theory. He later used this work to study the motion of the planets and the turbulent flow of air from a jet engine.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Wintner/">Aurel Friedrich Wintner</a>
: Aurel Wintner studied at Budapest and Leipzig. He spent most of his career in Johns Hopkins University in Baltimore, USA. He published on Number Theory, Differential Equations, Probability and Celestial Mechanics.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Van_der_Waerden/">Bartel Leendert van der Waerden</a>
: A Dutch mathematician best known for his "Modern Algebra" text-book. He also worked on topology and on the history of mathematics.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Segre_Beniamino/">Beniamino Segre</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Jeffreys_Bertha/">Bertha Swirles Jeffreys</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Rajagopal/">Cadambathur Tiruvenkatacharlu Rajagopal</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Steele/">Catherine Cassels Steele</a>
: Catherine Steele graduated from the University of St Andrews. She went on to a doctorate in Chemistry and studied at the University of Illinois and at Harvard. She taught for some time at a Horticultural College in Kent before returning to the USA.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Littlewood_Dudley/">Dudley Littlewood</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Walton/">Ernest Walton</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Williams_Evan/">Evan James Williams</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Ramsey/">Frank Plumpton Ramsey</a>
: An English mathematician best known for his so-called Ramsey theory in combinatorics.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Timms/">Geoffrey Timms</a>
: Geoffrey Timms studied at Leeds and Cambridge and then took up a post at St Andrews. During World War II he served at Bletchley Park and Cheltenham and joined the Foreign Office afterwards.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/De_Rham/">Georges de Rham</a>
: A Swiss mathematician, known for his contributions to differential topology and to algebraic topology.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Mackenzie/">Gladys Isabel Mackenzie</a>
: Gladys Mackenzie graduated from the University of Edinburgh and became an assistant in the Natural Philosophy department. She moved to Newnham College Cambridge and late to Bristol University and Queen Elizabeth College London. She published papers on X-ray spectroscopy.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Rohrbach/">Hans Rohrbach</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Robertson/">Howard Percy Robertson</a>
: Howard Percy Robertson studied at Washington University and California Institute of Technology. He spent some time in Germany and was appointed to a post in Princeton. He spent much of the period of World War II in Britain. He made important contributions to Differential Geometry and Relativity.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Flugge-Lotz/">Irmgard Flügge-Lotz</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Schoenberg/">Isaac Jacob Schoenberg</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Paton/">James Paton</a>
: James Paton graduated from Edinburgh and spent most of his career at the University finishing as head of the Department of Meteorology.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Tinbergen/">Jan Tinbergen</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Delsarte/">Jean Frédéric Auguste Delsarte</a>
: A French mathematician known for his work in mathematical analysis.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Von_Neumann/">John von Neumann</a>
: Von Neumann built a solid framework for quantum mechanics. He also worked in game theory, studied what are now called von Neumann Algebras, and was one of the pioneers of computer science.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Mahler/">Kurt Mahler</a>
: A German mathematician who made important contributions to the theory of transcendental numbers.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Asgeirsson/">Leifur Ásgeirsson</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Stone/">Marshall Harvey Stone</a>
: Stone is best known for the Stone-Weierstrass theorem on uniform approximation of continuous functions by polynomials.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Sutton/">Oliver Sutton</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Du_Val/">Patrick Du Val</a>
: An English mathematician who worked in algebraic geometry, differential geometry and general relativity.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Gillespie/">Robert Pollock Gillespie</a>
: Robert Gillespie studied at Glasgow and Cambridge and then lectured at Glasgow University. His main interests were in analysis.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Bosanquet/">Lancelot Stephen Bosanquet</a>
: Stephen's-by-Saltash, Cornwall, England.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Goldstein/">Sydney Goldstein</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Morishima/">Taro Morishima</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Kupradze/">Viktor Kupradze</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Birnbaum/">Zygmunt William Birnbaum</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Hodge/">William Vallance Douglas Hodge</a>
: Lowndean Professor of Astronomy and Geometry at Cambridge. His main interests were in Algebraic Geometry and Differential Geometry.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Prager/">William Prager</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Orlicz/">Wladyslaw Orlicz</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Rocard/">Yves-André Rocard</a>

### 1905 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Albert_Abraham/">A Adrian Albert</a>
