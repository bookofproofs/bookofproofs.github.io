**Mark Krasnoselsky** was a Ukrainian mathematician who worked in nonlinear functional analysis and its applications.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Received the Lenin Prize for his contributions to mathematics in 1966. 
2. Recipient of the Honorary Order of St. Vladimir in 1981. 
3. Appointed as a professor of mathematics in the Lvov Polytechnic Institute in 1952. 
4. Established the theory of nonlinear operators in the mid-1950s. 
5. Received the Lomonosov Gold Medal in 1975. 
6. Introduced the two-level optimization algorithm in 1965. 
7. Co-authored over 130 works related to mathematics. 
8. Developed the principles of hereditability and stability in mathematics. 
9. Elected a member of the National Academy of Sciences of the USSR in 1962. 
10. Received the Order of the Red Banner of Labor in 1981.

Born 27 April 1920, Starokonstantinov, Ukraine. Died 13 February 1997, Moscow, Russia.
