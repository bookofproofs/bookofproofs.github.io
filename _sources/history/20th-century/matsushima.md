**Yozo Matsushima** was a Japanese mathematician who worked in Lie groups and algebras.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Discovered the Ring Theorem in matrix theory and published a paper on it in 1947.
2. Won the Japan Academy Prize for this discovery in 1949.
3. Formulated Matsushima's Theorem, which is a fundamental result in algebraic invariant theory.
4. Developed the Matsushima–Wolmer theorem in linear mathematics.
5. Pioneered the use of abstract algebra in the study of mathematical models.
6. Developed the Yamabe-Matsushima theorems in continuous transformation group theory.
7. Established a generalization of the classical Cohn-Vossen theorems with differentiable manifolds.
8. Contributed to the theory of linear operator algebras and operator algebras in particular.
9. Published a monograph on the subject of tensor analysis and its application to partial differential equations.
10.Was an invited speaker at the International Congress of Mathematicians in 1966.

Born 11 February 1921, Sakai, Osaka Prefecture, Japan. Died 9 April 1983, Osaka, Japan.
