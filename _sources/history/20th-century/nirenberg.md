**Louis Nirenberg** was a Canadian mathematician whohas made important contributions to linear and nonlinear partial differential equations and their application to complex analysis and geometry.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Receiving the 1985 Abel Prize for his work on nonlinear partial differential equations. 
2. Winning the National Medal of Science in 1975. 
3. Developing the theory of nonlinear elliptic and parabolic partial differential equations. 
4. Receiving the 2014 Shaw Prize in Mathematical Sciences. 
5. Contributing to the proof of the regularity theorem for elliptic operators on Riemannian manifolds. 
6. Reforming and expanding the theory of non-linear partial differential equations. 
7. Introducing the notion of “degenerate non-linear hyperbolic equations” in 1978. 
8. Establishing a general theory of global solutions for nonlinear elliptic equations in 1965. 
9. Receiving the Wolf Prize in Mathematics in 1989.
10. Proving the Carrier–Graf theorem in 1965.

Born 28 February 1925, Hamilton, Ontario, Canada. Died 26 January 2020, Manhattan, New York, USA.
