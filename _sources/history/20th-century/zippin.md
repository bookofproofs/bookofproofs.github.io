**Leo Zippin** was an American mathematician who is best known for being one of those who solved Hilbert's Fifth Problem.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the first computer program to solve linear programming problems in 1948.
2. Developed the first computer program to optimize discrete programming problems in 1951.
3. Published mathematical research papers in linear and nonlinear programming, probability, and combinatorial optimization.
4. Founded the Mathematical Programming Society in 1973.
5. Helped to establish the first doctoral program in operations research.
6. Led research teams at USC and Cornell University in developing mathematical programming models and techniques for solving a variety of combinatorial optimization problems.
7. Received numerous awards including the National Medal of Science for his contributions to operations research.

Born 25 January 1905, New York City, New York, USA. Died 11 May 1995, Manhattan, New York, USA.
