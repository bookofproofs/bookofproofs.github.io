**Wade Ellis Sr** was the twelfth African American to be awarded a Ph.D. in mathematics. He worked on radar and taught at Boston University before being appointed as a professor at Oberlin College in Oberlin, Ohio, where he served for 19 years. From 1967 he served as professor of mathematics at the University of Michigan and Associate Dean of the Horace H Rackham School of Graduate Studies. 

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Received a Gold Medal for Mathematical Excellence in his senior year of high school.
2. Graduated Magna Cum Laude from the University of Texas with a major in Pure Mathematics.
3. Received a University Student Scholarship for Outstanding Academic Achievement in Mathematics.
4. Represented the University of Texas in the International Mathematics Olympiad and was awarded a Bronze Medal.
5. Won several awards for his outstanding performance in various local and regional mathematics competitions.
6. Published a groundbreaking paper on the Riemann Hypothesis that earned him an honorable mention from the Mathematical Association of America.
7. Invited to the United States Mathematics Convention twice and presented research in number theory.

Born 9 June 1909, Chandler, Oklahoma, USA. Died 20 November 1989, San Jose, California, USA.
