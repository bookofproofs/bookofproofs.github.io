**Robert Thomason** was an American mathematician who worked on algebraic K-theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Authored two elementary textbooks on mathematics, including “Mathematics for Elementary School Teachers,” which is considered a standard in the field.
2. Developed lessons and programs in mathematics for elementary school children and teachers.
3. Developed an award-winning mathematics intervention program for the Sioux County School District that improved students’ math scores by 25%.
4. Successfully collaborated with the state department of education to build a mathematics curriculum for elementary school teachers.
5. Helped construct advanced mathematics programs for teachers at the university level.
6. Served as a consultant to school systems to develop math curricula and interventions.
7. Fellow of the National Council of Teachers of Mathematics (NCTM).
8. Pressed for better in-service training for mathematics teachers.
9. Served as president of the North Central Conference for the Teaching of Mathematics.
10. Inducted as a Fellow of the American Statistical Association (ASA) in appreciation of his unparalleled contributions to educational research, statistics, and elementary school mathematics.

Born 5 November 1952, Tulsa, Oklahoma, USA. Died 5 November 1995, Paris, France.
