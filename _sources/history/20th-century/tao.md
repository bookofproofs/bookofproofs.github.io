**Terence Tao** won a Fields Medal in 2006 for his contributions to partial differential equations, combinatorics, harmonic analysis and additive number theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Won a Fields Medal in 2006 for his work in partial differential equations, harmonic analysis, additive combinatorics, ergodic Ramsey theory and analytic number theory.
2. In 2012, he became the first mathematician to be awarded the prestigious Breakthrough Prize in Mathematics.
3. He was the first researcher to receive the solving the Poincaré Conjecture and the Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture at the same time.
4. He holds the James and Carol Collins Chair at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) in mathematics.
5. He holds the Leibniz Prize (2002), the Wolf Prize (2008), and was the youngest ever to win the prestigious Fields Medal in August 2006.
6. He was elected to the United States National Academy of Sciences in 2002, the Royal Society of London A in 2012, the Royal Society of Edinburgh B in 2015, the American Philosophical Society in 2017, and the Fields Medal in 2006.
7. He has authored over 300 research articles and scientific reviews, including the influential “Terrence Tao: Solving Mathematical Problems” lecture series.
8. He has worked on a variety of topics including additive combinatorics, analytic number theory, harmonic analysis and partial differential equations.

Born 17 July 1975, Adelaide, South Australia, Australia.
