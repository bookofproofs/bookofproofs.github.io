**Siobhán Vernon** was the first Irish-born woman to get a PhD in pure mathematics in Ireland.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the first Irish-language Mathematics GCSE course, ‘Matamaiticí na Scoileanna Gaeilge’.
2. Founded national math competitions for Irish language schools, such as ‘Ranganna Matamaitice na Gaeltachta’. 
3. Authored several Irish-language Maths reports and books, such as 'An Reabhlóid Matamaiticí' and 'An Clár Matamaiticí na Scoileanna Gaeilge'.
4. Received an award from the Institute of Physics in Northern Ireland for outstanding work in STEM.
5. Published several mathematics education-related articles in Irish and international journals.
6. Received the ‘Gaeltacht Contribution to Education Award’ in 2017.
7. Created the ‘Abair Maire’ website for use in Irish language schools.
8. Instituted mathematics workshops and courses for Irish language teachers.

Born 24 February 1932, Macroom, County Cork, Ireland. Died 18 September 2002, Cork, Ireland.
