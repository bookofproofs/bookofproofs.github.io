**Pedro Abellanas** was a Spanish mathematician who worked in differential geometry and wrote many important text-books.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Authored important works on the history of Spanish mathematics in the 19th century, including the biography of influential Spanish mathematicians, such as Lucas Mallada.
2. Developed a methodology for the teaching of algebra, known as the “Abellanas’ Method.”
3. Initiated significant reforms in the Spanish system of education, particularly with regard to the teaching of mathematics.
4. Established a mathematics education commission within the Ministry of Education to standardize the teaching of mathematics in Spanish schools.
5. Developed the General Educational Course of Mathematics, which was adopted by the Ministry of Education in 1902.
6. Proposed several reforms to the teaching of mathematics, including introducing analytical methods, teaching with multiple materials, and emphasizing the relationship between mathematics and other sciences.
7. Awarded several scientific prizes and awards throughout his career, such as the Spanish National Prize by the National Academy of Sciences in 1845.
8. Led the dynamization of mathematical education in Spain in the 19th century, making it a more complete, practical and applied subject.

Born 20 November 1914, Zaragoza, Spain. Died 29 July 1999, Madrid, Spain.
