**Srinivasa Varadhan** is an Indian mathematician who works in America. He is who is known for his fundamental contributions to probability theory. He won the Abel prize in 2007.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Top prize in the world's most prestigious mathematical award, the Fields Medal in 1987.
2. He was honored with the Abel Prize, the highest award in mathematics in recognition of his fundamental contributions to probability theory in 2009.
3. His seminal work on Hydrodynamic Limits was recognized with the 2006 John Von Neumann Prize.
4. He developed a mathematical formula to explain the propagation of waves in an irregular medium in 1975.
5. He played an important role in the development of New York University's Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences. 
6. Varadhan is best known for his fundamental contributions to the theory of Large Deviations and its numerous applications in science, engineering and finance.
7. He has made major contributions to the theory of Stochastic Processes and Markov Chains. 
8. He received the National Medal of Science from President Barack Obama in 2010 in recognition of his achievements. 
9. Varadhan received the Shaw Prize in Mathematical Sciences in 2011 for his numerous contributions in probability theory, sums of independent random variables, Markov Chain Monte Carlo and other fields. 
10. He was awarded the Prashanthi Kirloskar Prize for Scientific Excellence by the Indian Government for his contributions to mathematics and statistical physics in 2018.

Born 2 January 1940, Madras, (now Chennai) India.
