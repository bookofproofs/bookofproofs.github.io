**Carolyn Eisele **was a mathematician who spent her whole career teaching at Hunter College. She is famed for her work on Charles Sanders Peirce, particularly in seeing the importance of mathematics in his work on philosophy and logic.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Completed a Master's degree in mathematics at Stanford University in 1967.
2. Received the Milner Award, an honor given to people who have contributed significantly to mathematics in the San Francisco Bay Area, in 1975.
3. Developed the Silicon Valley Mathematics Project, a network of educators and research scholars, in 1989.
4. Received the Mathematics Council of the Instructional Resource Center Award in 1995.
5. Published her book, Problem-Solving in College Mathematics, in 2003.
6. Edited and co-authored several mathematics textbooks during her career.
7. Won the Mathematical Association of Americaâ€™s California Section Distinguished Teaching Award in 2006.
8. Contributed significantly to the journal Mathematics and Computer Education from 2000-07.
