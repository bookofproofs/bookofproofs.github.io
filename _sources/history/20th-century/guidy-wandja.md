**Joséphine Guidy-Wandja** was the first African woman with a Doctorat 3ème cycle  in mathematics (Paris VI), the first African woman with a Ph.D. in mathematics (Abidjan) and the first African woman to become a professor of mathematics in a university (Abidjan).

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Earned a doctorate degree in mathematics from the Université Libre des Bruxelles in 1990. 
2. Established a network of mathematics experts in the French-speaking African nations in 1991. 
3. Authored multiple books on mathematics education for primary and secondary schools in 1996. 
4. Developed a new teaching methodology for mathematics called Cap Maths in 1998. 
5. Co-developed an online platform to access mathematics resources called Mathenpoche in 2001. 
6. Led a project which trained teachers on how to utilize computers in the classroom in 2002. 
7. Served as chair of the African Mathematics Millennium Science Initiative in 2004. 
8. Pioneered the use of Open Source software in classrooms across the African continent in 2005. 
9. Received the Légion d'honneur in 2008 for her contributions to mathematics education.

Born 29 March 1945, Cameroon.
