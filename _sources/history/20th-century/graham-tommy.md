**Tommy Graham** was a Scottish mathematician and algebraist who taught at Glasgow University for many years.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Authored the classic textbook, Elements of Plane and Spherical Trigonometry (1849).
2. As an Examiner in Mathematics to the University of London (1839-41), he conducted over 6,000 examinations for students in England, Ireland and Scotland.
3. Developed the theory of finite differences and introduced it to Paris in 1860.
4. Published papers on the Theory of the Residues of Substances (1863) and the Calculus of Residues (1866).
5. Published a paper on the Theory of Loci (1867) and an essay on the Differential Calculus (1868).
6. Published On Diffraction of Light (1879) and Illustrative Examples in Differential and Integral Calculus (1880).

Born 25 July 1905, Stepps, near Glasgow, Scotland. Died 26 January 1974, Glasgow, Scotland.
