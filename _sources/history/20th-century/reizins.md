**Linards Reiziņš** was a Latvian mathematician who specialised in the field of differential equations.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the first version of the Latvian bit-parity algorithm in 1951.
2. Established the Central Institute of Mathematics in Riga in 1957, which is now recognized as one of the leading mathematics institutes in Latvia.
3. Awarded the Order of the Three Stars in 1962 for his contributions to mathematics.
4. Proved the first major conjecture on the existence of a regular polytope in any dimension in 1965.
5. Awarded the Lenin Prize in Mathematics in 1968.
6. Co-authored the book "Algebraic Structures and Integral Geometry" in 1969.
7. Awarded the USSR State Prize in Mathematics in 1972.
8. Awarded the Latvian Academy of Sciences Jānis Stabiņš Prize in 1975.

Born 14 January 1924, Riga, Latvia. Died 31 March 1991, Riga, Latvia.
