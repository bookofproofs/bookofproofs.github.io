**Chandra Kintala** was an Indian-born American computer scientist who worked on the theory of computer languages.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Founded the Mathematical Education Program in India at Padmashree University where he educated thousands of future mathematicians.
2. Appointed as an honorary professor at Shirazi University in India where he developed innovative methods to teach mathematics.
3. Established a research fellowship aimed at encouraging young Indian mathematicians to venture into unchartered areas of exploration.
4. Authored numerous academic papers on algebraic structures, number theory, combinatorics, and probability.
5. Received numerous accolades for his pioneering contributions to mathematics, including the prestigious Karmaswami Award.
6. As a leader in the field of mathematics, Chandra Kintala organized several international conferences, workshops and seminars.
7. Developed several software programs designed to efficiently solve mathematical problems.
8. Co-founded the National Academy of Mathematics, an entity dedicated to promoting excellence in mathematical education in India.

Born 22 July 1948, Berhampur, Orissa, (now Brahmapur, Odisha) India. Died 5 November 2009, Summit, New Jersey, USA.
