**John Kemeny** was a Hungarian-born American mathematician and computer scientist best known as the co-inventor of the BASIC computer language.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the programming language BASIC in collaboration with Thomas Kurtz.
2. Worked with James Ax on the Ax-Kemeny logic of decision making.
3. Introduced important computer languages, such as Denton and Montebello.
4. Demonstrated the importance of computers in education through the Dartmouth College Time Sharing System (DTSS).
5. Created the “Kemeny Constant”, a mathematical value used in the evaluation of algorithmic efficiencies.
6. Received the U.S. Presidential Medal of Freedom in honor of his accomplishments.

Born 31 May 1926, Budapest, Hungary. Died 26 December 1992, Etna, New Hampshire, USA.
