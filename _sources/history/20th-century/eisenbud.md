**David Eisenbud** is an American mathematician who is known for his work in algebra, algebraic geometry and symbolic computation.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Appointed as Mathematical Science Research Institute (MSRI) Director and Professor of Mathematics at the University of California, Berkeley in 1997.
2. Winner of the National Medal of Science in 2016 for his pioneering work in commutative algebra and algebraic geometry.
3. Received the Wolf Prize in Mathematics in 2000 for his contributions to commutative algebra and algebraic geometry, as well as for his leadership of mathematical organizations.
4. Elected to the National Academy of Sciences in 1987 and American Philosophical Society in 2008.
5. Appointed to the National Science Board in 2002.
6. Received the Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany, one of the highest awards the nation gives.
7. Served as President of the American Mathematical Society (AMS) from 2003-2005. 
8. Co-authored the book “Cohomology of Toric Varieties”.
9. 2013 American Mathematical Society’s Leroy P Steele Prize for Lifetime Achievement in Mathematics. 
10. Founding director of the Mathematical Sciences Research Institute in Berkeley, California.

Born 8 April 1947, New York City, New York, USA.
