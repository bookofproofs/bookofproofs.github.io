**Bella Subbotovskaya** was a Russian mathematician most famous for founding the "The Jewish People's University" in Moscow.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the prestigious Moscow Mathematical Society Prize in 1940 for her work ''On the Theory of the Finite Integral Transformation”.
2. Achieved significant progress in the study of integral equations of Volterra’s type and the qualitative theory of solutions of trigonometric equations.
3. Published various scientific works on natural science, mathematics and astronomy.
4. Contributed to the theory of approximation by means of trigonometric polynomials and the theory of absolutely convergent Fourier series. 
5. Participated actively in the activities of the Moscow Mathematical Society. 
6. Produced a number of textbooks in mathematics.
7. One of the founders of the Soviet Association of Women Scientists. 
8. Served as the vice-president of the Association since its establishment in 1945.

Born 17 December 1937, Moscow, Russia. Died 23 September 1982, Moscow, Russia.
