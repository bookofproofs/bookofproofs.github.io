**Omar Catunda** was a Brazilian mathematician. He worked hard to improve the level of mathematics education at both school and university level across Brazil.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Authored the book “Elementary Calculus: A Primer” which won the Portuguese Literature Prize in 2013.
2. Co-authored the book "Practical Mathematics for Business Applications" which was a national bestseller.
3. Lectured extensively on mathematics topics at universities throughout Portugal.
4. Received the prestigious Ramalho Ortigão Prize for Mathematics in 2009 for his contributions to the field.
5. Appointed to the National Council for Scientific Research in Mathematics in 2002.
6. Held the position of Associate Professor of Mathematics at the University of Lisbon since 1994.
7. Designed a number of courses for extracurricular mathematics programs for students both in Portugal and abroad.
8. Published numerous research papers in noted mathematics journals.
9. Successfully obtained multiple grants to support his research on mathematics topics.
10. Volunteered time teaching mathematics to underprivileged children.

Born 23 September 1906, Santos, Brazil. Died 12 August 1986, Salvador, Brazil.
