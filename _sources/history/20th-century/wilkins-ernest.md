**J Ernest Wilkins** was an African-American mathematician and nuclear scientist. He worked on various pure mathematical topics as well as researching in nuclear engineering and optics.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded a doctoral degree in mathematics in 1945, becoming the first African-American to receive a doctoral degree in mathematics from the University of Chicago.
2. Published more than 50 articles and books on mathematics, covering fields like differential equations, number theory, hydrodynamics, and elasticity.
3. Appointed an associate professor at Howard University in 1949, becoming one of the first African-American mathematicians to teach at a historically black college or university.
4. Served as President of the Association for the Advancement of Science, representing the needs and interests of African-American scientists and engineers.
5. Served as a member of the President's Science Advisory Committee to the White House, providing advice to the President on science and technology-related issues.
6. Received the National Medal of Science in 1975 for his work in mathematics. 
7. Received honorary doctorate degrees from numerous universities, including Columbia University and Howard University.
8. Was made a fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences in 1978, becoming the first African-American to be so honored.

Born 27 November 1923, Chicago, Illinois, USA. Died 1 May2011, Fountain Hills, Arizona, USA.
