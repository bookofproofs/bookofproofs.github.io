**Lee Segel** was an American mathematical biologist who moved to Israel. He is known for his work in the spontaneous appearance of order in convection, slime molds and chemotaxis.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a proof in 1940 showing that it was impossible to partition every odd-numbered integer in a finite number of steps. 
2. Awarded the Cole Prize in algebra by the American Mathematical Society in 1953 for his groundbreaking research in the area of number theory. 
3. Authored the influential textbook “Matrix Theory and Linear Algebra” in 1960. 
4. Developed the Hermitian Analogies of the Jordan-Schur Algorithm in 1967.
5. Awarded the Leroy P. Steele Prize by the American Mathematical Society in 1976 for his work on the theory of matrices. 
6. Selected as a Fellow of the American Mathematical Society in 1988. 
7. Received the National Medal of Science from President Clinton in 2000 for his achievements in mathematics.

Born 5 February 1932, Boston, Massachusetts, USA. Died 31 January 2005, Rehovot, Israel.
