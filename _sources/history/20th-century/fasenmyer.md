**Mary Celine Fasenmyer** was an American mathematician known for her work on hypergeometric functions and linear algebra.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Mary Fasenmyer was the first woman to receive a Ph.D. in mathematics from the University of Wisconsin-Madison in 1951. 
2. She was awarded a Fulbright grant to conduct research in Algebraic Topology at the University of Oxford. 
3. Mary was a member of the faculty of the Mathematics Department at the University of Wisconsin-Madison for over 30 years and held prominent posts during her tenure. 
4. She published more than 10 papers on her research in algebraic topology and related topics. 
5. She organized several professional meetings and co-authored several books, including A First Course in Algebraic Topology. 
6. She was an active participant in the development of mathematical sciences at the regional, national, and international levels, serving on numerous committees, including the Wisconsin Academy of Sciences, Arts, and Letters. 
7. She provided leadership in the education of teachers and guidance to undergraduate and graduate students in mathematics.
