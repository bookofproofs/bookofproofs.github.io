**Elena Popoviciu** was a Romanian mathematician who worked in functional analysis.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Elena Moldovan Popoviciu is recognised for her research in the field of differential and integral equations, as well as for her fundamental contributions to the development and application of topological methods in analysis.
2. She is credited for developing the topological properties and methods of Fichera's Theory which is now known as the Fichera-Moldovan Theorem.
3. Her mathematician husband, Ioan Popoviciu, collaborated with her and developed the Popoviciu Inequality which they shared together.
4. She was awarded the Romanian Academy Prize (1966) and the Romanian National Order of Merit (2007). 
5. She was appointed the Head of the Mathematics Department at the University of Cluj within her lifetime.
6. She served as the President of the Romanian Mathematical Society for a year (1996-1997).
7. She supervised over 15 Ph.D. thesis and published numerous books, papers, and lectures.

Born 26 August 1924, Cluj, Romania. Died 24 June 2009, Cluj, Romania.
