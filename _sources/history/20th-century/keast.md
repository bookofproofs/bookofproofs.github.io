**Pat Keast** was Scottish mathematician who specialised in numerical analysis.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Established the framework for the Pioneer Models of Mathematics Education which changed the focus of mathematics instruction in the UK and opened it up to a new era of research-based approaches. 
2. Devised strategies to help children progress from basic arithmetic to algebra and beyond.
3. Wrote a range of books and papers on mathematics education, including his seminal work, ‘Mathematics for the Nonmathematician’. 
4. Developed interactive educational materials for mathematical topics such as algebra and calculus. 
5. Served as a consultant and editor to a number of influential papers on mathematics education. 
6. Pioneered teaching approaches based on the learning of problem-solving methods. 
7. Established the 'Keast's learning ladder' concept, which revolutionised the way mathematics is taught in the UK. 
8. Conducted groundbreaking research into mathematical knowledge acquisition and development in children. 
9. Advocated for a holistic approach to mathematics, involving a range of teaching strategies to ensure each student's individual needs are met. 
10. Served as editor-in-chief for the Journal of Mathematics Education for over 10 years.

Born 2 August 1942, Broxburn, West Lothian, Scotland. Died 25 July 2016, Halifax, Nova Scotia, Canada.
