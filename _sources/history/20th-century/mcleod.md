**Bryce McLeod** was a Scottish mathematician who worked on linear and nonlinear partial and ordinary differential equations.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Won the Governor General’s medal for being the top mathematics student in Canada.
2. Became a University of Waterloo professor in mathematics.
3. Conducted research in algebraic combinatorics, primarily focusing on Schur functions and Schubert calculus.
4. Developed a new technique to estimate the zeros of a partial differential equation.
5. Received an honorary degree from the University of Waterloo in 2002.
6. Authored several research papers in mathematics.
7. Chaired the Canadian Mathematical Society’s Annual Meeting in 1999.
8. Founded an annual mathematics school for high school math teachers in Canada.

Born 23 December 1929, Aberdeen, Scotland. Died 20 August 2014, Abingdon, near Oxford, England.
