**Donald Spencer** was an American mathematician, known for work on deformations of complex manifolds.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Donald C Spencer was a pioneering researcher and teacher in the field of mathematics. 
2. He helped to establish the modern foundations of mathematical analysis and made significant theoretical and practical contributions to the development of solving differential equations.
3. He was the first to apply the branching typology method to the study of singular integral equations.
4. He also developed a novel method of solving Sturmâ€“Liouville equations and an algorithm for calculating trigonometric series coefficients. 
5. He published several influential books and monographs on differential equations, including "Foundations of Analysis". 
6. As a professor, he encouraged integrative, exploratory learning and was an advocate for the use of mathematics in social science applications.

Born 25 April 1912, Boulder, Colorado, USA. Died 23 December 2001, Durango, Colorado, USA.
