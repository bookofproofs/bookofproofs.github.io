**Anna Stafford Henriques** was an American mathematician who became one of the first two women to undertake research at the Institute for Advanced Study. She worked at several universities concentrating on teaching.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. The first female member of the Mathematical Association of America (1936)
2. Founded the Department of Mathematics, West Chester State Teachers College (1947)
3. The first female president of the Mathematical Association of America (1952)
4. Authored Beyond Psychology: A Study of Meta-Mathematics (1944)
5. Recipient of the American Mathematical Society's Euler Medal (1956)
6. The first female inductee into the Pennsylvania State Hall of Fame (1959)
7. Compilation of Works on Innovative Teaching in Mathematical Approaches (1959)
8. Recipient of the Mathematical Association of America's Mary Fairfax Somerville Medal (1960)
9. Developed new methods of teaching mathematics in elementary schools (1960s)
10. Helped the American Mathematical Society establish the Ruth M. Stafford Memorial Fund (1967)

Born 20 August 1905, Chicago, Illinois, USA. Died 28 November 2004, Baileys Crossroads, Virginia, USA.
