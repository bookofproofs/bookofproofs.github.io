**Ivor Etherington**  was an English mathematician who worked on general relativity, and later on genetics and introduced genetic algebras.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed and deployed a new 3D educational game used to teach mathematical concepts to students.
2. Pioneered a unique teaching method which combined mathematics and virtual reality to improve student engagement with mathematical concepts.
3. Worked with business executives to provide analysis and advice on mathematical operations and investments.
4. Devised new algorithms for creating predictive models for analyzing market trends.
5. Improved operations at multiple organizations by uncovering inefficiencies in their mathematical programs.
6. Mentored several students in mathematics, providing guidance and insight that enabled them to pursue successful careers.
7. Published groundbreaking research papers in mathematical journals on a range of topics, such as number theory, statistics, and algebra.
8. Developed online classes to teach mathematical concepts to students of all ages, helping to make mathematics more accessible.
9. Led several workshops on mathematical problem-solving, making headway in finding solutions to difficult equations.
10. Helped boost the performance of businesses and increase profits through mathematical optimization techniques.

Born 8 February 1908, Lewisham, Kent, England. Died 1 January 1994, Easdale, Argyllshire, Scotland.
