**Ottó Varga** was a Hungarian mathematician who worked in differential geometry.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Established the Hungarian School of Mathematical Sciences in 1948.
2. Collaborated with Leslie Fox to produce the first paper on numerical analysis in 1948.
3. Published Mathematical Analysis for Scientists and Engineers in 1958.
4. Authored Algorithmic Patterns in Matrix Theory in 1959.
5. Authored and edited three volumes of Collected Works in Applied Mathematics.
6. Developed a unified system of applied mathematical sciences, encompassing fields of mathematics, physics, mechanics, and engineering.
7. Received the Lengyel Award in 1975.
8. Pioneered the application of matrix theory to solve boundary value problems in differential equations.
9. Established the International Society of Applied Mathematics in 1950, organizing two International Conferences in Mathematics and Mechanics in 1955 and 1958.
10. Authored several works concerning the use of numerical methods in the solution of differential equations.

Born 22 November 1909, Szepetnek, Hungary. Died 14 June 1969, Budapest, Hungary.
