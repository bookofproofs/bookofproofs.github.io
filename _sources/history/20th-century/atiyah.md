**Michael Atiyah** worked in Topology and Geometry and was best known for his work on K-theory and the Atiyah-Singer Index Theorem. He was awarded a Fields Medal in 1966. He retired from the mastership of Trinity College Cambridge to live in Scotland.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the Fields Medal in 1966 for his work in topology, geometry and differential equations. 
2. Became a Senior Research Fellow at Trinity College and a Royal Society Research Professor in 1962. 
3. Won the Wolf Prize in Mathematics in 1988 for his resolution of the mathematical challenges. 
4. Awarded the Abel Prize in 2004 for his work in geometry and the theory of irregularities of surfaces. 
5. Co-awarded the Copley Medal in 2018 for works on mathematics and its connections with theoretical physics. 
6. Named the first President of The Royal Society from 2003 to 2005. 
7. Announced in 2019 the mastery of the Riemann hypothesis.
