**Grace Alele-Williams** was the first Nigerian to be awarded a doctorate. She became a professor of mathematical education, then the first female Nigerian Vice-Chancellor of the University of Benin.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. The first woman appointed as the vice-chancellor of a Nigerian University.
2. Established an externally funded mathematics laboratory at the University of Benin.
3. Taught mathematics for over 25 years at various universities in Nigeria and Europe
4. Appointed a professor of mathematics at the University of Benin at the age of 35
5. As the Education Minister for the State of Bendel, she increased the number of technical courses, including mathematics
6. Established a mathematics series of textbooks for Nigerian secondary schools
7. Fostered the introduction of modern mathematics curriculums at secondary schools in Bendel State.
8. Set up mobile libraries to bring mathematics to remote rural schools in Nigeria.
9. Awarded with honorary degrees and awards, including the Nigeria National Merit Award in Mathematics Education and the Nigerian National Order of Merit in Mathematics.

Born 16 December 1932, Warri, Nigeria. Died 25 March 2022, Lagos, Nigeria.
