**Shimshon Amitsur** was an Israeli mathematician who worked in ring theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Appointed professor of algebra at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem in 1949 and held this position for the remainder of his career.
2. Pioneered research in group theory and ring theory, establishing the foundations of modern abstract algebra.
3. Developed a definitive set of Fixed Point Theorems, which became invaluable in the fields of ring theory and algebraic geometry.
4. Formulated the Amitsur-Levitzki Theorem, a highly influential theorem on field extensions.
5. Wrote over 160 works on algebra-related topics, making him one of the most prolific algebraists in history.
6. Won numerous awards including the Israel Prize for Mathematics in 1965, the Wolf Prize in 1985, and the EMET Prize for Science in 1988.

Born 26 August 1921, Jeruselem, Palestine (now Israel). Died 5 September 1994, Jeruselem, Israel.
