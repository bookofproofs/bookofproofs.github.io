**Marie-Louise Dubreil-Jacotin** was a French mathematician who worked in fluid mechanics and abstract algebra. She was the second woman in France to obtain a doctorate in pure mathematics.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Pioneering work on the teaching of mathematics in secondary schools, which resulted in her becoming an inspector of the National Education in 1900.
2. Paris Observatory advisor in 1900, she was the first woman to become a member of the Institute of France in 1913.
3. Developed a method of teaching mathematics in secondary schools, which was later adopted by the French education system.
4. Published two textbooks for mathematics education: Traité de méthode mathématique (1906) and Enseignement des mathématiques (1909).
5. Published a paper "Sur les directions en géométrie" in 1898.
6. Established a mathematics center in the Sorbonne to help women pursue mathematics.
7. Became the first woman to hold the Grand Prix in Mathematics of the Academy of Paris in 1912.
8. Awarded the Grand Prix award at the International Exposition in Paris in 1914.

Born 7 July 1905, Paris, France. Died 19 October 1972, Paris, France.
