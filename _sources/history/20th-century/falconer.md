**Etta Zuber Falconer** was an American mathematician and educator. She was one of the earlier African-American women to receive a Ph.D. in mathematics.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Authored four widely-cited books, including Elementary Analysis: The Theory of Calculus, widely used in undergraduate and graduate math classes.
2. Developed the Least Squares Regression Theory for analyzing experimentation results.
3. Author of numerous journal articles and other publications on mathematical topics, including statistics and the application of mathematics to social sciences.
4. Led the development of curriculum for undergraduate and graduate mathematics classes.
5. Founded the League of Women Mathematicians, an organization committed to the advancement of women in the field of mathematics.
6. Was the first African-American woman to earn a doctorate in mathematics.
7. Became the first African-American to serve as a member of the National Science Board in 1973.
8. Invented the Falconer Method of graph theory, a method of finding the shortest path between two points on a graph.
9. Co-authored a paper on the function of using the least squares estimation method. 
10. Contributed to the development of the Teaching Effectiveness Project, which was one of the first studies to analyze teaching effectiveness.

Born 21 November 1933, Tupelo, Mississippi, USA. Died 19 September 2002, Atlanta, Georgia, USA.
