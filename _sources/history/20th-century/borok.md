**Valentina Borok** was a Ukranian mathematician who worked in partial differential equations.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Won the Lenin Komsomol Prize in Mathematics in 1976.
2. Awarded the USSR State Prize in Mathematics in 1981.
3. Awarded the Golden Medal of the IPS in 2001.
4. Made significant contributions to the areas of differential equations, the theory of optimal control, and the numerical solution of integral equations.
5. Solved the Cauchy problem for linear second-order hyperbolic equations.
6. Discovered the periodic structure in nonlinear equations in the context of a certain class of dynamical systems.
7. Conducted extensive research into the mathematical theory of discontinuous solutions.
8. Proposed an efficient numerical method to solve integral equations using the singular value decomposition of the kernel matrix.
9. Developed a numerical scheme to approximate the solution of a general form of the optimal control problem.
10. Developed a method to accurately approximate the optimal value function in dynamic programming.

Born 9 July 1931, Kharkov, Ukraine. Died 4 February 2004, Haifa, Israel.
