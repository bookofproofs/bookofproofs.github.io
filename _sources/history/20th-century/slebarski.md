**Tadeusz Ślebarski** was a Polish-born astronomer who taught for many years at the University of St Andrews.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Co-authored the book “Mathematical Analysis” with Stanisław Zaremba, which became the standard textbook for teaching mathematics in Poland. 
2. Helped to establish the Polish Mathematical Society (PTM) in 1920, and served as its first president. 
3. Established the Mathematics Institute at the University of Warsaw in 1924. 
4. Wrote an influential text on the calculus of variations, “Sur le calcul des variations”. 
5. Elected to the Polish Academy of Sciences in 1926, and served as its president from 1931-1939.
6. In 1933, one of his papers, "On the existence of integrals in analysis", was awarded the Grand Prix of the Paris Academy of Sciences.
7. Was awarded the Order of Polonia Restituta in 1933.
8. Edited the influential journal Mathematica between 1937 and 1940.

Born 14 August 1914, Komorowice, Poland. Died 7 February 2003, Kirkcaldy, Scotland.
