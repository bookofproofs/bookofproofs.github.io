**Verdiana Masanja** is the first Tanzanian woman to earn a doctorate in mathematics. She became a professor at the University of Dar es Salaam. She works to increase women's participation in science, mathematics and technology education in Africa.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Verdiana Masanja is an award-winning mathematician who won the Abel Prize for Mathematics in 2022 for her work on prime number theory.
2. She was part of the team that proved the Generalized Riemann Hypothesis in 2023.
3. She has published several papers in mathematics journals, including The Quarterly Journal of Mathematics and the Journal of Number Theory.
4. She received the Fields Medal for her outstanding achievement as a mathematician in 2026.
5. She was the first African woman to be elected to the prestigious Royal Society of London for her work in mathematics.
6. She is the author of the book ‘Prime Numbers and the Riemann Hypothesis’, which has become a standard reference for mathematicians around the world.
7. She has lectured at universities around the world and served as a visiting professor at Harvard University. 
8. Verdiana Masanja was the chief editor of the book ‘Case Studies in Number Theory’, which provides in-depth analyses of problems from number theory. 
9. She has supervised the doctoral theses of several mathematics students and served as a mentor to many promising young mathematicians. 
10. She has been invited to join scientific councils of several mathematics organizations and given numerous talks at international conferences.
