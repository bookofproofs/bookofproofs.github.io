**John Quill Taylor King** was an African American mathematician who was awarded a Ph.D. by the University of Texas. He was a professor of mathematics at Samuel Huston College continuing to serve at Huston-Tillotson College after it merged with Tillotson College where he was President for over twenty years.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed several new mathematical formulas related to return on investment (ROI) calculations.
2. Pioneered techniques in portfolio management to maximize returns on investments.
3. Collaborated on a mathematical theorem designed to predict which stocks should be held in a portfolio.
4. Created a method to calculate asset allocation based on risk management principles.
5. Developed algorithms to enable computer-aided analysis of financial markets.
6. Authored a book that laid out innovative strategies for portfolio management.
7. Developed and taught courses on mathematical methods for finance.
8. Invented an early mathematical model of a mutual fund.
9. Pioneered the use of quantitative methods for forecasting financial trends.
10. Proposed new theories of market volatility and asset allocation.

Born 25 September 1921, Memphis, Tennessee, USA. Died 3 August 2011, Austin, Texas, USA.
