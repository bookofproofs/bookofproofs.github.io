**Pierre-Louis Lions** is a French mathematician best known for his work on partial differential equations and the calculus of variations.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Received the prestigious Fields Medal in 1994 for his work on Partial Differential Equations.
2. Awarded the Gerard Debreu Prize by the Society for Social Choice and Welfare in 2004.
3. Appointed to the French National Order of the Legion of Honor in 2005.
4. Awarded the Michael Croucher Award in 2006 by the Mathematical Association of France.
5. Appointed the Chair of the Inria-Microsoft Research Joint Centre in 2011.
6. Received the Abbe Fund Prize for his development of the theory of viscosity solutions in 2019.
7. Elected a Foreign Member of the Royal Society in 2020.

Born 11 August 1956, Grasse, Alpes-Maritimes, France.
