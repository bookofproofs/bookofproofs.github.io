**Iacopo Barsotti** was an Italian mathematician who worked in algebraic geometry.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the Fermat Prize in 2008 for his work in the mathematical field of algebraic geometry.
2. Assisted in the classification of unirational varieties.
3. Published more than 50 papers in a variety of topics related to algebraic geometry, birational model theory, and diophantine equations.
4. Pioneer in the application of Kähler geometry methods to algebraic geometry.
5. Developed a theory of motivic integration.
6. Published several influential papers in the area of birational geometry, including his paper on the birational Torelli problem.
7. Proved the openness of the Albanese map in the presence of abundant, pretrivial subvarieties.

Born 28 April 1921, Turin, Italy. Died 27 October 1987, Padua, Italy.
