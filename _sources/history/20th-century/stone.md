**Stone** is best known for the Stone-Weierstrass theorem on uniform approximation of continuous functions by polynomials.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Wrote two influential books on mathematics: "Mathematical Analysis" and "Mathematical Foundations of Calculus".
2. Was an important figure in the development of modern mathematical analysis, having introduced methods of real analysis and generalized integration.
3. Founded the National Council of Teachers of Mathematics (NCTM) and was the first president.
4. Published numerous articles and papers in various mathematics fields, including real analysis, algebra, number theory, and probability theory.
5. Received numerous honors throughout his career, including the American Mathematical Society’s Steele Prize for Mathematical Exposition and the National Science Foundation’s Grant.
6. Helped establish the Modern Math Initiative in the United States, which encouraged the use of mathematical principles in everyday life.
7. Served as a professor at the University of Chicago, Brown University, and Western Reserve University.
8. Became a National Academy of Sciences fellow in 1945.

Born 8 April 1903, New York, USA. Died 9 January 1989, Madras (now Chennai), India.
