**Celia Hoyles** is a Professor of Mathematics Education at University College London. She has received many honours for her contributions to education such as an OBE, a DBE, and the first Hans Freudenthal Medal by the International Commission on Mathematical Instruction.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Appointed the first female president of the Mathematical Association in 1999.
2. Awarded an OBE in 2005 for Services to Mathematics Education. 
3. Developed a framework for Primary Mathematics Guidelines in 2003 which has informed national curriculum guidance ever since. 
4. Compiled mathematics tests to assess the development of thought processes among young children. 
5. Appointed CBE in the Queen's birthday honors of 2008. 
6. Established national programs to promote the importance of mathematics education in schools. 
7. Contributed to many reports about mathematics and its role in education including the Practising Mathematics in the Primary School Report in 2009. 
8. Authored numerous publications such as Number Through the Ages and Mathematics Explained.

Born 18 May1946, London, England.
