**Alexei Kostrikin** was a Russian mathematician who worked in algebra and algebraic geometry.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Proved the Elliott-Kaloujnine Conjecture using the Borsuk-Ulam Theorem in 1962. 
2. Co-authored Kostrikin–Shmel’kin’s Theorem, which is used in quadratic form theory.
3. Developed a new method of analyzing finitely generated modules over principal ideal domains.
4. Published several influential works in algebra, including Linear Algebra, Linear Algebra and Algebraic Geometry, and Quadratic Forms. 
5. Rewrote I. M. Gelfand's "Algebra", an influential textbook on the subject.
6. Awarded the Laurea honoris causa in 1982 by the University of Padova. 
7. Recipient of the Ostrowski Prize in 1992.
8. Awarded the State Prize of the USSR in 1973. 
9. Named an honorary member of the London Mathematical Society in 1989. 
10. Elected a vice-president of the International Mathematical Union in 1991.

Born 12 February 1929, Bolshoi Morets, Yelan district, Volgograd, Russia. Died 22 September 2000, Moscow, Russia.
