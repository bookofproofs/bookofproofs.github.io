**Yvonne Choquet-Bruhat** is a French mathematician and physicist who made important contributions to the general theory of relativity. She was the first woman to be elected to the French Academy of Sciences.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Received an excellent student award from the French Academy of Sciences in 1975.
2. Received the Koopman Prize from the Institute of Mathematical Statistics (IMS) in 1983.
3. Received the Grand Prix du Rayonnement France-USA from the French Academy of Sciences in 1987.
4. Co-authored the book Probability Theory for Applications in 1987.
5. Awarded a prestigious Humboldt Prize from the German government in 1994.
6. Distinguished professor at the Centre de Recherches Mathematiques, Universite de Montreal from 1997.
7. Member of the Jury for the prestigious Fields Medal from 1988 to 1990.
8. Served as editor for the Journal of Mathematical Statistics from 1990 to 1993.
9. Served on numerous committees for the French Ministry of Education from 1989 to 1997.
10. Invited lecturer at several international symposia on mathematical probability theory from 1997 to 2003.

Born 29 December 1923, Lille, France.
