**Marília Chaves Peixoto** was the first Brazilian woman to be awarded a Ph.D. in mathematics. In 1951 she was the first Brazilian woman to be elected a member of the Brazilian Academy of Sciences. She made substantial contributions to what is now known as Peixoto's Theorem on the structural stability of dynamical systems.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Pioneering a groundbreaking technique for applying calculus and projecting economic growth in Brazil. 
2. Becoming the first woman in Latin America to be awarded a doctoral degree in mathematics.
3. Developing and teaching the first course on economic theory in Brazil.
4. Creating the first comprehensive mathematical formula for electricity costs throughout Brazil.
5. Establishing the first official scientific summit in Brazil, which focused on mathematics.
6. Authoring dozens of important mathematical papers, including her doctoral dissertation on Lie groups.
7. Founding several journals devoted to mathematics and economics.
8. Mentoring the next few generations of mathematicians through her work at the University of São Paulo and the University of Brasília.

Born 24 February 1921, Santana do Livramento, Brazil. Died 5 January 1961, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
