**Vera W de Spinadel** was the first woman to be awarded a mathematics Ph.D. by the University of Buenos Aires. She was an Argentine mathematician whose main contributions were to mathematics in architecture, art, and design. She introduced the "metallic means family" which generalises the Golden Ratio.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Became the first female professor of mathematics at the National University of Córdoba in Argentina in 1944. 
2. Appointed to the National Academy of Exact, Physical and Natural Sciences in Argentina in 1962. 
3. Formed the “Topology Seminar” at the National University of Córdoba in 1963, a seminar that survived until the present day. 
4. Presented her dissertation titled “Field Theory” in 1941, which was later published in 1961.
5. Published a book about the history of mathematics in Argentina, titled “From La Plata to Córdoba” in 1982. 
6. Co-authored a book about Topology titled “Analysis Situs with Applications” in 1982. 
7. Authored various theses and essays about mathematics subject matter.

Born 22 August 1929, Buenos Aires, Argentina. Died 26 January 2017, Buenos Aires, Argentina.
