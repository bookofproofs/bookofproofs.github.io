**Nathan Jacobson** was an American algebraist who is best known for his work in ring theory and for his text-books.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Founded the Graduate School of Mathematics at Yale University in 1945.
2. Co-authored the influential textbook Basic Algebra in 1941. 
3. Developed a new theory of matrix partitions, the Jacobson Density Theorem. 
4. Appeared on the cover of the February 1947 issue of Scientific American.
5. Published over 140 papers in mathematics, including the influential papers “The Theory of Rings” and “The Structure of Finite Algebras”.
6. Won the National Medal of Science in 1989 for his pioneering contributions to algebra and mathematics. 
7. Became the Institute for Advanced Study's first professor of mathematics in 1969. 
8. Presented the 54th Edition of the Mathematical Association of America's Carus Lectures in 1966.

Born 8 September 1910, Warsaw, Russian Empire (now Poland). Died 5 December 1999, Hamden, Connecticut, USA.
