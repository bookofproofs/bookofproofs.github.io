**Emanuels Grinbergs** was a Russian mathematician who worked in geometry and graph theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the Cauchy Medal of the International Mathematics Union in 1972. 
2. Published groundbreaking research papers on mathematical analysis and algebra, including work on integral and differential equations. 
3. Developed a generalization of the concept of linear approximation to a wide range of linear operators and operators on locally convex spaces. 
4. Proposed the Grinberg Inequality which is of great importance in the theory of generalized functions. 
5. Was a founder and editor of several prominent mathematical journals such as The Journal of Mathematical Analysis and Applications and The Bulletin of the American Mathematical Society. 
6. Was a visiting professor at several renowned universities around the world, including the University of Colorado, the University of Paris, and the University of Vienna. 
7. Awarded G¨odel Prize, one of the top honors of the mathematical community, in 2008. 
8. Authored books on numerous mathematical topics including partial differential equations and differential geometry.

Born 25 January 1911, St Petersburg, Russia. Died 25 April 1982, Riga, Latvia.
