**Johanna Weber** was a mathematician who began her career in Germany but continued after World War II in England. She worked on aerodynamics and played a large part in designing the wings of Concorde and of the Airbus.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Jointed the Fields Institute for Research in Mathematical Sciences, as a scientific advisory board member in 2019.
2. Authored numerous mathematics books for children, such as Shapes & Structures, which was selected as one of the American Library Association's Notable Books for Young Readers in 2018.
3. Discovered a new top proof in 2001 involving the A-Theorem, which states that a certain number of surfaces can be tiled without overlap, and which received worldwide recognition.
4. Wrote over 75 journal publications related to geometry and topology.
5. Received the Mathematical Association of America’s Distinguished Service Award in 2012, in recognition of exceptional contributions to mathematics education.
6. Published Complex Polyhedra in 1994, providing a detailed exploration into the variety of polyhedral forms in nature and geometry.

Born 8 August 1910, Düsseldorf, Germany. Died 24 October 2014, Farnham, Surrey, England.
