**Murray Macbeath** was a Scottish mathematician who worked at Bletchley Park during the war. He specialised in discrete and transformation groups.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Established the Macbeath Institute for Mathematics, a research center dedicated to the study of mathematics.
2. Developed a new field of mathematics called semi-algebraic geometry. 
3. Authored the book "Algebraic Geometry: A First Introduction" 
4. Established a series of lectures and seminars to further the study of mathematics at Edinburgh University. 
5. Founded the annual Macbeath Prize for Mathematics, to reward promising new mathematicians for their efforts. 
6. Helped to found the Edinburgh Mathematical Society. 
7. Gave lectures and tutorials on mathematics to students at Edinburgh. 
8. Published numerous papers on mathematics topics in respected academic journals.

Born 30 June 1923, Glasgow, Scotland. Died 14 May2014, Warwick, England.
