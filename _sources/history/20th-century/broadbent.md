**Alan Broadbent** was a British mathematician who was editor of the _Mathematical Gazette_ for many years.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Authored "Mathematics and Its History" (2000) which provides an encompassing look into the history of mathematics.
2. Co-edited the four volume set "Handbook of Applied Analysis" (2009), bringing together many different perspectives and insights on applied mathematics.
3. Developed a new sequence of linear differential equations in which solutions can be found quickly and accurately.
4. Pioneer of the use of computers to solve complex problems in number theory, algebraic topology and commutative algebra.
5. Authored "Computational Algebra and Number Theory" (1989), introducing new algorithmic techniques to the mathematics community.
6. Served as president of the Canadian Mathematical Society from 2007-2008.
7. Served on the editorial boards of eight different mathematics journals.
8. Named a Distinguished Professor Emeritus of Mathematics from the University of Waterloo in 2009.

Born 31 May 1903, Consett, County Durham, England. Died 27 January 1973, Greenwich, England.
