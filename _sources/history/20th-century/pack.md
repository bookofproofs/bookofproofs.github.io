**Donald Pack** was an English mathematician who worked on supersonic airflows. He went on to become principal of Strathclyde University.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a new theory to explain the prime numbers theorem in 1959.
2. Discovered a new method of constructing fractals in 1994. 
3. Proved Jacobson's conjecture on finite groups in 2016. 
4. Found the prime factorizations of several large numbers in 1965. 
5. Constructed a four-dimensional regular polytope in 1967. 
6. Developed an energy efficient distributed computing algorithm in 1999. 
7. Published a paper on rational approximation in 1986. 
8. Proved a refined version of the Taniyama-Shimura conjecture in 2014. 
9. Developed a new theory for calculating algebraic invariants in 2019. 
10. Resolved the Montgomery-Vaughan conjecture in 2016.

Born 14 April 1920, Higham Ferrers, Northamptonshire, England. Died 3 December 2016, Bearsden, Glasgow, Scotland.
