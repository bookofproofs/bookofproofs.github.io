**Joseph Kruskal** was an American mathematician, statistician and computer scientist who worked in combinatorics and graph theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the Kruskal-Wallis one-way analysis of variance by ranks 
2. Advanced Multi-dimensional scaling techniques 
3. Proposed the Kruskal-Katona theorem for graph theory 
4. Published groundbreaking research in graph theory, lattice theory, and logic 
5. Researched in combinatorial optimization 
6. Pioneered the first use of linear programming 
7. Established the mathematical foundations of cluster analysis 
8. Promoted the understanding of matrices and their properties 
9. Developed the theory of well-quasi-ordering
10. Advanced the mathematical field of graph minors theory

Born 29 January 1928, New York City, New York, USA. Died 19 September 2010, Maplewood, New Jersey, USA.
