**Richard Schoen** is an American mathematician known for his work in differential geometry and geometric analysis.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Proved the groundbreaking positive mass theorem in general relativity in 1979.
2. Became a Fellow of the American Mathematical Society in 1995. 
3. Won the Alfred P. Sloan fellowship in 1984.
4. Received the Bôcher Memorial Prize in 1983.
5. Awarded the National Medal of Science in 2018. 
6. Received the Chern Medal in 2011.
7. Awarded the Wolf Prize in Mathematics in 1991.
8. Earned an Excellence in Teaching Award at the University of California, Irvine. 
9. Awarded the American Mathematical Society's Rolf Schock Prize in 2003.
10. Appointed to the University of California President's Chair in 2000.

Born 23 October 1950, Celina, Ohio, USA.
