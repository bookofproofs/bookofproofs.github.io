**Leonard Adleman** is an American computer scientist best known for being one of the creators of the RSA encryption algorithm.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Development of the first provable algorithm for the Hamiltonian path problem in 1972.
2. Development of the RSA algorithm for public-key cryptography in 1977.
3. Work with Ron Rivest and Adi Shamir on the knapsack cryptosystem in 1978.
4. Pioneering research in molecular computation in 1994.
5. Introduction of DNA computing in 1994.
6. Coining the term "computational molecular biology" in 1994.
7. Research on graph theory, balancing theory, and finite automata in 1997.
8. Research on primality testing algorithms in 1998.

Born 31 December 1945, San Francisco, California, USA.
