**Ferran Sunyer** was a Catalan mathematician who overcame severe disabilities to produce some important results in complex analysis.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the influential Sunyer-Hedrick analytical method for calculating Dirichlet integrals.
2. Pioneer of a method for diverse and complex application of complex analysis.
3. Played a transformative role in the application of mathematics to grand challenges, such as transport and energy production.
4. Published numerous books and papers on mathematics, including topics such as nonlinear differential equations, numerical methods and the engineering mathematics of combustion.
5. Established the International Congress of Industrial and Applied Mathematics (ICIAM), founded in 1976.
6. Named Academician of the Real Academia de Ciencias at Barcelona (1954).
7. Received the Grand Cross of Alfonso X in 1975 and the Prince of Asturias Award in 1985. 
8. Awarded the Silver Medal of Merit by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers in 1990.
9. Authored books such as "The Theory of Orbits in Space Dynamics" (1959) and "Mathematics and the Natural Sciences" (1974).
10. Was an Honorary Member of the Royal Academy of Science and Art of Barcelona (since 1979) and the Astronomical Society of the Pacific (since 1992).

Born 6 February 1912, Figueres, Girona, Spain. Died 27 December 1967, Barcelona, Spain.
