**Dusa McDuff** is an English mathematician who works on symplectic geometry.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Became the first female professor of mathematics at Stony Brook University in 1975.
2. Became the first female full professor of mathematics at Barnard College in 1987.
3. Established the Stony Brook Women in Mathematics program in 1984 to encourage and support female mathematicians.
4. Received the National Medal of Science in 2009 for her extraordinary contributions in geometry and topology.
5. Was elected to the National Academy of Sciences in 1985 and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences in 1989.

7. Received numerous awards, including the Steele Prize for Lifetime Achievement in Mathematics (2002) and the Wolf Prize in Mathematics (2005).
8. Played a significant role in the proof of the existence of the Kronheimer-Mrowka knot invariants in 2003.
