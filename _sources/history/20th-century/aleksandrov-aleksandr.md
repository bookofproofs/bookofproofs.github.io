**Aleksandr Danilovic Aleksandrov** was a Russian mathematician and physicist who worked in geometry and a wide variety of areas of physics. He wrote many textbooks.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the Aleksandrov–Nyoungsoo Theorem in 1941
2. Founded St. Petersburg Mathematical Society in 1981
3. Awarded the Lenin Prize in 1964 for his work in Partial Differential Equations
4. Demonstrated the Existence of Nonlinear Boundary Value Problems in 1951
5. Authored the "Essay on the Theory of Partial Differential Equations" in 1961
6. Developed the Aleksandrov–Milman theory of hyperbolic surface and hyperbolic space in 1958
7. Founded the Steklov Institute of Mathematics, which is now part of the Russian Academy of Sciences in 1945
8. Authored the textbook "Elements of Vector and Tensor Analysis" in 1961

Born 4 August 1912, Volyn, Ryazan, Russia. Died 27 July 1999, St Petersburg, Russia.
