**James Mackay** is a Scottish mathematician and lawyer. As Lord Mackay of Clashfern he served 10 years as Lord Chancellor.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Received his PhD from Monash University in Mathematics in 1975.
2. Published significant research related to differential structures, complex geometry, and mathematical physics.
3. Taught Mathematics for over 30 years at the University of New South Wales.
4. Authored over 80 scientific papers during his mathematics career.
5. Received a Centenary Medal in 2003 in recognition of his contributions to mathematics.
6. Appointed a Fellow of the Australian Academy of Science in 2006.
7. Awarded degrees of Doctor of Science (honoris causa) from Monash University in 2009 and from the University of Queensland in 2012.
8. Became an Officer of the Order of Australia (AO) in 2018 in recognition of his distinguished service to education.

Born 2 July 1927, Edinburgh, Scotland.
