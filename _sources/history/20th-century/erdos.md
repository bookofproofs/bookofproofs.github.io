**Erdős** posed and solved problems in number theory and other areas and founded the field of discrete mathematics.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Co-authored over 1,500 mathematical papers
2. Developed the Erdős Number, which measures the relative collaboration distance between mathematicians
3. Developed the theory of random graphs
4. Proposed fundamental problems in graph theory, including the Erdős-Szekeres problem
5. Co-created the field of Ramsey theory
6. Helped to develop the fields of combinatorics and number theory
7. Pioneered the study of probabilistic methods in combinatorics
8. Helped to develop the ultrahyperbolic equation 
9. Together with Claude Shannon, Erdős developed the field of information theory

Born 26 March 1913, Budapest, Hungary. Died 20 September 1996, Warsaw, Poland.
