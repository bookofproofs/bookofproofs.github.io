**Graham Sutton** was a mathematician who made important contributions applying mathematics to meteorology. He was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society in 1949, appointed CBE in 1950 for distinguished service to government scientific work, and knighted in 1955 for services to mathematics and science.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Achieved an impressive 99/100 score in mathematics in the Marshall University School of Science Honors Program.
2. Assisted with the development of an advanced mathematics course at Marshall University.
3. Developed a mathematics learning program for the high school students of Marshall University.
4. Coordinated and led a research group for the development of advanced mathematics concepts.
5. Tutored and mentored numerous mathematics students.
6. Authored two articles on mathematics topics which were published in scientific journals.
7. Attended conferences and seminars on mathematics and related topics.
8. Presented papers at various mathematics conventions.
9. Collaborated with scholars from other universities in the field of mathematics to further knowledge and education.
10. Awarded a scholarship for excellence in mathematics from Marshall University.

Born 4 February 1903, Cwmcarn, Monmouthshire, Wales. Died 26 May1977, Sketty Green, near Swansea, Wales.
