**Caleb Gattegno** was an Egyption mathematical educator who is best known for his innovative approaches to teaching and learning mathematics, foreign languages and reading.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the theory of matchstick arithmetic, an innovative method of teaching mathematics to young children. 
2. Pioneered the use of “Tables” to represent numeric relationships in mathematics. 
3. Developed the Gattegno silence teaching method, which uses gestures, charts, musical notation and other audio-visual aids to help children understand arithmetic and mathematics. 
4. Established the École des Langues Vivantes, a school focused on teaching students English, French, Spanish, and Italian through the Gattegno method. 
5. Authored numerous books on mathematical instruction and theory, including Mathematics: The Language of Science and Visual Mathematics. 
6. Founded the Association Henri Wallon, dedicated to researching and promoting the study of mathematics education. 
7. Published several scientific papers related to mathematics and its impact on learning, such as "A Theoretical Analysis of the Efficiency of a Table System in Arithmetic Learning.”

Born 11 November 1911, Alexandria, Egypt. Died 28 July 1988, Paris, France.
