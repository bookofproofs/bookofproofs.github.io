**Shing-Tung Yau** is a Chinese mathematician who won a Fields Medal for his work on differential geometry and partial differential equations.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the Fields Medal in 1982 for his work on the Calabi conjecture. 
2. Received a Wolf Prize in Mathematics in 1990.
3. Awarded the Crafoord Prize in Mathematics in 1994.
4. Appointed as the William Caspar Graustein Professor of Mathematics at Harvard University in 1987.
5. Recipient of the United States National Medal of Science in 1997. 
6. Proved the Calabi-Yau conjecture, developing an important tool in string theory.
7. Published several monographs and research papers on Differential Geometry, Geometric Analysis and asymptotic theory. 
8. Authored the influential Cheng-Yau textbook in Mathematics.
9. Established the mathematics journal ‘Communications in Analysis and Geometry’ in 1993.
10. Received numerous honorary doctorates from renowned academic institutions.

Born 4 April 1949, Kwuntung (now Guangdong), China.
