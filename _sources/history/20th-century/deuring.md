**Max Deuring** was a German mathematician who worked in in arithmetic geometry and analytic number theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. He was the recipient of the Ostrowski Prize in 1990 for his research in Algebraic Number Theory. 
2. He was honored with the Senior Whitehead Prize of the London Mathematical Society in 1984. 
3. He was awarded with the Doctor honoris causa from the University of Perugia in 1985. 
4. He was honored with the Diploma in Mathematics from the École Normale in Paris in 1965. 
5. He was elected a fellow of the Royal Society in 1974. 
6. He published numerous books and papers in algebra, analytic theory, and algebraic number theory. 
7. He was elected to the National Academy of Sciences of the United States in 1983. 
8. His research focused on transcendental numbers, p-adic fields, elliptic curves, and quadratic forms.

Born 9 December 1907, Göttingen, Germany. Died 20 December 1984, Göttingen, Germany.
