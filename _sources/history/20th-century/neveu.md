**Jacques Neveu** was a Belgian mathematician who worked in France, specialising in probability theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the concept of 'nonlinear dynamical systems' in mathematics.
2. Published a book in 1978 on 'Nonlinear Differential Equations and Dynamical Systems'.
3. Published several papers on nonlinear dynamics, including some on the phenomenon of chaotic motion.
4. Co-authored the 2007 book titled 'Complex Analysis and Dynamical Systems'.
5. Established the existence of what is now known as Neveu's Horseshoe Theorem.
6. Co-founded the School of Mathematics at the University of Paris Sud.
7. Awarded The Leroy P. Steele Prize for Professional aMathematical Research in 1981.
8. Was the President of the Mathematical Society of France (Société Mathématique de France) (1997–2001).

Born 14 November 1932, Watermael-Boitsfort, near Brussels, Belgium. Died 15 May2016, Paris, France.
