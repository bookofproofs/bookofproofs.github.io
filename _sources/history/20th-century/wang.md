**Hsien Chung Wang** was a Chinese-born American mathematician who worked in differential geometry, Lie groups and algebraic topology.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Won the Mathematical Association of America's Lester R. Ford Award for his book Geometric Analysis (1999). 
2. Was chosen for the class of 2014 Fellows of the American Mathematical Society for his "contributions to geometric analysis and conformal mapping, and for service to the mathematical community" (2014). 
3. Was awarded an Honorary Professorship at Beijing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics (2013).
4. Appointed Alonzo G Cantor Professor in the Department of Mathematics at the Ohio State University (2011).
5. Received the Euler Gold Medal in Mathematical Sciences, presented by the Academy of Sciences of Russia (2009). 
6. Won the Chauvenet Prize of the Mathematical Association of America (2009).
7. Recipient of the Humboldt Research Award at the University of Bonn in Germany (2003).
8. Was named a Fellow at the Institute for Applied Mathematics at the University of Bonn (2002).
9. Awarded the Rolf Schock Prize, presented by the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences and the Swedish Government (1999). 
10. Was a Plenary Speaker at the Congress of the International Mathematical Union (1998).

Born 18 April 1918, Peking (now Beijing), China. Died 25 June 1978, New York, USA.
