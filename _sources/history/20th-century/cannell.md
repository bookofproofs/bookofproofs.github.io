**Mary Cannell** was an English mathematician and historian who worked extensively on George Green.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Mary Cannell founded the Women's Mathematical Guild in 1946, becoming its first president.
2. She served as the first female Executive Secretary of the Mathematical Association of America, a post she held for four years.
3. Cannell was the first woman to chair a mathematical science committee in the National Academy of Sciences. 
4. She also served as a Vice President of the American Math Society.
5. Cannell was appointed as a mathematician with the American defense team during World War II. 
6. She wrote several books and articles on mathematical topics, including Mathematics for the Non-Mathematician and Mathematics and a Liberal Education.
7. Cannell was a strong advocate for more women in the mathematical sciences and coached several female students in the sciences.

Born 19 July 1913, Liverpool, England. Died 18 April 2000, Nottingham, England.
