**Eižens Leimanis** was a Latvian mathematician who worked on the three-body problem.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a method for approximating solutions to the two-dimensional Helmholtz equation.
2. Developed a mathematical method for studying physical properties of fluids.
3. Devised a numerical analysis method for solving the Navier-Stokes equations of fluid dynamics.
4. Established the foundation for solving equations of mathematical physics.
5. Laid the groundwork for approximate numerical methods for solving linear differential equations.
6. Extended the boundary element method for solving boundary value problems in mathematical physics.
7. Proposed novel numerical techniques for studying systems of nonlinear differential equations.
8. Formulated an algorithm for solving elliptic partial differential equations.
9. Developed efficient numerical algorithms for solving system of equations.
10. Developed an adaptive mesh refinement technique for solving partial differential equations.

Born 10 April 1905, Vecbaizas (near Valmiera), Latvia. Died 4 December 1992, Vancouver, Canada.
