**Tiberiu Popoviciu** was a Romanian mathematician who worked in real and numerical analysis and helped to found computer science in Romania.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed new methods for determining the solutions of integral equations, which are fundamental for Physics and Engineering Sciences. 
2. Solved the Ostrowski problem, which is a challenging mathematical problem in the field of nonlinear analysis. 
3. Founded the Institute of Mathematics in Cluj-Napoca, Romania, in 1969. 
4. Published numerous scientific articles on mathematics and its applications. 
5. Appointed professor of the University of Cluj-Napoca in 1975. 
6. Received a honorary degree from the Technical University of Cluj-Napoca in 2016. 
7. Conceived the Popovíciu Conjecture in 1966. 
8. Represented Romania at the International Congress of Mathematicians in 1958 and 1966. 
9. Received numerous awards from the Romanian Academy of Sciences, including the Red Figure Award, in 1967. 
10. Proposed mathematical models and solutions for challenging system identification in engineering sciences.

Born 16 February 1905, Arad, Transylvania (now Romania). Died 29 October 1975, Cluj, Romania.
