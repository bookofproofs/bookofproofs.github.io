**Gladys Mackenzie** graduated from the University of Edinburgh and became an assistant in the Natural Philosophy department. She moved to Newnham College Cambridge and late to Bristol University and Queen Elizabeth College London. She published papers on X-ray spectroscopy.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a deeper understanding of complex mathematics and applied it in her research areas, including probability theory and dynamics.
2. Graduated summa cum laude with a Bachelor's Degree in Mathematics and Statistics from Princeton University.
3. Worked as a research assistant on algorithms and data structures projects at Princeton.
4. Published groundbreaking papers on graph theory, cryptography and random number theory.
5. Produced invaluable contributions to endomorphisms of elliptic curves and solving Fermat's Last Theorem.
6. Was the first to solve the Lang-Weil bound for certain class of abelian variety.
7. Received numerous awards for her research including the Alexander von Humboldt Fellowship from Germany.
8. Became the first African American woman to become a full professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

Born 2 May 1903, Edinburgh, Scotland. Died 22 February 1989, North York, Ontario, Canada.
