**Margaret Boyle** graduated from St Andrews and taught mathematics at Dalkeith High School.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. The first woman to be appointed to the National Research Council's Mathematics panel in 1926
2. Successfully promoted the National Committee of Mathematics in 1927, laying the foundations for many successful mathematics projects
3. Established a research institute dedicated to applied mathematics in 1928
4. Helped to create an International Mathematical Society in 1929
5. Contributed to the reorganization of the American Mathematical Society in 1945
6. Achieved the distinction of becoming the first woman to be appointed as a Fellow of the American Association for the Advancement of Science in 1948
7. Created the Biennial Conference on Applied Mathematics in 1950
