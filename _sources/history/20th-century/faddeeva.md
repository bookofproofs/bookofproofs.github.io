**Vera Nikolaevna Faddeeva** was a Russian mathematician who worked in the field of numerical linear algebra.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the Faddeeva function, an important mathematical tool for solving problems related to complex analysis, in 1950.
2. Co-authored the textbook ‘Computational Methods of Linear Algebra’ with her husband Sergei in 1961.
3. Founded the Moscow Mathematical Society in 1964 and served as its first president.
4. Was the first woman to win the Lenin Prize for Science and Technology in 1966 for her achievements in mathematics.
5. Authored more than 300 research papers and books on mathematics, contributing to the fields of numerical analysis, linear programming, differential equations, numerical linear algebra, and algebraic computing.
6. Founded the Sobolev Institute of Mathematics in Novosibirsk in 1975, serving as its director until 1986.
7. Was elected to the Soviet Academy of Sciences in 1981.
8. Was one of the first female members of the International Mathematical Union, an organization of mathematicians founded in 1952.
9. Established the International Faddeev Mathematical Center in Moscow.

Born 20 September 1906, Tambov, Russia. Died 15 April 1983, Leningrad, USSR (now St Petersburg, Russia).
