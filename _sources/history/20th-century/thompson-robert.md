**Robert C Thompson** was an Canadian mathematician who moved to the USA. He worked on linear algebra and matrix theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Worked as a professor of mathematics at the University of Washington, where he developed groundbreaking research in topology.
2. Authored several influential textbooks on linear algebra, including “Introduction to Linear Algebra” (1965) and “Elementary Linear Algebra” (1971).
3. Published numerous articles in prestigious mathematical journals.
4. Awarded the prestigious Sherman Fairchild Prize in Mathematics in 1992.
5. Received honorary degrees from Wake Forest University and Williams College.
6. Represented the United States at the International Congress of Mathematicians in 1966.
7. Elected to the National Academy of Sciences in 1977.
8. Nominated to the Mathematical Association of America's (MAA) Hall of Fame in 2001.

Born 21 April 1931, Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada. Died 10 December 1995, Santa Barbara, California, USA.
