**Jack Carr** was a Scottish mathematician who worked in differential equations and dynamical systems.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Authored the book 'Calculus for Dummies', which became a bestseller.
2. Developed an innovative approach for introducing calculus to students.
3. Established a summer program to teach mathematics to teenagers.
4. Applied interactive teaching methods to help students understand mathematics.
5. Regularly received awards at mathematics competitions for his students.
6. Mentored several students who went on to pursue successful careers in mathematics.
7. Discovered new concepts related to the mathematical field of integrals.
8. Implemented methods to simplify integration calculations.
9. Re-wrote numerous books on calculus and published multiple papers on the subject.
10. Actively promoted the use of mathematics in everyday life.

Born 29 August 1948, East Heddon, Northumberland, England. Died 14 July 2016, Edinburgh, Scotland.
