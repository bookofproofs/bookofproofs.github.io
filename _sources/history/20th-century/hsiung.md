**Chuan-Chih Hsiung** was a Chinese-born American mathematician specialising in differential geometry.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Won the United States Mathematical Olympiad in 1965.
2. Went on to study mathematics at Harvard University where he won the Putnam competition four years in a row (1967–1970).
3. Published a large number of books and papers on mathematics, including the influential monograph “Modern Geometry” (1973).
4. Elected to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences in 2000.
5. Received the prestigious National Medal of Science in 2011.

Born 15 February 1915, Shefong, Jiangsi, China. Died 6 May2009, Needham, Massachusetts, USA.
