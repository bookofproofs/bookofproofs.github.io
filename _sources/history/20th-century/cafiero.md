**Federico Cafiero** was an Italian mathematician who worked in real analysis, measure theory and ordinary differential equations.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Founding the Italian Mathematical Union (1955), a society that advances the progress of mathematics in Italy.
2. Initiating and contributing to the publication of important mathematical journals such as the Annali di Matematica Pura ed Applicata (1966) and Rendiconti del Circolo Matematico di Palermo (1884). 
3. Writing and editing several important mathematical books, such as L' Integration Permanente (1959). 
4. Bringing together many prominent mathematicians in Italy to collaborate on mathematical problems and discoveries. 
5. Establishing an award at the University of Turin to recognize the achievements of mathematicians (1982). 
6. Awarding the Riccardo Parodi Prize for outstanding contributions to mathematics in Italy (1983).
7. Promotion of the development of mathematics in secondary-school. 
8. Serving as President of the Circolo Matematico di Palermo from 1952 to 1966.

Born 24 May1914, Riposto, Catania, Sicily. Died 7 May1980, Naples, Italy.
