**Olga Ladyzhenskaya** was a Russian mathematician who worked on partial differential equations and finite-difference methods. 

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. She is best known for her contributions to the mathematical theory of partial differential equations and the study of fluid dynamics.
2. She was the first female professor at the Steklov Institute, who was appointed in 1953.
3. She was recognized as the foremost 20th-century female Russian mathematician, and was a key figure in the 20th-century development of mathematics.
4. She published 171 scientific works and was awarded Doctor of Science and Honored Scientist of the Russian Federation in 1979.
5. She set up the mathematical research school in Leningrad and its first Russian higher school program in numerical mathematics.
6. She received many honors and awards, including the Lomonosov Gold Medal in 1965.
7. She was honored as a Member of the Academy of Sciences of the Soviet Union in 1975.
8. She was the only female mathematician invited to the International Congress of Mathematicians in 1958, which was attended by over 1000 delegates from 54 countries.
9. She was made a full member of the Russian Academy of Sciences in 1989.
10. She became a member of the Italian Academy of Sciences in 1991.

Born 7 March 1922, Kologriv, Kostroma province, Russia. Died 12 January 2004, St Petersburg, Russia.
