**Friedrich Hirzebruch** was a German mathematician who made important contributions to topology.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Proved the Atiyah– Hirzebruch vanishing theorem in complex K-theory,
2. Developed the Hirzebruch–Riemann–Roch theorem,
3. Participated in the invention of topological K-theory,
4. Developed spectral sequence techniques,
5. Constructed the Hirzebruch surface,
6. Pioneered the study of toric varieties,
7. Introduced the signature theorem for quadratic forms,
8. Proved the Congruence Subgroup Theorem,
9. Demonstrated the Birch-Swinnerton-Dyer Formula,
10. Developed the famous “Hirzebruch Proportionality Principle”.

Born 17 October 1927, Hamm, Westphalia, Germany. Died 27 May2012, Bonn, Germany.
