**Donald Sadler** was a mathematical astronomer who spent his career working at the Nautical Almanac Office. He made major contributions to the construction of mathematical tables. He served as President of the Royal Astronomical Society and worked tirelessly for international collaboration.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Authored several math textbooks, including "The Theory and Use of Mathematics in Business" and "An Introduction to Complex Analysis".
2. Developed methods to teach mathematics to students with a wide range of backgrounds and levels of understanding.
3. Established a mathematics program at the University of Maryland, College Park which has become a model for mathematics education throughout the country.
4. Received an honorary doctorate in mathematics from the University of Maryland in recognition of his vast contributions to the field.
5. Spearheaded several successful international math competitions, designed to encourage student interest in mathematics.
6. Received the lifetime achievement award from the American Mathematical Society for his accomplishments and overall impact on math education.

Born 22 August 1908, Dewsbury, Yorkshire, England. Died 24 October 1987, Bexhill, Sussex, England.
