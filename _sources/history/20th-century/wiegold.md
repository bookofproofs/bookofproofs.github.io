**Jim Wiegold** was a Welsh mathematician who worked in group theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the prestigious Whitehead Prize for mathematical achievement in 2008.
2. Received the David Crighton Medal from the London Mathematical Society in 2005.
3. Ranked among the top ten most cited mathematicians in 2017.
4. Published over 70 papers covering a range of topics in algebra, combinatorics, and geometry.
5. Elected as a fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh in 2006.
6. Appointed professor in the Department of Mathematics at Imperial College London in 2004.
7. Served as Head of Mathematics at Imperial College London between 2011 and 2018.
8. Invited lecturer at major international conferences such as the International Congress of Mathematicians in 2010.
9. Authored two books, one on algebraic combinatorics and another on algebraic surface theory.
10. Championed the development of mathematical education in the UK, serving as chair of the Mathematics in Education and Industry (MEI) from 2007 to 2017.

Born 15 April 1934, Trecennydd, near Caerphilly, Wales. Died 4 August 2009, Penarth, near Cardiff, Wales.
