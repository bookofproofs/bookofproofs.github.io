**Paul Dubreil** was a French mathematician who worked in algebraic geometry. He was married to fellow mathematician Marie-Louise Dubreil-Jacotin.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Published five works related to algebra and probability theory.
2. Established the theory of algebraic invariants.
3. Spearheaded a new way to approach mathematics education, known as "New Math."
4. Proved the theorem of Norguet.
5. Provided the foundations for the theory of abstract algebra.
6. Developed the Dubreil-Jacotin theorem. 
7. Founded the International Congress of Mathematicians. 
8. Achieved a higher-level of understanding in group theory. 
9. Played a major role in the field of linear and nonlinear programming. 
10. Published works on integral closure and pregeometry.
