**Renfrey Potts** was an Australian mathematician who worked in operations research and network problems.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Appointed professor of applied mathematics at the University of Queensland in 1904.
2. Founded the first department of mathematics at the University of Queensland in 1907.
3. Established Australia's first graduate program in mathematics in 1909.
4. Became the first professor of mathematics at the University of Melbourne in 1922.
5. Founded the Australian Mathematical Society in 1924.
6. Established Australia's first mathematics journal, the Australian Mathematical Gazette, in 1926.
7. Served as the first editor of the Australian Mathematical Gazette from 1926-1931.
8. Co-founded the Mathematical Association of Victoria in 1929.
9. Authored numerous research papers on topics such as linear equations, integral equations, and calculus.
10. Awarded the Sylvester Medal of the Royal Society of London in 1932.

Born 4 October 1925, Adelaide, Australia. Died 9 August 2005, Adelaide, Australia.
