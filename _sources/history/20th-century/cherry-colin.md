**Colin Cherry** was an English scientist who worked on electric circuits, telecommunication principles and the psychology of speech and hearing.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Led the team that first identified and described the auditory process of shadowing in 1950.
2. Developed the Sperling partial report technique for studying working memory.
3. Published groundbreaking work on the cocktail party phenomenon in 1953.
4. Proposed a three-stage model of selective attention.
5. Pioneered the use of mathematical models in the study of cognition.
6. Invented the artificial reverberation chamber, used to study acoustic sciences.
7. Co-authored the influential book 'Coding Processes in Human Memory'.

Born 23 June 1914, St Albans, England. Died 23 November 1979, London, England.
