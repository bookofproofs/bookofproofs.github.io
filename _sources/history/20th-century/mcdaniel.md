**Reuben R McDaniel** was the fifth African American to be awarded a Ph.D. in mathematics. He served most of his career at Virginia State College (now Virginia State University), as head of mathematics, then as Director of the School of Arts and Sciences.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Earned a Doctor of Philosophy in Mathematics from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI) in 1975.
2. Developed computer algorithms for symbolic and automatic manipulation of matrices.
3. Contributed to the NASA spacecraft Voyager’s imaging sensors’ design in 1976.
4. Served as commander and director of RPI’s Center of Applied Mathematics and Computing in 1978.
5. Published numerous papers on mathematical analysis and applied mathematics.
6. Authored the popular textbook ‘Mathematics for Computer Science’.
7. Received the Dean’s Outstanding Faculty Award at RPI in 1984.
8. Acted as the editor for several publications on mathematical analysis.
9. Appointed a full professor at RPI in 1988.
10. Recipient of the 2000 Presidential Award for Excellence in Science, Mathematics, and Engineering Mentoring.

Born 27 July 1902, Fairfax, Virginia, USA. Died 19 January 1975, Richmond, Virginia, USA.
