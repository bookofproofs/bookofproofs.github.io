**Francis Allotey **was a mathematician and physicist from Ghana who was awarded a Ph.D. by Princeton University in 1966. He returned to Ghana where he spent his career as a professor of mathematics and did outstanding work promoting mathematics and physics at all levels throughout Africa.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. First African mathematician to be elected a Fellow of the Royal Society of London (1992).
2. Awarded the Alexander von Humboldt Prize for Research Achievements (1998).
3. Instrumental in founding the African Mathematics Millennium Science Initiative (2000).
4. Co-authored the first African Mathematics Textbook (2002).
5. Credited with helping Ghana earn its highest-ever position in the International Mathematical Olympiad (2011).
6. Established the Francis Allotey Institute of Mathematics at the University of Ghana (2013).
7. Established the West African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (2013).

Born 9 August 1932, Saltpond, Gold Coast (now Ghana). Died 2 November 2017, Accra, Ghana.
