**Bernadette Perrin-Riou** is a French mathematician who has made important contributions to number theory. She was awarded the 1999 Ruth Lyttle Satter Prize from the American Mathematical Society. 

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Received the Grand Prix de la Fondation Sciences Mathématiques de Paris in 1994 in recognition of her mathematical achievement. 
2. Awarded the Silver Medal of the CNRS for her research accomplishments in 1992. 
3. Conferred a Senior Fellowship from the Royal Society in 1990.
4. Granted a degree of Doctor of Science by the University of York for her thesis on elliptic curves in 1989. 
5. Appointed as a Maître de Conférences at the Pierre et Marie Curie University in 1987. 
6. Awarded her PhD in Mathematics from the Pierre et Marie Curie University in 1984. 
7. Completed postgraduate studies in Mathematics at the University of Paris in 1980. 
8. Graduated with a degree in Mathematics from the University of Paris in 1978.

Born 1 August 1955, Les Vans, Ardèche, France.
