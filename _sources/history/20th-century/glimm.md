**James Glimm** is an American mathematician who researched in both pure and applied mathematics and whose work has revolutionised shock-wave theory and other fields of study.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Led the development of mathematical theory to model complex physical systems, such as shock waves and turbulence.
2. Developed mathematical methods and software for portions of the U.S. Human Genome Project.
3. Pioneered the use of high-performance computers for numerical analysis of partial differential equations.
4. Received the National Medal of Science from President Jimmy Carter in 1980.
5. Founded the math department at the State University of New York at Stony Brook and served as its chairman for twenty-two years.
6. Received the American Mathematical Society's Steele Prize for Lifetime Achievement in 2009.
7. Authored over four hundred highly cited scientific articles in Partial Differential Equations, Applied Mathematics, and Computational Mathematics.
8. Served as President of the American Mathematical Society from 1983 to 1985.

Born 24 March 1934, Peoria, Illinois, USA.
