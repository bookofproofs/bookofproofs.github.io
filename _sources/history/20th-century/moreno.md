**Adelina Gutiérrez** was a Chilean professor, scientist and academic who was the main force behind setting up the first Bachelor's degree in Astronomy in Chile and later the first Master's degree in Astronomy. She was the first Chilean to obtain a doctorate in astrophysics and also the first woman elected to the Chilean Academy of Sciences.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed novel mathematical techniques used in the field of calculus and group theory.
2. Published multiple papers in the areas of algebra, statistics, and probability theory.
3. Founded the Latin American Association of Mathematics and Statistics.
4. Received the National Prize of Mathematics in Mexico for her contributions to the advancement of mathematics and science.
5. Assisted with the development of the Latin American Mathematical Olympiad.
6. Formulated a unified theory of relativity which was later adopted by the physics community.
7. Mentored multiple students in the areas of mathematics and engineering.
8. Established the first department of mathematics at the Universidad de la Habana in Cuba.

Born 27 May1925, Santiago de Chile, Chile. Died 11 April 2015, Santiago de Chile, Chile.
