**Schieffelin Claytor** was an American mathematician specialising in topology.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Published her first paper, titled “The correlation of Clustering Tendencies with other Variables in the Navy Mathematics Program” in the journal Mathematics Magazine in 1953.
2. Awarded a fellowship from the National Science Foundation in 1952 to further her research in algebra and number theory.
3. Awarded a Fulbright Fellowship for advanced study at Cambridge University in 1955. 
4. Received a doctorate in mathematics from Cambridge University in 1958.
5. Authored “Generalized Linear Congruences”, a textbook on number theory, in 1959.
6. Earned tenure in 1964, the first African-American mathematician to do so.
7. Published numerous research papers on algebra and number theory.
8. Founded the MAA's Committee on Minority Participation in Mathematics in 1971.

Born 4 January 1908, Norfolk, Virginia, USA. Died 14 July 1967, Washington, D.C., USA.
