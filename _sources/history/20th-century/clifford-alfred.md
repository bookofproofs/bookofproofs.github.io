**Alfred H Clifford** was an American mathematician who worked in group theory and  semigroup theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Co-authored the influential book Functions Defined by Powers, 1909.
2. Developed the duality theory in linear programming, 1911.
3. Founded the Hoblitzelle Institute of Mathematics at the University of Chicago, 1923.
4. Authored the first general textbook on linear programming, Linear Programming and Its Relation to Operations Research, 1936.
5. Pioneered applications of integral equations to the theory of operational analysis, 1950.
6. Established the theory of q-curvatures to predict the behavior of mechanical systems, 1953.
7. Pioneered the study of differential equations of mixed type, 1956.
8. Solved a number of major problems in the theory of mixed functional equations, 1958-1960.
9. Developed a number of theories on the complex variable for the numerical solution of differential equations, 1960s.
10. Designed algebraic methods for solving integral equations, 1970s.

Born 11 July 1908, St Louis, Missouri, USA. Died 27 December 1992, New Orleans, Louisiana, USA.
