**Ron Graham** was an American mathematician who  made important contributions to combinatorics. He collaborated extensively with Paul Erd≈ës.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Received the National Medal of Science in 1999 for his contributions to mathematics, computer science, and economics. 
2. Made fundamental contributions to discrete mathematics, including work on Ramsey theory, quasirandomness, computability, and computational complexity. 
3. Published more than 240 research papers in mathematics and computer science, and eleven books.
4. Founded the mathematics department at the University of California, San Diego.
5. Awarded the Leroy P. Steele Prize for Lifetime Achievement by the American Mathematical Society in 2015. 
6. Served as the President of the American Mathematical Society (1993-1994). 
7. Served as the Vice President of the International Mathematical Union from 1995 to 2002. 
8. Established the Algorithmic Decision Theory Group at Bell Labs in 1967. 
9. Received numerous honorary degrees and awards, including a Wolf Prize in Mathematics in 1992.

Born 31 October 1935, Taft, California, USA. Died 6 July 2020, La Jolla, California, USA.
