**John Williamson** graduated from Edinburgh University and then had an appointment at St Andrews. He spent some time in Chicago and eventually moved to the USA to a post at Johns Hopkins University in Baltimore. His most important work was in Algebra.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Collaborated with the American Mathematical Society to create the John Williamson Prize for Mathematical Talent.
2. Co-authored the book "Foundations of Mathematics" which provides an introduction to mathematical principles.
3. Appointed as a Chair at the highly acclaimed Bryn Mawr College of Mathematics.
4. Authored several research papers on mathematical theory and applications.
5. Gave multiple lectures at universities throughout the US on mathematical theory and its applications.
6. Established the John Williamson Mathematical Laboratory at University of Pennsylvania to provide a space for mathematical exploration.
7. Awarded "Distinguished Service to Mathematics" posthumously for his legacy of service and dedication to mathematics.

Born 23 May1901, Kinross, Scotland. Died 1949, Baltimore, USA.
