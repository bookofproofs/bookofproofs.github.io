**Lenore Blum** is an American mathematician who has made important advances in computer science.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Became the first woman to be appointed to a full professorship at the Carnegie Mellon University School of Computer Science in 1974.
2. Founded the Pittsburgh Supercomputing Center in 1984.
3. Promoted the development and use of computing technologies for research in mathematics and scientific computing.
4. Collaborated with colleagues on the development of technical software and algorithms for single-machine supercomputing and parallel computing.
5. Established the field of rigorous practical complexity theory in collaboration with Joe Blass, Michael Rabin and Manuel Blum.
6. Organized and led funding from the U.S. Government for the National Science Foundationâ€™s Algorithmic Foundations Program, which aimed to further theoretical research in the field of computer science.
7. Established the Mathematics and Computer Science Division (MACS) at the University of Pittsburgh, securing the stability and growth of both fields at the University.
8. Co-founded the Association for Women in Mathematics in 1971.
9. Chaired the Conference Board of the Mathematical Sciences from 1996 to 1998. 
10. Created the Lenore Blum Lectures in the Mathematical Sciences in 2008, featuring prominent female mathematicians.

Born 18 December 1942, New York, USA.
