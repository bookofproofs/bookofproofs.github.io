**Persi Diaconis** is an American mathematician and magician who works in combinatorics and statistics, but may be best known for his card tricks and other conjuring.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Persi Diaconis was awarded the Cole Prize in Number Theory from the American Mathematical Society in 1990. 
2. He served as the Honorary President of the International Congress of Mathematicians in 1994. 
3. His work on random matrices with Barry Simon won the AMS Frank and Brennie Morgan Prize in Mathematics in 1996. 
4. He was elected to the National Academy of Sciences in 1997. 
5. He received the National Medal of Science in 1998. 
6. In 1999, he was named a MacArthur Fellow. 
7. He received the Rolf Schock Prize in Mathematics in 2003. 
8. He was awarded the Leroy P. Steele Prize for Lifetime Achievement by the American Mathematical Society in 2013.

Born 31 January 1945, New York City, New York, USA.
