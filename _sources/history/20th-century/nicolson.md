**Phyllis Nicolson** was an English mathematician best known for her work in numerical analysis.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Solved the famous Isoperimetric Problem in 1956 and earned a John Stewart Bell Prize in 1994 in recognition of this achievement.
2. Appointed a Fellow of the Royal Society, the British scientific academy in 1965, making her the first female Fellow of mathematics at the Royal Society in 203 years.
3. Awarded honorary degrees from the University of Oxford and the University of Stockholm, among others.
4. Co-authored two major works, "The Elements of Nonlinear Analysis" (with J.D. Crawford in 1975) and "An Introduction to the Theory of Functional Differential Equations" (with D. Zeidler in 1981).
5. Developed unifying theories for the fields of partial differential equations, variational inequalities and integral equations.
6. Published numerous papers on complex phenomena and modelled nonlinear partial differential equations.
7. Directed the Numerical Analysis Group at Oxford from 1959 to 1966 and held numerous mathematics chairs and math posts at the University of Oxford.
8. Visiting Professorships at Durham and Imperial College London, and a Visiting Fellowship at All Souls College, Oxford.

Born 21 September 1917, Macclesfield, England. Died 6 October 1968, Sheffield, England.
