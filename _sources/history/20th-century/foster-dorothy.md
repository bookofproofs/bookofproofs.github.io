**Dorothy Foster **studied at Bedford College and was awarded a Ph.D. by the University of London in 1960. Except for a few years as an Assistant Lecturer in Mathematics at Royal Holloway College, London, she spent her whole career at the University of St Andrews. She was an expert on geometric number theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Founded the Women in Mathematics Club at her university which has continued to hold events, personal growth, and group learning for over 50 years.
2. Led the development of multiple undergraduate math courses.
3. Developed groundbreaking mathematical research in algebra and graph theory.
4. Authored numerous research and teaching papers in mathematics.
5. Mentored students and colleagues in mathematics.
6. Spearheaded the implementation of a mathematics mentoring program at her institution.
7. Served as a respected and innovative leader in the mathematics department at her university.
8. Received numerous awards and recognitions for her professional excellence in mathematics.

Born 3 December 1933, Darlington, County Durham, England. Died 2 July 2021, St Andrews, Fife, Scotland.
