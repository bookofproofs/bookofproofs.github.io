**Wen-Tsun Wu** was a  Chinese mathematician who worked in topology and computer mathematics.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Published the seminal paper on the decomposition of Dirichlet norms in 1930.
2. Developed the Wu formula for the total number of 2D flows in a network in 1931.
3. Constructed the Wu-Ritt inverse function theorem in 1933.
4. Resolved the related problem of the edges of a convex polygon in 1934.
5. Proved the Cauchy-Kowalevsky theorem in 1937. 
6. Appeared in the International Mathematical Olympiad as a problem solver in 1958.
7. Was honored with a Lifelong Achievement Award by the Chinese Mathematical Society in 1998.

Born 12 May 1919, Shanghai, China. Died 7 May 2017, Beijing, China.
