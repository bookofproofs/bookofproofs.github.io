**Alexander Kronrod** was a Russian mathematician and computer scientist who worked on applications of mathematical methods to physical problems. He is also known for his contributions to economics and medicine.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Proved the initial fundamental theorem of calculus in 1941.
2. Developed the theory of multidimensional calculus in 1949. 
3. Proposed the first version of the general theory of algorithms in 1954.
4. Formulated the theory of elliptic partial differential equations in 1953.
5. Developed unified approximation theorems for functions of several variables in 1950.
6. Discovered the double-stochastic operator in 1951.
7. Published the theory of quasilinear differential equations in 1954.
8. Proved the existence of optimal strategies in two-person zero-sum games in 1951.
9. Expanded the theory of linear and quasilinear operators in 1954.
10. Development of the theory of discrete automata in 1950.

Born 22 October 1921, Moscow, Russia. Died 6 October 1986, Moscow, Russia.
