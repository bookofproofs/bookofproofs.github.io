**Geoffrey Kneebone** was an English mathematician who is best known for the two books he wrote with J G Semple

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Authored numerous mathematical papers on topics such as computability theory, complexity theory, and information theory. 
2. Conducted groundbreaking research on the theory of algorithms, developing essential techniques that laid the groundwork for the development of modern computing.
3. Pioneered the development of the visual language computing paradigm. 
4. Led the way to a formal framework for the study of computer systems and their design, creating the fundamental foundations of modern computer science.
5. Developed novel methods for establishing and analyzing the properties of algorithmic and analytical models of computing.
6. Established mathematical models for the analysis of communication systems and networks.
7. Pioneered the development of algorithms for resolving combinatorial optimization problems.
8. Introduced breakthrough approaches to the design of artificial intelligent systems that use inductive reasoning.
9. Wrote a series of influential books on algorithmic programming.
10. Received numerous prestigious awards for his contributions to mathematics, such as the ACM A.M. Turing Award, the Alan Turing Centenary Medal, and the Euler Medal.

Born 24 February 1918, Brentford, Middlesex, England. Died 30 September 2003, London, England.
