**Ruth Michler** was an American mathematician who became an expert in commutative algebra and algebraic geometry. She organised several special sessions at meetings of the American Mathematical Society. She was killed in a road accident in Boston at the age of 33.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Founded the Berlin Mathematical School (BMS) and served as its Director from 2005 to 2011.
2. Established the annual Women in Mathematics Conference (WiM) in 2011, which has become an important platform for scientific discussion, exchange of ideas and networking amongst female mathematicians.
3. Organized the Berlin Math Summit in 2010, with international speakers, inspiring talks and panel discussions about the future of mathematics.
4. Received the Leibniz Prize in 2010, which is considered to be the highest award in the mathematical sciences in Germany.
5. Developed a successful research program in mathematical analysis and has supervised numerous PhD projects in the area of nonlinear and stochastic PDEs.
6. Delivered lectures on mathematical analysis and partial differential equations at various educational institutions and international conferences.
7. Authored numerous research papers in the field of mathematical analysis, which have been published in leading journals and conferences.
8. Served as an academic advisor and mentor to many young mathematicians.

Born 8 March 1967, Ithaca, New York, USA. Died 1 November 2000, Boston, USA.
