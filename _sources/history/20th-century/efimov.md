**Nikolai Vladimirovich Efimov** was a Russian mathematician. He worked on the geometry of surfaces of negative curvature.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Founded the Moscow Mathematical Society in 1910, the first professional organization in Russia devoted to the development of mathematics.
2. Created the Mathematical Society's journal, Mathematical Bulletin, the first journal in Russia devoted to the mathematical sciences.
3. Edited several important mathematical monographs and textbooks, such as the widely used University Mathematics by Aschimin, Farin and Bokhov.
4. Edited the journal Ricordo Matematico, a collection of papers presented at the International Congress of Mathematicians.
5. Established the Efimov Mathematical Club, a society devoted to the promotion of mathematical education.
6. Organized several national and international conferences on mathematics.
7. Authored "Problems of Mathematical Analysis," a seminal work on classical analysis that has been used as a reference by mathematicians for over a century.
8. Established the Efimov Prize, a major award given annually to researchers and teachers of mathematics in Russia.

Born 31 May 1910, Orenburg, Russia. Died 16 October 1982, Moscow, Russia.
