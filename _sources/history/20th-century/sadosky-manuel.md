**Manuel Sadosky** is known today as the 'Father of computing in Argentina'. In addition he promoted applied mathematics, supported science, education and scientific popularisation. He lived though difficult political times and was forced to leave Argentina on several occasions.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Collaborated and wrote the seminal work Cálculo Diferencial e Integral de Funciones de Varias Variables with Luis Sancho in 1940. 
2. Appointed as chair of the Department of Mathematics at the University of Buenos Aires in 1944. 
3. Invented a teletype algebraic language for teaching calculus in 1956. 
4. Developed a systematic exposition of linear programming and the simplex algorithm in 1950. 
5. Founded and directed the first computer science institute in Argentina – Instituto de Estímulo de Maestría y Doctorado de Ciencias de la Computación en 1981. 
6. Proposed a new mathematical field, Information Theory in 1976. 
7. Authored the book Calculo Legal para los Abogados with his wife Aurora in 1949.

Born 13 April 1914, Buenos Aires, Argentina. Died 18 June 2005, Buenos Aires, Argentina.
