**Aldo Andreotti** was an Italian mathematician who worked on algebraic geometry, on the theory of functions of several complex variables and on partial differential operators.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the Andreotti-Mayer-Looijenga Theorem for complex algebraic geometry in 1975.
2. Established the Andreotti-Frankel Theorem for analytic cycles in 1968.
3. One of the founders of modern algebraic geometry and the motivic viewpoint.
4. Developed fundamental concepts in constructive algebraic geometry.
5. Won the Lesley Prize for his work in mathematical research in 1995.
6. Contributed to the development of transcendental methods in algebraic geometry.
7. Published over 80 articles and four books on algebraic geometry and other related topics.
8. Awarded the Italian Prize “Valentino de Angelis 2000” for his achievements in mathematics.
9. Served on the editorial boards of several prestigious mathematical journals.
10. Received honorary doctorates from Universities of Nancy and Lausanne.

Born 15 March 1924, Florence, Italy. Died 21 February 1980, Pisa, Italy.
