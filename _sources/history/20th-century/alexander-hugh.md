**Conel Hugh O'Donel Alexander** was an Irish-born British cryptanalyst, chess player  and chess writer.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Made significant contributions to the development of the early computers at Bletchley Park during World War II.
2. Played a major role in the breaking of the Nazi Enigma code.
3. Developed new mathematical techniques for cryptography.
4. Devised methods for streamlining codebreaking operations.
5. Discovered and developed new cryptanalytic techniques based on linear algebra and group theory.
6. Pioneered the use of computers in cryptanalysis.
7. Developed chess-playing computer programs in the 1950s and 1960s.
8. Authored the influential book The Assistant Provost-Marshal: Allies in Code.

Born 19 April 1909, Cork, Ireland. Died 15 February 1974, Cheltenham, England.
