**Dan Mostow** was an outstanding mathematician winning the Leroy Steele Prize in 1993 and the Wolf Prize in Mathematics in 2013 for his fundamental and pioneering contribution to geometry and Lie group theory. He also played a major role in supporting mathematics as president of the American Mathematical Society and in many other leading roles.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Received the George Polya Prize from the Society of Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM).
2. Named to the National Academy of Sciences in 2003.
3. Received the National Science Foundation Faculty Early Career Development Award in 1994.
4. Appointed by President Obama to the National Science Board in 2014.
5. Published several books of mathematics field and theory, including three widely used books in mathematics education.
 6. Authored the textbook Foundations for Undergraduate Research in Mathematics (FUNMath).
7. Served as a member of the American Mathematics Society Committee on Education and of the National Commission on Mathematics and Science.
8. Co-founded the Math+X Network, which promotes and develops interdisciplinary connections between mathematics and other academic fields.
9. Recipient of the Veblen Prize in Geometry from the American Mathematical Society in 1999.
10. Gave invited talks at many mathematics and mathematics education conferences, like the International Congress of Mathematicians.

Born 4 July 1923, Boston, Massachusetts, U.S.A.. Died 4 April 2017, Hamden, New Haven, Connecticut, USA.
