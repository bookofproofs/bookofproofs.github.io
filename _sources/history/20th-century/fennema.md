**Elizabeth Fennema** was an American mathematician who worked on mathematical education. She received many honours such as the first Annual Award for Outstanding Contribution to Research on Women and Education from the American Educational Research Association, and the Dora Helen Skypek Award from the Association for Women and Mathematics Education.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Co-authored the seminal book on mathematically based teaching called Mathematics Teaching in the Middle School.
2. Developed and implemented a comprehensive Teacher Enhancement Program for mathematics instruction in elementary and middle schools.
3. Contributed to numerous research studies on the effectiveness of mathematics reform in elementary and middle schools.
4. Taught mathematics and mathematics education classes at the university level. 
5. Acted as a national leader in efforts to improve mathematics instruction in schools.
6. Selected as a Fellow of the National Academies of Education.
7. Served on the National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP) Advisory Committee.
8. Made significant contributions to mathematics education policy documents.
9. Received the Lifetime Achievement Award from the National Council of Teachers of Mathematics in 2006. 
10. Developed the Cognitively-Guided Instructional Model for teaching mathematics in elementary grades.

Born 8 April 1928, Winfield, Kansas, USA. Died 20 December 2021, Madison, Wisconsin, USA.
