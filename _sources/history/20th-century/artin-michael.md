**Michael Artin** is an American mathematician known for his contributions to algebraic geometry.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Became a professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1975.
2. Won the Leroy P. Steele Prize for Mathematical Exposition from the American Mathematical Society in 1992.
3. Received the National Medal of Science from US President Barack Obama in 2013.
4. Published his influential book “Algebra” in 1991.
5. Became a Gordon MacKay lecturer at Harvard University in 2012.
6. Earned a Fields Medal in 1974 for his exemplary work in Algebraic Geometry.
7. Awarded honorary doctorates from Brandeis University, the University of Chicago, and the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign.
8. Received a Wolf Prize in Mathematics in 2017. 
9. Presented the Cantor Lecture at the Johns Hopkins University in 2012.
10. Worked as the Director of the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in Bonn, Germany.

Born 28 June 1934, Hamburg, Germany.
