**Rajeev Motwani** was an Indian-born American computer scientist whose research focused on theoretical computer science and is known for his contribution to the Google search engine.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Rajeev Motwani was a renowned mathematics professor at Stanford University, where he served for nearly two decades.
2. He was a key contributor to the development of streaming algorithms and pursuing research in randomized algorithms, distributed computing, and data privacy.
3. He led development of the Cousin-Linderstam randomized algorithm, as well as the local search algorithm for the travelling salesperson problem.
4. He also made valuable contributions to learning theory, game theory, and computational geometry.
5. He was a pioneer of the SUM-PRODUCT algorithm, which was used to solve algebraic systems.
6. His work in optimisation of the Hidden Markov Network has been widely cited in Machine Learning research.

Born 26 March 1962, Jammu, Jammu and Kashmir, India. Died 5 June 2009, Atherton, San Mateo County, California, USA.
