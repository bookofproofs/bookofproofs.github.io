**Irving Segal** was an American mathematician known for work on theoretical quantum mechanics.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Was the founding chair of the mathematics department at Brandeis University in 1952, and served in that role for 20 years.
2. He made contributions to the fields of functional analysis and differential equations.
3. He was awarded a National Science Foundation Fellowship.
4. Co-authored the book “Mathematical Analysis: An Introduction to Functions of Several Variables” in 1970.
5. Served as chair of the editorial board of Mathematical Reviews.
6. Established the Segal Prize in Functional Analysis, Differential Equations and Related Areas, an International Prize endowed by Irving Segal and his wife Elizabeth, to recognize contributions to research or teaching in his field. 
7. Awarded numerous honorary doctorates from prominent universities, including Harvard University and the Weizmann Institute of Science.

Born 13 September 1918, Bronx, New York, USA. Died 30 August 1998, Lexington, Middlesex, Massachusetts, USA.
