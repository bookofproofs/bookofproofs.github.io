**Florence Nightingale David** was an English statistician who worked to increase the opportunities for women to study statistics. She became head of the Statistics Department at the University of California, Riverside. Among her research interests were the history of probability and of statistical ideas.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Pioneered the use of statistical information in health care, using tables and diagrams to demonstrate clear links between causes and effects of diseases such as cholera, typhoid and dysentery.
2. Established the concepts of standardization and normalization, which could be applied to track and calculate average mortality rates.
3. Created a structured and organized method of gathering and analyzing vital health data which later became known as the “Nightingale Method.”
4. Developed the first accurately represented polar area diagram, which was an early form of the pie chart.
5. Applying statistical evidence, she reduced the death rate from 42% to 2% among her patients.

Born 23 August 1909, Ivington, near Leominster, England. Died 23 July 1993, Kensington, Contra Costa County, California, USA.
