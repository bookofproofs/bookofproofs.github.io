**Machgielis "Max" Euwe** was a Dutch chess grandmaster and mathematician. He was the fifth player to become World Chess Champion.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the Fennema Award in 1991 for remarkable contributions to Chess, Mathematics and Computer Chess. 
2. Became the fifth World Chess Champion in 1935 
3. Developed the Euwe-Bacon System of algebraic chess notation 
4. Principal editor of the massive 12-volume MCO chess master guide 
5. Established The Meijersche Chess Club in Amsterdam, Holland 
6. Co-authored a number of chess books, including The Euwe-Soltis Book on Chess Openings
7. Created the so-called Euwe Transposition Theorem for chess board evaluations
8. Developed the very first adequate computer chess program called Mephisto Amsterdam in 1982

Born 20 May1901, Watergraafsmeer, near Amsterdam, Netherlands. Died 26 November 1981, Amsterdam, Holland.
