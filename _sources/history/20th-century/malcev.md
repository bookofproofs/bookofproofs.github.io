**Anatoly Malcev** was a Russian mathematician noted for his work on the decidability of various algebraic structures.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Co-authored the influential text Linear Algebra in 1955.
2. Authored the book Theory of Algorithms, which had a major influence in the development of computer science.
3. Developed the dual space theory and the representation theory of groups.
4. Contributed to the calculus of variations.
5. Solved the problem of linear programming in highly structured setting.
6. Proved the celebrated Burnside theorem for the theory of finite groups.
7. Developed the spectrum of a ring theory and the theory of modules.
8. Established the theory of algebraic actions.
9. Pioneered the development of the theory of generalized algebra.
10. Made powerful contributions in the field of topology.

Born 27 November 1909, Misheronsky (near Moscow), Russia. Died 7 July 1967, Novosibirsk, USSR.
