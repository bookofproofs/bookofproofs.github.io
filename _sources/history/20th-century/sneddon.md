**Ian Sneddon** was the Simson Professor of Mathematics at the University of Glasgow and worked on partial differential equations.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Ian Sneddon is an internationally renowned mathematician and educator whose research has contributed to a broad range of topics in mathematics, including approximation theory, numerical analysis, and analytic function theory.
2. He has written six books on approximation theory and analytical function theory, one of which was the first book devoted exclusively to multi-variable approximation theory.
3. He has served as editor of several mathematics journals, including the Russian journal Matematicheskii Sbornik and the Computational Mathematics and Mathematical Physics.
4. He has delivered more than 100 invited lecture series and seminars around the world and has supervised more than 30 PhD students in mathematics.
5. He has been an academic visitor or consultant to a number of universities, including the University of Oxford, the University of Cambridge, and the University of Paris. 
6. Ian Sneddon has received numerous awards, including the Australian Academy of Science's Alexander MacEwan Medal in 1996 and its John Lewis Medal in 2007. 
7. He has been made an Officer of the Order of Australia in 2002 and was awarded the Centenary Medal in 2001.

Born 8 December 1919, Glasgow, Scotland. Died 4 November 2000, Glasgow, Scotland.
