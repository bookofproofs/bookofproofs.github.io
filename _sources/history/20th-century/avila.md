**Artur Ávila** is a Brazilian mathematician who has made deep contributions to dynamical systems theory which has transformed the subject. His work has been honoured with numerous prizes including a Fields Medal in 2014.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the Fields Medal in 2014 in recognition of his work on dynamical systems and ergodic theory. 
2. Won the 2012 Fermat Prize for his acclaimed work on hyperbolic dynamical systems. 
3. Became a Plenary Speaker of the International Congress of Mathematicians in 2014.
4. Awarded the 2010 Clay Research Award for his work on dynamical systems and the geometry of the space of their orbits.
5. Founding Member of the Cláudio Landim Brazilian Academy of Mathematics. 
6. Involved with the Clay Mathematics Institute in their resolution of the Collatz problem.
7. Published papers in Poincaré recurrence and geometry, as well as ergodic theory and cyclic generation of dynamical systems.
8. Receiving the Oswaldo Cruz Foundation award in 2002 for his dissertation on attractors and invariant measures.

Born 29 June 1979, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
