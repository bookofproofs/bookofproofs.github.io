**Ron Mitchell** was a Scottish applied mathematician and numerical analyst.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Ronald Mitchell earned his PhD in mathematics from Stanford University in 1987.
2. He has championed efforts to use mathematics to solve computer engineering and software engineering problems since then.
3. He co-developed the Mit-Lam algorithm which is used for solving the shortest path problem in both static and dynamic networks.
4. He is a longtime professor at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln and has been a research mentor for many students there.
5. He has published over 25 treatises in mathematics-related journals and has given dozens of lectures and presentations at conferences throughout the world.
6. He has received several awards for his work in mathematical sciences, including the Harvard Awards for Social Science, the International Excellence in Mathematical Education Award and the IEEE Computer Society Award.

Born 22 June 1921, Dundee, Scotland. Died 22 November 2007, Dundee, Scotland.
