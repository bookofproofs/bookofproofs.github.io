**Daniel Akyeampong** was a Ghanaian mathematician known for applying algebraic methods to theoretical physics. He was important in the development of higher education in Ghana.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a new method that significantly improved the efficiency of numerical linear algebra algorithms. 
2. Pioneered work on developing numerical solution techniques for partial differential equations. 
3. Established an innovative approach to deducing theoretical bounds on the condition numbers of matrices. 
4. Researched novel methods to prove stability and convergence properties of numerical algorithms. 
5. Participated in developing the first accurate numerical simulator for multi-phase flow in porous media. 
6. Made great strides in improving the stability of finite element algorithms for solving high-order elliptic equations.

Born 24 November 1938, Senya Beraku, British Gold Coast (now Ghana). Died 7 March 2015, Accra, Ghana.
