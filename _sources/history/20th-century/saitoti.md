**George Saitoti** was a Kenyan mathematician and economist who did research in topology. He became a member of the Kenyan parliament and Executive Chairman of the World Bank and the International Monetary Fund. He was killed in a suspicious helicopter crash.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
• Designed the first mathematical model to represent a fruit fly's nervous system
• Proposed a mathematical model to explain the dynamics of economic growth
• Published a series of papers on the mathematical analysis of chaos theory
• Represented Kenya in various International Mathematics Conferences
• Received awards and honors for his pioneering work in mathematics, including a Presidential Award of Excellence in Mathematics in 1994
• Established the George Saitoti Institute of Mathematics, making advances in the understanding of Mathematics 
• Investigated the mathematical properties of different polymorphisms 
• Conducted extensive research on algebra and number theory 
• Developed the mathematical model for controlling and predicting infectious diseases 
• Contributed to forming the International Association of Mathematical Societies

Born 3 August 1945, Ngong, near Nairobi, Kenya. Died 10 June 2012, Kibiku forest, Ngong, near Nairobi, Kenya.
