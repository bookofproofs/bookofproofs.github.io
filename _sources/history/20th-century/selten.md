**Reinhard Selten** was a Polish mathematician and economist who shared the 1994 Nobel Prize in Economics with John Nash for his work on game theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Member of the honor committee for the International Congress of Mathematicians in 1986.
2. Awarded the Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences in 1994.
3. Gained an honorary degree from the University of Cologne in 1995.
4. Received the Institute for Operations Research and the Management Sciences (INFORMS) John von Neumann Theory Prize in 1985.
5. Received the 2005 Deutsche Bank Prize in Financial Economics.
6. Awarded the Forth International Prize in Economics in 2002.
7. Received the Gossen Prize in 1974.
8. Developed game theory into "bounded rationality" in 1975.
9. Published a fundamental treatise on game theory and the mathematics of economic behavior in the year 2000.
10. Received the Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany in 1995.

Born 5 October 1930, Breslau, Germany (now Wrocław, Poland). Died 23 August 2016, Poznań, Poland.
