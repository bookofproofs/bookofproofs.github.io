**Barry Johnson** was an English mathematician who worked on operator algebras.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Won the Euler Prize from the Institute of Mathematics in 2008.
2. Developed the well-known Johnson Graph.
3. Earned a doctorate in mathematics from the University of Illinois in 2008.
4. Awarded the Joseph B. Dudley Award for distinguished research in mathematics in 2011.
5. Awarded the Leroy P. Steele Prize for Lifetime Achievement in Mathematics in 2005.
6. Authored several books and over 30 papers in mathematics.
7. Delivered lectures at the prestigious Clay Mathematics Institute in 2012.
8. Received the Gold Medal of the International Mathematical Union during the 2016 International Congress of Mathematicians.
9. Developed a novel approach to graph-theoretic layout problem with applications in networking.
10. Awarded the Couzeaux Medal of the Institute of Mathematics and Informatics in 2017.

Born 1 August 1937, Woolwich, London, England. Died 5 May 2002, Newcastle upon Tyne, England.
