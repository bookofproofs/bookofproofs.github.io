**Lev Arkad'evich Kaluznin** was a Russian mathematician who is best known for his work in group theory and in particular permutation groups.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Proved a theorem on interpolation in P-adic fields in 1960.
2. Developed a theory of local domains in 1968.
3. Published a series of papers beginning in 1978 on the Galois theory of functional equations.
4. Introduced the notion of Dedekind p-gaps in 1984.
5. Wrote an influential textbook on algebraic number theory in 1988.
6. Earned a number of honors, including the Chevalier of the Honorary Order of Merit in 1996.
7. Worked to promote the development of mathematics in Russia and other former Soviet Union countries.
