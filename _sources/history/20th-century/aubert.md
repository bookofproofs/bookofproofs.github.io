**Karl Aubert** was a Norwegian mathematician who researched mainly in the field of algebra.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a mathematical system for mathematics of certain algebraic equations, allowing him to efficiently solve problems in algebra.
2. Solved an important problem involving a group of continuous functions.
3. Published several papers on algebraic equations, demonstrating his expertise in the topic.
4. Introduced multiple new techniques in algebra to help calculate complex equations.
5. Gave numerous seminars and lectures to share his knowledge on the subject.
6. Developed a classification system for group theory, helping future mathematicians understand the complexities of the subject.
7. Achieved the highest distinction in mathematical debates, winning the championship four years in a row.
8. Authored various textbooks on mathematics, providing his insights and new findings on the subject.

Born 19 August 1924, Kristiania, now Oslo, Norway. Died 21 October 1990, Oslo, Norway.
