**David Mumford** is an English-born American mathematician who won a Fields Medal for his work on algebraic geometry.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Won the Fields Medal in 1974 for his work on algebraic geometry. 
2. Received the Wolf Prize in Mathematics in 2015. 
3. Delivered the plenary address at the International Congress of Mathematicians in Beijing, China in 2002.
4. Became a fellow of the Royal Society in 1995.
5. Led the development and launching of the Digital Archive of Mathematical Papers in 2002. 
6. Awarded the National Medal of Science in 2006.
7. Received the Steele Prize in 1995 for his work in algebraic geometry. 
8. Named a Corresponding Member of the Academy of Sciences of France in 2006.

Born 11 June 1937, Worth, Sussex, England.
