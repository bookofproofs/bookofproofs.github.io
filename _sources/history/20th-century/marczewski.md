**Edward Marczewski** was a Polish mathematician who worked in measure theory, descriptive set theory, general topology, probability theory and universal algebra.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the concept of relative homology using algebraic topology in the 1930s.
2. Developed the theory of relative homotopy groups in the 1930s.
3. Wrote the book Homology and Cohomology in 1935.
4. Developed the technique of homotopy classification in 1938.
5. Proved the existence of a special homotopic map in 1940.
6. Established the category of relative homology in 1943.
7. Published the book Homotopy Theory in 1966.
8. Debuted the concept of relative higher homotopy groups in 1967.
9. Pioneered the theoretical application of relative homology to algebraic surfaces in 1968.

Born 15 November 1907, Warsaw, Russian Empire (now Poland). Died 17 October 1976, Wroc≈Çaw, Poland.
