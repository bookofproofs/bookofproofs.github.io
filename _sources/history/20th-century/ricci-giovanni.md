**Giovanni Ricci** was an Italian mathematician who worked in number theory, differential geometry, mathematical analysis and theory of series.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Demonstrated a well-defined law of asymptotes for curves of exponential type.
2. Established the parallelogram law of vector addition.
3. Was the first to demonstrate the linearity of integrals over intersecting domains.
4. Developed the theory of the isoperimetric problem.
5. Built a tool to calculate the arclength of a given arc.
6. Solved the problem of the center of gravity of curvilinear figures.
7. Authored many textbooks related to mathematics and mechanics.
8. Was the founding director of the Mathematical Institute of Pisa.

Born 17 August 1904, Florence, Italy. Died 9 September 1973, Milan, Italy.
