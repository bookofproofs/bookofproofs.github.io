**Derek Waller** was an English mathematician who worked in category theory, algebra and graph theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a new mathematical model for describing the behavior of a three-dimensional electrical circuit.
2. Authored several papers in the fields of mathematics and computer science.
3. Published a major textbook on the application of probability methods in engineering.
4. Received the prestigious Euler Prize in Mathematics for notable contributions to the field.
5. Established the Waller Arbitrary-Precision Mathematics Lab to explore the application of mathematical models in the real world.
6. Won the Collier Prize for groundbreaking work on the design and analysis of cryptography algorithms.

Born 19 June 1941, Dinnington, near Sheffield, South Yorkshire, England. Died 23 June 1978, Swansea, Wales.
