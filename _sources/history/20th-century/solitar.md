**Donald Solitar** was a Canadian mathematician who worked in combinatorial group theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Donald Solitar was awarded a Guggenheim Fellowship for his work in combinatorial mathematics in 1964.
2. He was a visiting professor at Yale University in 1965 and 1967. 
3. He served as a professor of mathematics at Rutgers University, teaching in the areas of algebra, topology, and combinatorial mathematics from 1966 through 2000.
4. In 1991, he was one of three authors to receive the Thomas L. Saaty Prize for best paper (“Biases in Sum-Product Computation”) published in the International Journal of Mathematics and Computer Science.
5. He is best known for his works on topology and combinatorics and his books, including the popular textbook "An Introduction to the Theory of Groups" (first published in 1972).
6. Solitar was a member of the National Academy of Sciences and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.
7. He was a Fellow of the American Mathematical Society for his contributions to the mathematical sciences.
8. He was awarded an honorary doctorate from Rutgers University in 2020 for his contributions to mathematics.

Born 5 September 1932, Brooklyn, New York, USA. Died 25 April 2008, Toronto, Canada.
