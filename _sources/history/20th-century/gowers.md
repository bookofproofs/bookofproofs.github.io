**Timothy Gowers** won a Fields medal for his important contributions to functional analysis.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the Fields Medal (the highest honor in mathematics) in 1998 for his work in functional analysis, combinatorics, and number theory.
2. Awarded the Copley Medal (the oldest scientific award in the world) in 2016 for his achievements in mathematics.
3. Named a Fellow of the Royal Society in 1996 for his work in mathematics.
4. Elected a Foreign Member of the American National Academy of Sciences in 2006.
5. Named a Fellow of the American Mathematical Society in 2012.
6. Awarded the Abel Prize in 2012 for his work in the area of mathematics of additive combinatorics.
7. Received the Rolf Schock Prize in 2009.

Born 20 November 1963, Marlborough, Wiltshire, England.
