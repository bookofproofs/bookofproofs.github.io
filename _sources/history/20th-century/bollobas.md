**Professor Bollobás** is a Hungarian-born British mathematician who is one of the world's leaders in combinatorics.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded a scholarship from the Hungarian Academy of Sciences to pursue his PhD at Eötvös Loránd University.
2. Appointed professor at the University of Memphis in 1977 and University of Cambridge in 1985. 
3. Authored several influential books on graph theory, including "Modern Graph Theory" and "Extremal Graph Theory".
4. Made seminal contributions to random graph theory, matrix theory, and combinatorics.
5. Received several awards for his work, including the Pólya Prize (1997) and the London Mathematical Society's Senior Whitehead Prize (1998).
6. Named a honorary Fellow of the London Mathematical Society in 2011. 
7. Appointed fellow of the Royal Society in 2015.

Born 3 August 1943, Budapest, Hungary.
