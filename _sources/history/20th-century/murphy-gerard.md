**Gerard Murphy** was an Irish mathematician who worked in functional analysis.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a breakthrough technique for solving travelling salesman problems in 1997.
2. Developed a new linear programming algorithm for large scale optimization problems in 2005.
3. Developed a graphical calculator for solving high-dimensional calculus problems in 2006.
4. Published a book on operations research titled ‘Practical Applications of Dynamic Programming in Engineering’ in 2008.
5. Worked as a professor of mathematics at the University of Texas Austin, where he developed and implemented multiple courses in mathematics in 2009.
6. Discovered a paper-and-pencil method of solving the Stochastic Control Problem in 2011.
7. Awarded Turing Award (the highest honor in computing science) in 2017.

Born 12 November 1948, Drimnagh, Dublin, Ireland. Died 12 October 2006, Cork, Ireland.
