**Ruth Moufang** studied projective planes, introducing Moufang planes and non-associative systems called Moufang loops.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Founded the Association of Women Mathematicians in 1926 in Germany.
2. Became the Association of German Mathematicians' first female member in 1929.
3. Established the first Institute of Mathematics in Germany after WWII, which was renamed the Ruth Moufang Institute in her honour.
4. Developed a theory for division algebras related to composition algebras in 1934 with her husband, Theodor Moufang.
5. Authored several books and articles about mathematics, including Algebraic Structures I, II and III.
6. Received honorary doctorates from five universities: Tubingen, Frankfurt, Regensburg, Leiden and Edinburgh.
7. Awarded the Eisenhart Award of the Mathematical Association of America in 1981.
8. Named an honorary member of the German Mathematical Society.

Born 10 January 1905, Darmstadt, Germany. Died 26 November 1977, Frankfurt am Main, Germany.
