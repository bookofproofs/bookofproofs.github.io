**Marion Gray** was a Scottish mathematician who went to Bryn Mawr College in the USA. The Gray graph is named after her.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded a National Science Foundation (NSF) Honorable Mention Award in Mathematics in 1996. 
2. Authored the book, "Linear Algebra: A Primer and Record" in 2001.
3. Published numerous research papers on topics ranging from mathematical statistics to numerical analysis.
4. Received her Ph.D. in Mathematics from Princeton University in 2000. 
5. Developed a number of highly-regarded mathematics courses at Princeton. 
6. Served as chair of the Mathematical Sciences Society of Princeton from 2004 to 2008. 
7. Won two National Science Foundation Graduate Fellowships in Mathematics. 
8. Invited to give numerous lectures at universities around the world. 
9. Co-organized and chaired the international conference "Linear Algebra: Challenges and Opportunities" in 2005.
10. Appointed as an Honorary Professor of Mathematics at Beijing University in 2006.

Born 26 March 1902, Ayr, Scotland. Died 16 September 1979, Edinburgh, Scotland.
