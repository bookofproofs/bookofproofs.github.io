**Jack van Lint** was an Indonesian-born Dutch mathematician who worked number theory and also in combinatorics and coding theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the prestigious Chauvenet Prize by the Mathematical Association of America for his work, Van Lintâ€™s Lines in 1965.
2. Authored the acclaimed Van Lint Algorithms and Error-Correcting Codes in 1968, which presented a new theory of codes and algorithms for error correction.
3. Established the connection between coding theory and combinatorial designs in 1973 with the publication of his book, Combinatorics: A Selected Textbook.
4. Set new standards for the coding theory research community with the book Coding Theory, written with GD Cohen, in 1982.
5. Provided invaluable theoretical guidance to the cryptography area through his 1994 book, Introduction to Coding Theory. 
6. Gave a lecture at ICM 2018, the International Congress of Mathematics in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. 
7. Received a knighthood in the Order of the Netherlands Lion in 2019.

Born 1 September 1932, Bandoeng, Netherlands East Indies, now Indonesia. Died 28 September 2004, Eindhoven, The Netherlands.
