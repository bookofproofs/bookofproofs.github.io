**Bertha Swirles Jeffreys** was an English physicist who carried out research on quantum theory. She co-authored an important text-book on mathematical physics with her husband.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1.The first woman to be elected a member of the London Mathematical Society in 1931. 
2.The first female to be awarded the Sylvester Medal, the highest honor of the Society, in 1941.
3.Received an honorary doctorate from Girton College in Cambridge in 1948. 
4.Contributed to important mathematical papers on electromagnetic theory, vortex theory and methods of approximation. 
5.Edited the unpublished notebooks of her husband, physicist Arthur Eddington.
6.The Royal Society of London awarded her the Hughes Medal in 1948 “for her distinguished investigations in mathematical physics.” 
7.Published research in several mathematical journals.
8.Served as a lecturer in mathematics at Bryn Mawr College in the United States.

Born 22 May1903, Northampton, England. Died 18 December 1999, Cambridge, England.
