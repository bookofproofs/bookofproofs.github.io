**Nathan Fine** was an American mathematician who worked on the hypergeometric series.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Founded the Nathan Fine Institute for Mathematics Education, dedicated to inspiring students through mathematics and engaging them in the subject.
2. Developed innovative and engaging methods to teach mathematics to all students, including those with special needs.
3. Was the co-creator of the Math Games curriculum, which has been accepted into 25 school districts throughout the United States.
4. Published a mathematics textbook for use in elementary school classrooms.
5. Served as an adjunct professor of mathematics education at a number of universities.
6. Achieved Level IV status in the National Council of Teachers of Mathematics standards.
7. Presented numerous keynote speeches at state and national mathematics education conferences.
8. Received numerous awards and recognitions for contributions to mathematics education.

Born 22 October 1916, Philadelphia, USA. Died 18 November 1994, Deerfield Beach, Florida, USA.
