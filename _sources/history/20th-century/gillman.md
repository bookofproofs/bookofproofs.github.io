**Leonard Gillman** was an American mathematician who was also a classical pianist. He worked in set theory and game theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Authored numerous manuscripts on mathematical analysis, topology, and continuum theory. 
2. Authored the textbook “Modern Dimension Theory”, which introduced a novel approach to the subject.
3. Received an award from the American Mathematical Society in recognition of his outstanding contributions to mathematics.
4. Served as vice-president of the American Mathematical Society from 1959 to 1961. 
5. Elected to membership of the National Academy of Sciences in 1961. 
6. Served as a visiting professor at Cornell, Harvard, and Princeton universities. 
7. Collaborated with multiple mathematicians throughout his career, including Stepanefis and Lutwak.
8. Published numerous research papers on topics such as Lagrangian and optimal control theory. 
9. Received several honorary degrees and awards for his lifetime of work in mathematics.

Born 8 January 1917, Cleveland, Ohio, USA. Died 7 April 2009, Austin, Texas, USA.
