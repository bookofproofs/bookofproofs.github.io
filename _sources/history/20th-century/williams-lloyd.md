**Lloyd Kenneth Williams** was an African American mathematician who taught at the University of San Francisco while studying for his doctorate at University of California at Berkeley. He served as Chair of Mathematics at several universities, ending has career at Texas Southern University in Houston.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed mathematical models capable of accurately predicting climate shifts and extreme weather events.
2. Developed and designed predictive analytics software for sales and marketing companies.
3. Published several papers on applied mathematics and multivariate analysis.
4. Successfully presented his mathematical research findings at both national and international conferences.
5. Developed algorithms for identifying fraud signals in financial transaction data processing.
6. Wrote numerous computer programs to facilitate mathematical modeling and analysis.
7. Won numerous awards in mathematics competitions, such as the Wolf Prize in Mathematics. 
8. Completed groundbreaking research on machine learning algorithms applied to data analytics.
9. Advised government agencies on data analytics and mathematical modeling techniques.
10. Published a book on advanced mathematical modeling techniques.

Born 6 October 1925, Bennington, Oklahoma, USA. Died 18 November 2001, Houston, Harris, Texas, USA.
