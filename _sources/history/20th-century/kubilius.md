**Jonas Kubilius** was a Lithuanian mathematician who worked in probability theory and number theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a computer program to analyze the behavior of nonlinear dynamical systems.
2. Proposed a new concept of the multiple hypotheses extension of the maximum likelihood method. 
3. Established a mathematical model of a behavior optimization process in learning.
4. Contributed to establishing the stochastic and statistical models of sequential decision processes.
5. Authored over 70 research papers and two books on dynamic programming algorithms, mathematical models, artificial intelligence and computer vision.
6. Developed a mathematical model of memory search processes in learning.
7. Published a research on the application of quantum structures for neural networks. 
8. Awarded numerous prizes for his research achievements in mathematics.

Born 27 July 1921, Fermos, Erzvilkas, Jurbarkas, Lithuania. Died 30 October 2011, Vilnius, Lithuania.
