**Lê Văn Thiêm** was a Vietnamese mathematician who became one of the founders of higher education in his country.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Lectured at the University of Hanoi from 1929 to 1930.
2. Developed the theory of binomial coefficients used to calculate probability.
3. Pioneered the research of combinatorial structures and maintained active interest in the field for more than two decades.
4. Founded the first Vietnamese Mathematics Institute in 1937, which focused on mathematical research.
5. Made progress in the theory of matrices and in Algebraic Topology.
6. Founded and supervised the Mathematical Magazine, the first mathematics-focused journal in Vietnam.
7. Co-authored the first mathematics textbook in the country, which was published in 1940.

Born 29 March 1918, Duc Tho District, Ha Tinh Province, Vietnam. Died 3 July 1991, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam.
