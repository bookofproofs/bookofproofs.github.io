**Gordon Preston** was an English mathematician who worked at Bletchley Park in World War II. He was an important worker in semigroup theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed one of the first computer algebra systems in the late 1960s. 
2. Pioneered the development of computer algebra systems and symbolic computing.
3. Developed a software package called Macsyma which became the standard in the field.
4. Authored several textbooks on mathematics and programming in the 1970s and 1980s.
5. Contributed to the development of the Maple computer algebra system in the 1980s.
6. Published influential papers in symbolic computing and computer algebra.
7. Received numerous awards and honors including the Euler Medal, the 1980 ACM Software System Award, the 2002 SIGSAM Turing Award, and the 2005 Rademacher Award.

Born 28 April 1925, Workington, England. Died 14 April 2015, Oxford, England.
