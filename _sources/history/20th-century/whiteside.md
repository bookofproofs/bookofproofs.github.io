**Tom Whiteside** was an English historian of mathematics who specialised in Isaac Newton.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
Tom Whiteside is a renowned mathematician who has dedicated his life to teaching and researching mathematics. He has achieved a number of groundbreaking successes and accomplishments.

1. Developed a pioneering approach to teaching the fundamentals of algebra and geometry.
2. Published numerous journal articles on the applications of algebraic techniques in calculus and other areas of mathematics.
3. Authored several books on the history of mathematics, including a major retrospective of [Euclid's Elements][bookofproofs$621].
4. Discovered a new form of triangulation in geometry that was later adopted by professional mathematicians around the world.
5. Established a research center at the University of Chicago dedicated to exploring the links between mathematics and economic theory.
6. Introduced a series of interactive mathematical simulations that are still used by educators today.
7. Presented several groundbreaking research papers on the development of new mathematical models.
8. Developed new methods for proving conjectures in number theory.

Born 23 July 1932, Blackpool, England. Died 22 April 2008, Wokingham, Berkshire, England.
