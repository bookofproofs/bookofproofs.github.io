**Tosio Kato** was a Japanese mathematician who worked in partial differential equations, mathematical physics and functional analysis.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the theory of finite differences.
2. Pioneered the modern analytic theory of partial differential equations.
3. Coined the term “functional analysis”.
4. Authored the work “Fourier series”.
5. Established the First Japanese School of Mathematics.
6. Made fundamental contributions to various branches of mathematics.
7. Proposed major advances in the study of the Euler-Cauchy equations. 
8. Contributed groundbreaking research in the field of analysis. 
9. Analyzed the solutions of second order linear differential equations with piecewise constant coefficients. 
10. Published the monograph “Differential and Integral Equations”.

Born 25 August 1917, Kanuma City, Tochigi-ken, Japan. Died 2 October 1999, Oakland, California, USA.
