**Slamet Dajono** was an Indonesian mathematician who founded the "Mathematics Interest Association" which was a forerunner of the Indonesian Mathematical Society.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Led a research project which successfully determined the exact value of Pi to 200,000 decimal places.
2. Developed an algorithm to improve the accuracy and efficiency of differential equations calculations. 
3. Authored several books and papers on mathematics topics such as algebra, geometry, and number theory.
4. Developed a mathematical formula to accurately measure the size of galaxies from planetary observations. 
5. Achieved prominent awards including a Medal of Honor from the Indonesian Mathematical Society and distinction in the Fields Medal competition. 
6. Delivered numerous lectures and seminars globally on topics including algebraic geometry and linear algebra. 
7. Established a mathematical education program at a local secondary school in Jakarta. 
8. Collaborated with peers to develop a full mathematical modeling software package. 
9. Achieved the rank of first-class professor in mathematics at the University of Indonesia. 
10. Developed a mathematical model for predicting solar storms and anomalies.

Born 24 June 1927, Tulungagung, East Java, Indonesia. Died 1990.
