**Peter Hilton** was an English mathematician who made important contributions to homotopy theory. He is best known for his text-books and for his work as a code-breaker in World War II.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Co-authored the book Theory of Homotopy Groups (1953) with Jean-Pierre Serre.
2. Received the American Mathematical Society's Leroy P. Steele Prize for lifetime achievement in 2009.
3. Developed the theories of homotopy theory and homology theory.
4. Received the Frederic Esser Nemmers Prize in Mathematics in 2015.
5. Elected to the Royal Society in 1979.
6. Published numerous papers on algebraic topology in the 1950s and 60s.
7. Authored and co-authored several books on algebraic topology.
8. Developed the theory of obstructions to deformation of maps.
9. Established a new area of research, titled cubical homotopy theory.
10. Defined Metric spaces and built the foundation for metric space topology.

Born 7 April 1923, London, England. Died 6 November 2010, Binghampton, New York, USA.
