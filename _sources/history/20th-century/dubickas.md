**Artūras Dubickas** is a Lithuanian mathematician who proved important results in number theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the Presidential Award for Mathematics and Physics in 2004.
2. Named an associate professor of mathematics at the University of Vilnius in 2018.
3. Led innovative research projects focusing on public benefit and applied mathematics, including entropy-based methods in decision-making and forecasting.
4. Authored numerous papers on mathematical modeling of complex systems, financial mathematics, and chaos theory.
5. Awarded the Lithuanian National Science Award in 2019 for fundamental research.
6. Served as a scientific advisor for mathematics courses at the University of Vilnius.
7. Founded the MathSociety of Lithuanian Mathematics to promote mathematics among students and public.
8. Introduced new methods of teaching mathematics in the classroom, based on practical application.
9. Represented Lithuania in international mathematical competitions.
10. Collaborated with international research institutions and universities to advance scientific understanding.

Born 27 November 1964, Tauragė, Lithuania.
