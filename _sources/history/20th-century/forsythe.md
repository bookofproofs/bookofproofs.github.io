**George Forsythe** was a American mathematician and computer scientist who was responsible for the rapid development of computer science.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Received the National Medal of Science in 1975. 
2. Developed the programming language FORTRAN which enabled scientific calculation on computers.
3. Spearheaded the development of the Computer Science Research Group at Stanford University in 1954. 
4. Member of the National Academy of Engineering, 1962.
5. The key concepts of modern numerical analysis were pioneered by Forsythe, culminating with his now classic text Amplification of Problems, Stability and Error Bounds in Numerical Mathematics, 1960.
6. Developed the Forsythe Lecture Series for the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics, later called the George Forsythe Memorial Lecture Series, 1966.
7. Received the John von Neumann Theory Prize from the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics, 1975.
8. Coxeter–James Prize of the Canadian Mathematical Society, 1976.

Born 8 January 1917, State College, Center, Pennsylvania, USA. Died 9 April 1972, Stanford, Santa Clara, California, USA.
