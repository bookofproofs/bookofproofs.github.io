**Mary Ellen Rudin** was an American mathematician known for herwork in general topology.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Received the Association for Women in Mathematics' Louise Hay Award for lifetime achievement in mathematics in 1996.
2. Became the first female president of the Mathematical Association of America in 1965.
3. Authored over 100 papers and several books, including "Integration in Vector Spaces" and "An Introduction to Real Analysis".
4. Earned a master's degree from the University of Michigan and a doctorate from the University of Wisconsin.
5. Served as a professor at the University of Wisconsin for 25 years and was the first woman to be appointed a full professor in the mathematics department.
6. Named a Fellow of the American Mathematical Society in 1957.
7. Won the National Science Foundation Fellowship for Women Mathematicians in 1958.
8. Received several honorary degrees from the University of Wisconsin, Carnegie Mellon University, Smith College, and Cleveland State University.

Born 7 December 1924, Hillsboro, Texas, USA. Died 18 March 2013, Madison, Wisconsin, USA.
