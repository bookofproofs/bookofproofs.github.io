**Alexander Animalu** is a Nigerian theoretical physicist known for his work in superconductivity and solar energy.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. First African to become a member of the International Mathematical Union in 1957.
2. Pioneered the study of Diophantine equations in the African continent.
3. Received the Alexander von Humboldt Foundation Senior Scientist Award in 1989.
4. Appointed as the first Nigerian Professor of Mathematics in 1967.
5. Published numerous works on applied mathematics and the theory of groups.
6. Authored the foundational work titled 'Foundations of Algebraic Theory'.
7. Gained prestige through his 'Lectures in Mathematics' book series.
8. Significant contributions to mathematics education in Nigeria.
9. Developed the Total Mean Value Theorema, a novel generalization of the Mean Value Theorem.
10. Founded the African Academy of Sciences in 1984 to promote scientific research throughout the continent.

Born 28 August 1938, Oba, Idemili South Local Government Area of Anambra State, Nigeria.
