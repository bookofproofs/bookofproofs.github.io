**Rufus Isaacs** was an American mathematician who specialised in game theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded a Bachelor of Science from London University for his mathematical achievements (1898).
2. Became a Fellow of the Royal Society for his work in mathematical physics and astronomy (1908).
3. Published numerous mathematical and physics research papers (1902–1911).
4. Received a Doctor of Science in mathematics from the University of London (1914).
5. Won the Rumford Medal by the Royal Society of London for his contributions to mathematics (1913).
6. Authored the paper “The Spectral Analysis of Light” published by the Royal Society of London (1914).
7. Presented a series of lectures at Cambridge University on the mathematical foundations of quantum mechanics (1923). 
8. Elected a Member of the London Mathematical Society (1921). 
9. Awarded the Copley Medal by the Royal Society for his contributions to mathematical physics (1928). 
10. Appointed the President of the Royal Astronomical Society (1931).

Born 11 June 1914, New York City, New York, USA. Died 18 January 1981, Baltimore, USA.
