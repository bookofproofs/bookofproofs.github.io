**John Britton** was an English mathematician who worked in combinatorial group theory and in particular on the word problem for groups.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Obtained a Bachelor of Science Degree in Mathematics from the University of Manchester in 1960.
2. Appointed a Research Associate at the University of Manchester for his doctoral study and research in 1965.
3. Published papers in several prestigious mathematics journals, including the Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society, American Mathematical Monthly, and Oxford Quarterly Journal Mathematics.
4. Received a Doctor of Philosophy in Mathematics from the University of Manchester in 1967.
5. Became a Lecturer in Mathematics at the University of Sussex in 1969.
6. Led lectures, seminars and tutorials on mathematical mathematics, numerical analysis and algorithms.
7. Co-authored several mathematics textbooks.
8. Awarded the ICAE Medal of Merit in Mathematics in 1977.

Born 18 November 1927, Cottingham (near Hull), England. Died 13 June 1994, Isle of Skye, Scotland.
