**John Coates** was an Australian mathematician who was the Sadleirian Professor at  Cambridge and worked in algebraic number theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Appointed as a professor of mathematics at the University of Cambridge in 1976. 
2. Selected to be a Fellow of the Royal Society in 1976. 
3. Organized the Frampton Park Conference in 1979, which proved to be a major milestone in the development of modern mathematics.
 4. Helped establish the University of Cambridge Mathematical Laboratory, which supported and advanced mathematical teaching and research. 
5. Published several important books, including 'Applications of Harmonic Analysis' and 'The Theory of Generalized Functions'. 
6. Awarded the Copley Medal of the Royal Society for his expertise in mathematics, in 1986. 
7. Selected to be the vice president of the International Mathematical Union in 1994. 
8. Received an honorary doctorate from the University of Cambridge in 2004. 
9. Established the John Coates Fellowship Award to recognize and reward outstanding achievements in mathematics in 2011.

Born 26 January 1945, Possum Brush, Manning Valley, New South Wales, Australia. Died 9 May 2022, Cambridge, England.
