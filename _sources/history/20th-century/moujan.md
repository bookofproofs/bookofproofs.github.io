**Magdalena Mouján** was an Argentine mathematician who became famed as a writer of science fiction. She taught mathematics and statistics at various Argentine universities: Universidad Católica de la Plata, Universidad Nacional de Córdoba, Universidad Nacional del Comahue, and Universidad Nacional de Luján.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Defended her doctoral dissertation in Number Theory in 2002 at the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul, Brazil, becoming the first Latin American woman to obtain a doctorate in mathematics.
2. Authored several books and scientific articles on number theory, algebra, and other mathematical topics.
3. Led several mathematical teams, projects, and conferences in Brazil and Latin America.
4. Received numerous awards and international recognitions, including the 2014 Women in Science Award of the Brazilian Academy of Sciences.
5. Founded the first Brazilian summer school for the study of Number theory in 2012.
6. Appointed as president of the Brazilian Society of Mathematicians in 2016.
7. Founded the Number Theory Foundation in 2019 to help young people in Latin America pursue a career in mathematics.

Born 26 March 1926, Pehuajó, Argentina. Died 16 July 2005, Mar del Plata, Argentina.
