**Dorothy Bernstein** was awarded a Ph.D. in 1939 and had an excellent career overcoming prejudice against her as a woman and as a Jew. She became the first woman president of the Mathematical Association of America.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Co-authored a well-known monograph on abstract algebra entitled 'A Survey of Modern Algebra' (1952) with Garrett Birkhoff.
2. Authored the book 'Noncommutative Algebra' (1956), which became an influential reference work for mathematicians. 
3. Co-founded the Cambridge Conference on Modern Algebra in 1955, which was a gathering of mathematicians that discussed algebraic theories and abstract algebra. 
4. Established a PhD program in mathematics at Brandeis University, her alma mater, in 1958. 
5. Received the American Mathematical Societyâ€™s Ruth Lyttle Satter prize in 1988 for her contributions in mathematics. 
6. Published numerous papers on abstract algebra during her career, including 'Groups with Prescribed Factor Groups' (1946) and 'Totally Skew Fields' (1954). 
7. Served as a professor of mathematics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology from 1946 until her retirement in 1972. 
8. Provided support and mentorship to many female students, which helped to encourage more young women to pursue mathematics. 
9. Was inducted into the National Academy of Science in 1975.

Born 11 April 1914, Chicago, Illinois, USA. Died 5 February 1988, Providence, Rhode Island, USA.
