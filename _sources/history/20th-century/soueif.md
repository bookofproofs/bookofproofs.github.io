**Laila Soueif** is an Egyptian mathematician who became a professor of mathematics at Cairo University. She is an activist for human rights and has been involved in many demonstrations including calling for academic freedom at Cairo University.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Completed her undergraduate studies in mathematics from Cairo University in 1978 and received a master's degree in mathematics from the same university in 1981.
2. Received the State Merit Award for her Masters Degree in Math.
3. Was an honorary Research Fellow at the Faculty of Mathematics, Cairo University from 1981 to 1992.
4. Published a research paper titled “A Survey on Subdifferential Operators in Finite-Dimensional Normed Spaces”.
5. Won the 2009 Thirteenth Annual Blaise Pascal International Award presented by UNESCO and the International Council of Science.
6. Received Women Scientist Medal in 2010 from the National Council for Women in Egypt.

Born 1 May1956, London, England.
