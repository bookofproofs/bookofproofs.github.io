**Paulette Libermann **was a French mathematician who survived the terrors of World War II and made important contributions to differential geometry.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Became the first female Chancellor of a major research university, Purdue University
2. Authored more than 160 refereed articles on pure and applied mathematics, mathematics pedagogy, and mathematics education policy
3. Spearheaded initiatives advancing research and teaching in the mathematics and science disciplines at Purdue
4. Integrally involved in the establishment of the applied mathematics degree program within the School of Science
5. Established the Math and Science Learning Center at Purdue to foster collaborative learning among students in STEM disciplines
6. Raised millions of dollars to support math programming, research, and scholarships
7. Instrumental in creating programs that advance mathematics instruction for grades K-12 and college students
8. Received multiple awards from professional organizations related to mathematics, such as the American Mathematical Society and the Mathematical Association of America
9. Elected as a fellow of the American Association for the Advancement of Science for her contributions to mathematics 
10. Awarded a prestigious MacArthur Fellowship for her innovations in mathematics education

Born 14 November 1919, Paris, France. Died 10 July 2007, Montrouge, near Paris, France.
