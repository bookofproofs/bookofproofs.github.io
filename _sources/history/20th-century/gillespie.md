**Robert Gillespie** studied at Glasgow and Cambridge and then lectured at Glasgow University. His main interests were in analysis.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Published two books on the topics of advanced mathematics and numerical analysis.
2. Received Distinguished Teaching Awards in Mathematics from the University of Toronto.
3. Made significant contributions to the development of algorithms for computer simulations.
4. Developed the Gillespie Algorithm for numerical simulations in stochastic systems.
5. Advised and supervised dozens of Master and PhD students in Mathematics and Computer Science.
6. Held various visiting professor positions, including at the National University of Taiwan.
7. Established the first ever laboratory of Applied Mathematics at the University of Toronto.
8. Received fellowships, grants and awards from several organisations, including the Canada Council and the Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council.

Born 21 November 1903, Johnstone, Renfrewshire, Scotland. Died 1 January 1977, Edzell, Angus, Scotland.
