**Lee  Lorch** was an American mathematician who was an early civil rights activist, and communist.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Became the first African American to receive a Ph.D. in Mathematics from an American university in 1941.
2. Was among the first African Americans to integrate a graduate school program in mathematics when he enrolled at Harvard University in 1939.
3. Was a professor at Fisk University who fought to end segregation in graduate schools.
4. Appointed to the faculty of the City College of New York in 1952, becoming the first African American on its faculty.
5. Published numerous scholarly works throughout his career including two books on linear programming theory and numerical methods. 
6. Mentored and encouraged other African American mathematicians to pursue their studies, including Marjorie Lee Browne and Euphemia Lofton Haynes. 
7. Founded the Brooklyn College Mathematics Department in 1953 and served as its chair until his retirement in 1973. 
8. Involved in a landmark court case, Lee v. Mississippi (1968), which declared that segregation of public universities was illegal.

Born 20 September 1915, New York City, USA. Died 28 February 2014, Toronto, Canada.
