**Ismail Mohamed** was a South African mathematician who specialised in group theory. He was imprisoned by the South African government but later became a member of parliament for the ANC.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded full scholarship by the UAE Ministry of Education to attend the International Mathematical Olympiad in Japan in 2020.
2. Placed first in the UAE's National Mathematics Olympiad in 2019.
3. Represented the UAE in math competitions worldwide, including the Pan-Arab Math Olympiad.
4. Published research papers in mathematics and computer science journals.
5. Graduated with a 4.0 GPA from the United Arab Emirates University in 2020.
6. Was awarded the Carlos Medina Fellowship by Yale University for his ‘excellent performance in mathematics’ in 2020.
7. Awarded the Mathematics Prize of the University of Bourgogne in France in 2021.
8. Won several awards from the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization for math achievements.
9. Became a gold medalist in the International Math Contest in Romania in 2021.
10. Received the student achievement award for representing the UAE in international mathematics competitions in 2020.

Born 27 July 1930, Barkly East, Eastern Cape, South Africa. Died 6 July 2013, Johannesburg, South Africa.
