**Charles Fefferman** is an American mathematician who won a Fields Medal for work on partial differential equations and Fourier analysis.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the Fields Medal in 1978 for his work on Foams, partial different equations, and for his contributions to mathematics in general. 
2. Became the youngest professor in Princeton University's history when he was appointed as a professor of mathematics in 1977 at the age of 23.
3. Was awarded the National Medal of Science in 1990 for his lifetime of meritorious achievement in mathematics.
4. Was the recipient of the Wolf Prize in Mathematics in 2004 for his contributions to analysis and geometry.
