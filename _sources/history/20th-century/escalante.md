**Jaime Escalante** was a high school mathematics teacher in both his native Bolivia and in the United States. He became famous when his students became so successful they were accused of cheating, leading to the 1988 film 'Stand and Deliver'.




### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a groundbreaking mathematics program for high school students at Garfield High School in East Los Angeles. 
2. Reinvigorated a demoralized educational system by teaching students algebra and trigonometry. 
3. Inspired countless students to develop a deep appreciation for mathematics and pursue studies in related fields.
4. Lead Garfield High School to become one of the top-ranked schools in California for mathematics and science.
5. Saw twelve of his students pass the Advanced Placement Calculus tests in 1982. 
6. Helped to popularize math as a rewarding subject to learn and master, through the feature film Stand and Deliver.

Born 31 December 1930, La Paz, Bolivia. Died 30 March 2010, Roseville, California, USA.
