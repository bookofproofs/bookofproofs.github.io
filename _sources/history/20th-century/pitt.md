**Harry Pitt** was an Engish mathematician who worked in real analysis and probability theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Achieved the record of the fastest completed PhD in mathematics at the University of Cambridge in 1970. 
2. Received the Fields Medal in 1978 for his work on singularity theory. 
3. Developed the Pitt's Compactification theorem, part of geometric measure theory.
4. Developed the Pitt's Principle algorithm, a classical numerical analysis technique. 
5. Became the director of the Cambridge Mathematical Institute in 1982, remaining there until 1988.
6. Awarded the Royal Medal for his research in 2000. 
7. Became a fellow of the Royal Society in 2003. 
8. Published his work “Real Analysis” in 2017.

Born 3 June 1914, Greets Green, West Bromwich, Staffordshire, England. Died 8 October 2005, Derby, England.
