**Mary Gray** is an American mathematician, statistician, and lawyer. She has written on mathematics, education, computer science, statistics and academic freedom.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the first computer software package to facilitate the teaching of mathematics. 
2. Pioneered the development of interactive computer-assisted instruction in mathematics. 
3. Published several books and journal articles on the use of computers in mathematics education. 
4. Presented at mathematics and computer science conferences. 
5. Helped develop three major software packages in mathematics education. 
6. Serving as the Director of the Mathematical Sciences Institute at the University of Virginia.
7. Recipient of awards and recognition from various mathematics and computer science organizations. 
8. Collaborated with other mathematics experts to improve standardized math test scores. 
9. Developed game-based activities to motivate student interest in mathematics. 
10. Supervised PhD students in the field of mathematics education.

Born 4 April 1939, Hastings, Nebraska, USA.
