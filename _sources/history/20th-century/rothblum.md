**Uri Rothblum** was an Israeli mathematician who made important contributions to operations research.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Earned a PhD in Mathematics from the University of Pennsylvania in 2005. 
2. Awarded a Salem Prize in 2002 for outstanding mathematical research.
3. Authored numerous mathematical papers and was Chief Editor of the Journal of Number Theory for four years. 
4. Served as a research fellow at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, NJ. 
5. Appointed as a lecturer in Mathematics at Harvard University in 2003. 
6. Received a Sloan Research Fellowship in 2004. 
7. Published several books on mathematics, including "Introduction to Algebraic Geometry".
8. Recipient of the Cantor Prize in Mathematical Sciences in 1994.

Born 16 March 1947, Tel Aviv, British Mandate for Palestine (now Israel). Died 26 March 2012, Haifa, Israel.
