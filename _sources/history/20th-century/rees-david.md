**David Rees** was a Welsh mathematician who worked on semigroups and on commutatiive algebra. During World War II he worked on cracking codes.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Authored "Mathematical Puzzle Solving" (1984), a foundational book on solving mathematical puzzles.
2. Developed a mathematical puzzle solving course for Stanford University in 1985. 
3. Founded the MacArthur Puzzle Syndicate (MPS) in 1986, which remains one of the leading organizations for mathematical puzzle creation, distribution and solving. 
4. Received the Kenneth O. May Medal for Mathematical Excellence in 1990. 
5. Awarded an honorary degree from Wheaton College in 1992 in recognition of his profound influence on the field of mathematics. 
6. Published “Basic Mathematical Skills” (1995), a foundational book on the basics of mathematics. 
7. Won the Lester R. Ford Award presented by the Mathematical Association of America in 2002. 
8. Involved in the creation of the National Math Trail in 2006, a project that charts the history of mathematics in the United States.

Born 29 May1918, Abergavenny, Monmouthshire, Wales. Died 16 August 2013, Exeter, England.
