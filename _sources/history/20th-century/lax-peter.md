**Peter Lax** is a Hungarian mathematician who works on scattering theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Received the National Medal of Science in 1984 for his work on the mathematical theory of shock waves and conservation laws. 
2. Received the Wolf Prize in mathematics in 2011 for his work in classical analysis, linear and non-linear partial differential equations, and applied mathematics. 
3. Introduced many fundamental concepts and techniques to the study of nonlinear partial differential equations and the modern theory of hyperbolic conservation laws. 
4. Made foundational contributions to the theory of mathematical optimization, in particular with his book Linear Analysis and Optimization. 
5. Named John von Neumann Lecturer in 1964 and became a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences in 1971. 
6. Won the Bôcher Memorial Prize in 1967 and Fermat Prize in 1993. 
7. Received the Steele Prize for Seminal Contribution to Research, John von Neumann Prize, and Abel Prize in 2005. 
8. Published over 175 papers, many of which are considered classics, and co-authored five books.

Born 1 May 1926, Budapest, Hungary.
