**Clarence Francis Stephens** was the ninth African American to be awarded a Ph.D. in mathematics. He developed what became known as the 'Morgan-Potsdam Miracle' method of teaching, based on problem solving with very few lectures.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Successfully identified a computational algorithm and established a corresponding set of computational aids.
2. Developed a comprehensive program of mathematical education which enabled students to achieve better results in their studies.
3. Developed innovative approaches to teaching mathematics, including the use of computers and other electronic devices to augment instruction.
4. Pioneered the development of manipulative materials to aid in teaching concepts in mathematics.
5. Developed a comprehensive evaluation program for mathematics teachers.
6. Established an improved system for the maintenance and organization of mathematics libraries.
7. Published several books and papers on mathematics and educational topics, contributing to the advancement of the field.

Born 24 July 1917, Gaffney, South Carolina, USA. Died 5 March 2018, Rochester, New York, USA.
