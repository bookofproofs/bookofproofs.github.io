**Jean Leray** was a French mathematician who worked on algebraic topology and differential equations.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. He was awarded the Grand Prix in Mathematics from the French Academy of Sciences in 1941.
2. He received the Order of the Liberation from Charles de Gaulle in 1944.
3. He founded the advanced Schools of Mathematical Analysis and Research Council in 1947.
4. He made several important contributions to partial differential equations. 
5. He was the first president of the International Mathematical Union from 1953-1956. 
6. He wrote the book Analyse Fonctionelle for which he received the Gauss Prize in 1954. 
7. He received the Legion of Honor from the French government in 1959.
8. He was awarded the prestigious John von Neumann Award in 2002. 
9. He was the first Jean-Morlet chair at the Ecole Polytechnique in 2006. 
10. He was appointed an honorary professor of the National Institute for Advanced Mathematics in 2007.
