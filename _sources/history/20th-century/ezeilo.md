**James Ezeilo** was a Nigerian mathematician who worked in the theory of ordinary differential equations. He was the first professor of mathematics in Nigeria and went on to become vice chancellor of two Nigerian universities.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. First Nigerian to obtain a Ph.D in Mathematics from University College Dublin.
2. Led research projects to design efficient algorithms for solving optimization problems in Nigeria.
3. Pioneered applications for mathematics in business in Nigeria.
4. Helped create Nigeria's National Mathematical Centre (NMC).
5. Conducted numerous lectures and seminars for aspiring mathematicians in Nigeria.
6. Awarded the Order of the Federal Republic (OFR) by the Nigerian government for his exemplary achievements in the field of mathematics.
7. Served as Director of the Nigerian Institute of International Affairs and Chairman of the Governing Council of the University of Abuja.
8. Founder of the Centre for Advancement of Higher Education in Developing Countries.

Born 17 January 1930, Nanka, Orumba North Local Government Area, Anambra State, Nigeria. Died 4 January 2013, Lagos, Nigeria.
