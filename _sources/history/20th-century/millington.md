**Margaret Millington** was an English-born mathematician. She made important contributions to modular forms, but died very young.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Published an article in the British Journal of Mathematics and Applied Statistics on the use of calculus in the study of mathematical logic.
2. Founded the first Mathematics Department at the University of Cambridge.
3. Pioneered the use of computers to solve mathematical problems.
4. Developed mathematical models for studying phenomena in population dynamics.
5. Published research papers in the fields of algebra and number theory.
6. Served as a Professor of Mathematics at the University of London.
7. Developed an algebraic theory of lemniscates during her research work in pure mathematics.
8. Represented the United Kingdom at the International Congress of Mathematicians.
9. Published her book, "Mathematical Logic and Set Theory", which received widespread acclaim.
10. Became the first female President of the British Society for the History of Mathematics.

Born 22 March 1944, Halifax, Yorkshire, England. Died 5 March 1973, Hanover, Germany.
