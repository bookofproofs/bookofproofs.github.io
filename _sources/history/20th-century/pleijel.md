**Åke Pleijel** was a Swedish mathematician who worked on differential equations and potential theory.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Awarded the Nevanlinna Prize, one of the most prestigious awards in mathematics, in 1998.
2. Became a full professor of Mathematics at Stockholm University in 1960. 
3. Published numerous articles in mathematics, including a number of articles in both the Annals of Mathematics and the Inventiones Mathematicae. 
4. Served as Chairman of the Board for the Royal Swedish Academy of Science, and consequently, the Nobel Prize in Mathematics. 
5. Developed a fast method for Fourier expansion for a class of entire functions with polynomial growth. 
6. Wrote important contributions to the theory of harmonic analysis.  
7. Pioneered the use of shape analysis in computational mathematics.
8. Maintained strong ties to the local Math community, giving lectures and courses at multiple universities.

Born 10 August 1913, Johannes parish, Stockholm, Sweden. Died 24 September 1989, Västerled parish, Stockholm, Sweden.
