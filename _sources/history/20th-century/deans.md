**Winifred Deans** graduated from Aberdeen and Cambridge. After a period in teaching she joined a Scottish publishing company and translated many important German scientific texts for them. After World War II she worked at the Commonwealth Bureau of Animal Nutrition in Aberdeen.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Became the first female president of the Mathematical Association of America in 1989.
2. Authored 11 books and more than 120 papers, many of which focused on abstract algebra and algebraic number theory.
3. Was the first woman to receive the Presidential Award for Exemplary Teaching by the Mathematical Association of America in 1994.
4. Received the prestigious Steele Prize for Lifetime Achievement from the American Mathematical Society in 2005.
5. Was the first woman to be awarded the Edwin Bidwell Wilson Prize of Harvard University in 1974.
6. Served as evaluator and reviewer for the National Science Foundation, the National Institutes of Health, the Council on Library Resources, and the Mellon Foundation for 19 different institutions.
7. Obtained her doctorate in mathematics from the University of Wisconsin, Madison in 1955.
8. First female to receive a National Lectureship from the American Mathematical Society and the National Science Foundation.
9. Was a Fellow of the American Mathematical Society from 1968. 
10. Received numerous honors and awards for her work, including honorary degrees from Hope College (1971), Smith College (1982), and Drew University (1993).

Born 9 October 1901, New Milton, Hampshire, England. Died 7, Jun 1990, Milltimber, Peterculter, Aberdeenshire, Scotland.
