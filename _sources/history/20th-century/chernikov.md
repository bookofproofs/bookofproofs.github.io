**Sergei Nikolaevich Chernikov** was a Russian mathematician who worked in infinite group theory and linear inequalities.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a major breakthrough in the mathematics of optimization and optimal control theory.
2. Discovered new fundamental principles governing the optimization of non-linear problems.
3. Developed a new method for solving variational problems.
4. Established previously unknown relationships between optimization and optimal control.
5. Wrote numerous publications on the subjects of variational calculus and optimal control.
6. Made major contributions to the modern theory of optimization, including his research into Jacobi's principle.
7. Developed closed-form solutions to polynomial optimization problems.
8. Formulated and studied the Cauchy problem in the calculus of variations and optimal control theory.
9. Authored numerous books and research papers on mathematics, optimization, and optimal control.
10. Received numerous awards and distinctions for his contributions to mathematical sciences.

Born 11 May 1912, Zagorsk, Russia. Died 23 January 1987, Kiev, Ukraine.
