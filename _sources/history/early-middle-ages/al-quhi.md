**Al-Quhi** was an Islamic mathematician who was a leading figure in the revival and continuation of Greek geometry.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a system for solving third-degree equations.
2. Established the first school for teaching mathematics in Baghdad.
3. Developed generalizations of Euclidean geometry.
4. Studied spherical geometry and trigonometry.
5. Derived accurate trigonometric tables for calculating sine and cosine values.
6. Developed an algebraic formula for calculating amicable numbers.
7. Introducing a new and far more efficient method for extracting roots.
8. Developed the concept of reducing an equation to its simplest form.

Born about 940, Tabaristan (now Mazanderan), Persia (now Iran). Died about 1000.
