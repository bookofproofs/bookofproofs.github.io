**Mahāvīra** was an Indian mathematician who extended the mathematics of Brahmagupta.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the concept of 'caha' which is the metaphorical concept of zero. 
2. Developed the idea of basic concepts of algebra, including equations and solutions. 
3. Is credited for devising a system for expressing numbers up to 10 to the power 54.
4. Developed the concept of fractions.
5. Provided the theory of rates and ratios. 
6. Developed the theory of basic operations of multiplication, division and subtraction.
7. Introduced the concepts of algebraic equations to solve practical problems. 
8. Developed the basic principle of using of negative numbers in counting. 
9. Developed the mathematical principles of quadratic equations and cubical equations. 
10. Developed the concept of permutations and combinations.

Born about 800, possibly Mysore, India. Died about 870, India.
