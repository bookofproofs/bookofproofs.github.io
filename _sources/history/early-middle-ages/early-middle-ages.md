## Chronology

### 500 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Yativrsabha/">Yativrsabha</a>
: A Jaina mathematician who gave a description of the universe which is of historical importance in understanding Jaina science and mathematics.

### 505 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Varahamihira/">Varahamihira</a>
: An Indian astrologer whose main work was a treatise on mathematical astronomy which summarised earlier astronomical treatises. He discovered a version of Pascal's triangle and worked on magic squares.

### 580 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Wang_Xiaotong/">Wang Xiaotong</a>
: A Chinese mathematician and astronomer who was involved in calendar reform and the solution of cubic equations. 

### 598 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Brahmagupta/">Brahmagupta</a>
: Brahmagupta was the foremost Indian mathematician of his time. He made advances in astronomy and most importantly in number systems including algorithms for square roots and the solution of quadratic equations.

### 600 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Bhaskara_I/">Bhaskara I</a>
: An Indian mathematician who wrote commentaries on the work of Aryabhata I.

### 602 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Li_Chunfeng/">Li Chunfeng</a>
: A Chinese mathematician who worked in astronomy and calendar reform. 

### 720 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Lalla/">Lalla</a>
: An Indian mathematician who wrote mainly on the application of mathematics to astronomy.

### 735 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Alcuin/">Alcuin</a>
: An English mathematician who wrote elementary texts on arithmetic, geometry and astronomy. 

### 790 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Khwarizmi/">Abu Ja'far Muhammad ibn Musa Al-Khwarizmi</a>
: An Islamic mathematician who wrote on Hindu-Arabic numerals. The word algorithm derives from his name. His algebra treatise "Hisab al-jabr w'al-muqabala" gives us the word algebra and can be considered as the first book to be written on algebra.

### 800 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Jawhari/">al-Abbas ibn Said Al-Jawhari</a>
: Al-Jawhari was an Islamic mathematician whose main work was a commentary on [Euclid's Elements][bookofproofs$621].

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Banu_Musa/">Banu Musa brothers</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Banu_Musa_Muhammad/">Jafar Muhammad Banu Musa</a>
: Jafar Muhammad Banu Musa was one of the three Bana Musa brothers. He wrote about conics. 

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Govindasvami/">Govindasvami</a>
: An Indian mathematical astronomer whose most famous treatise was a commentary on work of Bhaskara I.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Mahavira/">Mahāvīra</a>
: An Indian mathematician who extended the mathematics of Brahmagupta. 

### 805 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Banu_Musa_Ahmad/">Ahmad Banu Musa</a>
: One of the three Bana Musa brothers. He wrote about mechanics. 

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Kindi/">Abu Yusuf Yaqub ibn Ishaq al-Sabbah Al-Kindi</a>
: Al-Kindi was an Islamic mathematician who wrote on the Indian mumber system as well as geometry and optics. 

### 808 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Hunayn/">Hunayn ibn Ishaq</a>
: A Nestorian Christian mathematician who is most important as a translator, making Greek works available to the Islamic mathematicians.

### 810 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Banu_Musa_al-Hasan/">Banu Musa, al-Hasan</a>
: Al-Hasan Banu Musa was one of the three Bana Musa brothers. He wrote about the ellipse. 

### 820 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Mahani/">Al-Mahani, Abu Abd Allah Muhammad ibn Isa</a>
: Al-Mahani was an Islamic mathematician who tried to solve some of Archimedes' problems on cutting up spheres.

### 830 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Prthudakasvami/">Prthudakasvami</a>
: An Indian mathematician best known for his work on solving equations.

### 835 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Ahmed/">Ahmed ibn Yusuf</a>

### 836 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Thabit/">Thabit ibn Qurra, Al-Sabi</a>
: Thabit ibn Qurra was an important Islamic mathematician who worked on number theory, astronomy and statics. 

### 840 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Sankara/">Sankara Narayana</a>
: An Indian astronomer and mathematician. He wrote a commentary on the work of Bhaskara I.#

### 850 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Abu_Kamil/">Abu Kamil Shuja ibn Aslam</a>
: An Islamic mathematician. He was one of Al-Khwarismi's successors and applied algebraic methods to geometric problems. 

### 868 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Battani/">Al-Battani, Abu Abdallah Mohammad ibn Jabir</a>
: Al-Battani or Albategnius was an Islamic astronomer and mathematician. He made important accurate measurements of the stars, moon and planets. His measurements and methods were used by later astronomers.

### 870 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Sridhara/">Sridhara</a>
: An Indian mathematician who wrote on practical applications of algebra and was one of the first to give a formula for solving quadratic equations. 

### 875 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Nayrizi/">Al-Nayrizi, Abu'l Abbas al-Fadl ibn Hatim</a>

### 880 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Sinan/">Sinan ibn Thabit ibn Qurra, Abu</a>
: Sinan ibn Thabit was the son and father of other Islamic mathematicians. He is more important for his contributions to medicine than to mathematics. 

### 900 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Khazin/">Al-Khazin, Abu Jafar Muhammad ibn Al-Hasan</a>
: An Islamic mathematician who worked on number theory and astronomy. 

### 908 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Ibrahim/">Ibrahim ibn Sinan</a>

### 920 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Uqlidisi/">Al-Uqlidisi, Abu'l Hasan Ahmad ibn Ibrahim</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Aryabhata_II/">Aryabhata II</a>
: An Indian mathematician who wrote about astronomy as well as geometry. He constructed tables of sines accurate up to about 5 figures. 

### 940 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Abul-Wafa/">Al-Buzjani,Mohammad Abu'l-Wafa</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Khujandi/">Al-Khujandi, Abu Mahmud Hamid ibn al-Khidr</a>
: An Islamic astronomer and mathematician. He made accurate astronomical observations and may have made some advances in trigonometry. 

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Quhi/">Al-Quhi, Abu Sahl Waijan ibn Rustam</a>
: An Islamic mathematician who was a leading figure in the revival and continuation of Greek geometry. 

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Vijayanandi/">Vijayanandi</a>
: An Indian mathematician and astronomer who made some contributions to trigonometry.

### 945 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Sijzi/">Al-Sijzi, Abu Said Ahmad ibn Muhammad</a>
: An Islamic astronomer and mathematician who wrote on the geometry of spheres. 

### 946 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Gerbert/">Gerbert of Aurillac</a>
: Gerbert of Aurillac or Pope Sylvester II was a talented French mathematics teacher who popularised the Indo-Arabic numerals. 

### 950 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Yunus/">Ibn Abd al-Rahman Ibn Yunus, Abu'l-Hasan Ali</a>

### 953 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Karaji/">Al-Karaji, Abu Bekr ibn Muhammad ibn Al-Husayn</a>
: An Islamic mathematician who wrote about the work of earlier mathematicians and who can be regarded as the first person to free algebra from geometrical operations and replace them with the type of operations which are at the core of algebra today. 

### 965 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Haytham/">Ibn al-Haytham, Abu Ali Al-Hasan</a>
: Al-Haytham is also known as Alhazen. He was an Islamic mathematician who wrote early works on optics as well as geometry and number theory. 

### 970 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Mansur/">Mansur, Abu Nasr ibn Ali ibn Iraq</a>

### 971 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Kushyar/">ibn Labban, Kushyar</a>
: A Persian astronomer who wrote an early work on Hindu numerals.

### 973 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Biruni/">Al-Biruni, Abu Arrayhan Muhammad ibn Ahmad</a>
: One of the major figures of Islamic mathematics. He contributed to astronomy, mathematics, physics, medicine and history. 

### 980 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Baghdadi/">Al-Baghdadi, Abu Mansur ibn Tahir</a>
: An Islamic mathematician who wrote about different systems of arithmetic in a work of great importance in the history of mathematics. 

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Avicenna/">Avicenna, Abu Ali al-Husain ibn Abdallah ibn Sina</a>

### 989 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Al-Jayyani/">Al-Jayyani, Abu Abd Allah Muhammad ibn Muadh</a>
