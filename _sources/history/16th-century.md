 1500|"Nicolo Tartaglia":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/nicolo-tartaglia Nicolo Tartaglia was born 1500 in Brescia, Republic of Venice (now Italy). Tartaglia was an Italian mathematician who was famed for his algebraic solution of cubic equations which was eventually published in Cardan's Ars Magna. Nicolo Tartaglia died on 13 December 1557 in Venice, Republic of Venice (now Italy)./||
 1501|"Girolamo Cardan":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/girolamo-cardano Girolamo Cardano was born on 24 September 1501 in Pavia, Duchy of Milan (now Italy). Girolamo Cardan or Cardano was an Italian doctor and mathematician who is famed for his work Ars Magna which was the first Latin treatise devoted solely to algebra. In it he gave the methods of solution of the cubic and quartic equations which he had learnt from Tartaglia. Girolamo Cardano died on 21 September 1576 in Rome (now Italy)./||
 1502|"Pedro Nunes Salaciense":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/pedro-nunes-salaciense Pedro Nunes Salaciense was born 1502 in Alcácer do Sal, Portugal. Pedro Nunes or Nunez was a Portuguese scholar who worked in geometry, spherical trigonometry, algebra as well as geography, physics, and cosmology. Pedro Nunes Salaciense died on 11 August 1578 in Coimbra, Portugal./||
 1506|"Frederico Commandino":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/frederico-commandino Frederico Commandino was born 1506 in Urbino, Italy. Frederico Commandino was an Italian mathematician who published important translations of works of the Ancient Greek mathematicians. Frederico Commandino died on 5 September 1575 in Urbino, Italy./||
 1508|"Regnier Gemma Frisius":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/regnier-gemma-frisius Regnier Gemma Frisius was born on 8 December 1508 in Dokkum, Friesland, The Netherlands. Regnier Gemma Frisius was a Dutch mathematician who applied his mathematical expertise to geography, astronomy and map making. He became the leading theoretical mathematician in the Low Countries. Regnier Gemma Frisius died on 25 May 1555 in Louvain, Brabant (now Belgium)./||
 1510|"Robert Recorde":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/robert-recorde Robert Recorde was born 1510 in Tenby, Wales. Recorde was a Welsh doctor and mathematician who invented the "equals" symbol '=' which appears in his book The Whetstone of Witte (1557). Robert Recorde died in 1558 in London, England./||
 1512|"Gerardus Mercator":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/gerardus-mercator Gerardus Mercator was born on 5 March 1512 in Rupelmonde, Burgundian Netherlands (now Belgium). Gerardus Mercator was a Flemish map-maker and geographer who is best known for the map projection which bears his name. Gerardus Mercator died on 2 December 1594 in Duisburg, Duchy of Cleves (now Germany)./||
 1514|"Georg Joachim von Lauchen Rheticus":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/georg-joachim-von-lauchen-rheticus Georg Joachim von Lauchen Rheticus was born on 16 February 1514 in Feldkirch, Austria. Georg Joachim Rheticus was an Austrian mathematician and astronomer who published the trigonometrical sections of Copernicus's De Revolutionibus. Georg Joachim von Lauchen Rheticus died on 4 December 1574 in Kassa, Hungary (now Kosice)./||
 1515|"Peter Ramus":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/peter-ramus Peter Ramus was born 1515 in Cuts (near Noyon), Vermandois, France. Peter Ramus was a French mathematician who wrote a whole series of textbooks on logic and rhetoric, grammar, mathematics, astronomy, and optics. Peter Ramus died on 26 August 1572 in Paris, France./||
 1522|"Lodovico Ferrari":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/lodovico-ferrari Lodovico Ferrari was born on 2 February 1522 in Bologna, Papal States (now Italy). Ferrari was an Italian scholar who started as Cardan's secretary and went on to devise the solution of the quartic equation. Lodovico Ferrari died on 5 October 1565 in Bologna, Papal States (now Italy)./||
 1526|"Rafael Bombelli":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/rafael-bombelli Rafael Bombelli was born on January 1526 in Bologna, Papal States (now Italy). Rafael Bombelli was an Italian mathematician who wrote an influential algebra text and made free use of both negative numbers and complex numbers. Rafael Bombelli died in 1572 in (probably) Rome, Papal States (now Italy)./||
 1527|"John Dee":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/john-dee John Dee was born on 13 July 1527 in Tower Ward, London, England. John Dee was an English scholar who wrote on trigonometry, on calendar reform, on navigation, on geography and on astrology. He became astrologer to Queen Mary but was imprisoned for being a magician. John Dee died on 26 March 1609 in Mortlake, London, England./||
 1530|"Giovanni Battista Benedetti":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/giovanni-battista-benedetti Giovanni Battista Benedetti was born on 14 August 1530 in Venice, Venetian States (now Italy). Giovanni Benedetti was an Italian mathematician who anticipated some of Galileo's work on free fall. Giovanni Battista Benedetti died on 20 January 1590 in Turin, Duchy of Savoy (now Italy)./||
 1533|"Cheng Dawei":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/cheng-dawei Cheng Dawei was born 1533 in China. Cheng Dawei was a Chinese mathematician who published the Suanfa tong zong (General source of computational methods). Cheng Dawei died in 1606 in China./||
 1535|"Giambattista Della Porta":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/giambattista-della-porta Giambattista Della Porta was born on 1 November 1535 in Vico Equense (near Naples), Italy. Giambattista della Porta was an Italian scholar who worked on cryptography and also on optics. He claimed to be the inventor of the telescope although he does not appear to have constructed one before Galileo. Giambattista Della Porta died on 4 February 1615 in Naples, Italy./||
 1536|"Egnatio Pellegrino Rainaldi Danti":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/egnatio-pellegrino-rainaldi-danti Egnatio Pellegrino Rainaldi Danti was born on April 1536 in Perugia, Italy.  1537|"Franciscus Barocius":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/franciscus-barocius Franciscus Barocius was born on 9 August 1537 in Candia (now Iráklion), Crete.  1538|"Christopher Clavius":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/christopher-clavius Christopher Clavius was born on 25 March 1538 in Bamberg (now in Germany). Christopher Clavius died on 2 February 1612 in Rome (now in Italy)./||
 1540|"Thomas Allen":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/thomas-allen Thomas Allen was born on 21 December 1540 in Uttoxeter, Staffordshire, England. Thomas Allen was an English mathematician and antiquary important for his large collection of mathematical (and other) manuscripts. Thomas Allen died on 30 September 1632 in Cambridge, England./||
 1540|"Ludolph Van Ceulen":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/ludolph-van-ceulen Ludolph Van Ceulen was born on 28 January 1540 in Hildesheim, Germany. Ludolph Van Ceulen was a German mathematician who is famed for his calculation of $\pi$ to 35 places. In Germany, $\pi$ used to be called the Ludolphine number. Ludolph Van Ceulen died on 31 December 1610 in Leiden, Netherlands./||
 1540|"François Viète":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/francois-viete François Viète was born 1540 in Fontenay-le-Comte, Poitou (now Vendée), France. François Viète was a French amateur mathematician and astronomer who introduced the first systematic algebraic notation in his book In artem analyticam isagoge . He was also involved in deciphering codes. François Viète died on 13 December 1603 in Paris, France./||
 1545|"Guidobaldo Marchese del Monte":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/guidobaldo-marchese-del-monte Guidobaldo Marchese del Monte was born on 11 January 1545 in Pesaro, Italy.  1546|"Tycho Brahe":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/tycho-brahe Tycho Brahe was born on 14 December 1546 in Knutstorp, Skane, Denmark (now Svalöv, Sweden. Tycho Brahe died on 24 October 1601 in Prague, Bohemia (now Czech Republic)./||
 1546|"Thomas Digges":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/thomas-digges Thomas Digges was born 1546 in Wootton, Kent, England. Thomas Digges died on 24 August 1595 in London, England./||
 1546|"Paul Wittich":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/paul-wittich Paul Wittich was born 1546 in Breslau, now Wrocław, Poland.  1547|"Baha' ad-Din al-Amili":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/baha-ad-din-al-amili Baha' ad-Din al-Amili was born on 27 February 1547 in Baalbek, now in Lebanon.  1548|"Giordano Bruno":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/giordano-bruno Giordano Bruno was born 1548 in Nola (near Naples), Italy. Giordano Bruno was an Italian philosopher of science who was eventually burnt in Rome as a heretic. Giordano Bruno died on 17 February 1600 in Rome, Italy./||
 1548|"Pietro Antonio Cataldi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/pietro-antonio-cataldi Pietro Antonio Cataldi was born on 15 April 1548 in Bologna, Papal States (now Italy). Pietro Cataldi was an Italian mathematician who wrote around 30 books on mathematics, and some on other topics. Pietro Antonio Cataldi died on 11 February 1626 in Bologna, Papal States (now Italy)./||
 1548|"Simon Stevin":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/simon-stevin Simon Stevin was born 1548 in Bruges, Burgundian Netherlands (now Belgium). Simon Stevin died on February 1620 in The Hague, Dutch Republic (now Netherlands)./||
 1549|"Sir Henry Savile":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/sir-henry-savile Sir Henry Savile was born on 30 November 1549 in Bradley (near Halifax), Yorkshire, England.  1550|"Michael Mästlin":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/michael-maestlin Michael Mästlin was born on 30 September 1550 in Göppingen, Baden-Würtemberg, Germany.  1550|"John Napier":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/john-napier John Napier was born 1550 in Merchiston Castle, Edinburgh, Scotland. John Napier was a Scottish scholar who is best known for his invention of logarithms, but other mathematical contributions include a mnemonic for formulas used in solving spherical triangles and two formulas known as Napier's analogies. John Napier died on 4 April 1617 in Edinburgh, Scotland./||
 1552|"Jost Bürgi":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/jost-burgi Jost Bürgi was born on 28 February 1552 in Lichtensteig, St Gallen, Switzerland. Jost Bürgi was a Swiss mathematician who discovered logarithms independently of the Scottish mathematician Napier. Jost Bürgi died on 31 January 1632 in Kassel, Hesse-Kassel (now Germany)./||
 1552|"Matteo Ricci":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/matteo-ricci Matteo Ricci was born on 6 October 1552 in Macerata, Papal States (now Italy). Matteo Ricci was an Italian Jesuit who went to China as a missionary and introduced the Chinese to Western mathematics. Matteo Ricci died on 11 May 1610 in Peking, China./||
 1552|"Luca Valerio":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/luca-valerio Luca Valerio was born 1552 in Naples, Italy. Luca Valerio was an Italian mathematician who applied methods of Archimedes to find volumes and centres of gravity of solid bodies. He corresponded with Galileo. Luca Valerio died on 17 January 1618 in Rome, Italy./||
 1555|"João Baptista Lavanha":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/joao-baptista-lavanha João Baptista Lavanha was born 1555 in Lisbon, Portugal. Joao Baptista Lavanha was a Portuguese engineer who wrote on mathematics and navigation. João Baptista Lavanha died on 31 March 1624 in Madrid, Spain./||
 1555|"Giovanni Antonio Magini":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/giovanni-antonio-magini Giovanni Antonio Magini was born on 13 June 1555 in Padua, Venetian States (now Italy). Giovanni Antonio Magini died on 11 February 1617 in Bologna, Papal States (now Italy)./||
 1560|"Thomas Harriot":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/thomas-harriot Thomas Harriot was born 1560 in Oxford, England. Thomas Harriot was an English mathematician who did outstanding work on the solution of equations, recognising negative roots and complex roots in a way that makes his solutions look almost like a present day solution. Thomas Harriot died on 2 July 1621 in London, England./||
 1561|"Henry Briggs":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/henry-briggs Henry Briggs was born on February 1561 in Warleywood, Yorkshire, England. Henry Briggs was was an English mathematician who published tables of Napier's logarithm and was the man most responsible for scientists' acceptance of logarithms. Henry Briggs died on 26 January 1630 in Oxford, England./||
 1561|"Thomas Fincke":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/thomas-fincke Thomas Fincke was born on 6 January 1561 in Flensburg, Schleswig (now Germany). Thomas Fincke was a Danish doctor who wrote a textbook on geometry. Thomas Fincke died on 24 April 1656 in Copenhagen, Denmark./||
 1561|"Bartholomeo Pitiscus":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/bartholomeo-pitiscus Bartholomeo Pitiscus was born on 24 August 1561 in Grünberg, Silesia (now Zielona Góra, Poland). Bartholomeo Pitiscus was a Polish theologian who first coined the word Trigonometry. Bartholomeo Pitiscus died on 2 July 1613 in Heidelberg, Germany./||
 1561|"Adriaan van Roomen":https://www.bookofproofs.org/history/adriaan-van-roomen Adriaan van Roomen was born on 29 September 1561 in Leuven, Spanish Netherlands (now Belgium).
