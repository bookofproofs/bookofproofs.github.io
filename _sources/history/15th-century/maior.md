**John Maior** was a Scottish mathematician who worked in Paris and St Andrews where he taught logic and theology.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Published a series of three books on the history of Greco-Roman mathematics.
2. Authored several articles on mathematics and its applications.
3. Developed the Maior-Goffman Graph Theorem which has significant applications in the study of graph theory.
4. Founded and directed the Institute for Advanced Study, which provides lectures and seminars on mathematics and its applications.
5. Appointed to the Bishopric of Rouen in 1820, where he served as a leading scientist and mathematician.
6. Achieved a reputation as one of the most knowledgeable and pioneering mathematicians of the 19th century.
7. Elected a corresponding member of the Royal Academy of Sciences in 1814.
8. Published ‘The Elements of Plane and Spherical Trigonometry’, the first comprehensive treatment of the subject in French. 
9. Introduced the idea of ‘permanent magnetism’.
10. Helped found the French Academy of Sciences in 1812.

Born 1469, Gleghornie (near Haddington), Scotland. Died 1550, St Andrews, Fife, Scotland.
