**Al-Qalasadi** was a Spanish Islamic mathematician who took the first steps toward the introduction of algebraic symbolism by using letters in place of numbers.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the concept of algebraic symbolism and equations, introducing them to Europe and the rest of the world.
2. Published a pioneering mathematical treatise, ‘The Compendious Book on Calculation by Completion and Balancing’.
3. Developed a method for solving quadratic equations with real coefficients.
4. Introduce the arithmetic operations on decimal fractions and decimal numbers.
5. Was a prominent interpreter of the works of Ptolemy, Euclid and Archimedes.
6. Pioneered the study of Optics, mechanics, and practical mathematics.
7. Recognized for his skills in practical mathematics and for his groundbreaking work in the field of algebra.

Born 1412, Bastah (now Baza, Spain). Died 1486, Béja, Tunisia.
