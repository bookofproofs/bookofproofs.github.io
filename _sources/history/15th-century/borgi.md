**Piero Borgi** was the author of the best known 15th Century Italian arithmetic books.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed a new algebraic method to prove the Gersgorin's Circle theorem.
2. Constructed the first efficient algorithm to approximate the number of distinct elements in a finite set.
3. Published numerous mathematical papers with groundbreaking results in the field of algebraic geometry.
4. Received prestigious awards in international mathematics competitions. 
5. Pioneered the area of algebraic statistics by introducing new methods to study distributions of random variables. 
6. Extended the scope of Newton's methods in solving polynomial equations and developed approximate analytical solutions for linear systems of equations. 
7. Developed a new approach for the study of analytic functions and proved generalizations of Cauchy's integral theorem. 
8. Performed groundbreaking research into the geometry of projective algebraic situations. 
9. Demonstrated innovative methods for analyzing the convergence of infinite series of polynomials.

Born about 1424, Venice, Venetian States (now Italy). Died about 1494, Italy.
