**Adelard** was an English philosopher who translated Arabic mathematical works into Latin.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed the use of Arabic numerals in Europe.
2. Developed the technique of reducing geometric problems to algebraic equations.
3. Wrote the first surviving Latin translation of Euclidâ€™s Elements.
4. Was the first to calculate the circumference of the Earth using a technique of circles and triangles.
5. Proposed a revolutionary idea of calculating the motion of heavenly bodies based not on circular orbits, but on epicycles, an idea used by the astronomer Claudius Ptolemaeus in the 2nd century.
6. Was the first to calculate the position of the planet Venus, by recording its angular distance from both the sun and the stars.
7. Developed the use of iterative methods in algebra.

Born 1075, Bath, England. Died 1160.
