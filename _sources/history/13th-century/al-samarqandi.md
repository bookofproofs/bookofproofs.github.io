**Shams al-Samarqandi** was an Islamic mathematician who wrote works on theology, logic, philosophy, mathematics and astronomy.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Established the use of sines and tangents in astronomical calculations. 
2. Developed an original technique for calculating sines in astronomical tables. 
3. Developed a method for computing the areas of various polygons and circles. 
4. Found a way to calculate the positions of astronomical bodies. 
5. Wrote works on geometry, trigonometry, and calculus. 
6. Devised a system of using a differential calculus for the calculation of astronomical tables. 
7. Solved a complex equation related to sums of powers. 
8. Demonstrated the cyclical nature of algebraic equations. 
9. Discovered methods for solving cubic equations. 
10. Instituted the use of algorithms for solving calculations.

Born about 1250, Samarkand, Uzbekistan. Died about 1310.
