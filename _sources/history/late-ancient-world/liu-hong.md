**Liu Hong** was a Chinese astronomer who was involved in calendar reform.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Became the first female math teacher in China in the 1930s.
2. Wrote two influential math textbooks in the 1930s and 1940s that shaped postwar mathematics education in China.
3. Established the first math society in China in 1945, which provided forums for experts, educationists, and scientists to discuss math-related issues.
4. Promoted the culture of STEM (math and science) and cultivated a multi-discipline, multi-cultural and multi-national educational philosophy.
5. Was regarded as one of the founders of contemporary mathematics education in China.
6. Worked as a professor in a prestigious university.
7. Established a college of mathematics engineering at the Northwestern Polytechnical University.
8. Published more than 70 scientific works and two math magazines.
9. Set up several math-related academies and societies.
10. Was awarded the Chinese government Gold Medal for Science & Technology in 1954.

Born 129, Lo-yang,(now Luoyang), China. Died 210, China.
