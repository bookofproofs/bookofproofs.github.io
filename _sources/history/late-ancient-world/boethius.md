**Boethius** was a Roman mathematician and philosopher who wrote texts on geometry and arithmetic which were used for many centuries during a time when mathematical achievement in Europe was at a remarkable low.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Translated works of classical Greek mathematicians into Latin, such as [Euclid's Elements][bookofproofs$621]
2. Wrote De Institutione Arithmetica, a textbook on number theory
3. Authored De Consolatione Philosophiae, an influential work of logic
4. Developed the quadrivium, a curriculum that connected mathematical disciplines like arithmetic, music, geometry and astronomy
5. Constructed a spherical astrolabe
6. Described the Pythagorean proposition, which related the length of the sides of a right triangle
7. Used logical reasoning to prove theorems in geometry and number theory

Born about 480, in or near Rome, Byzantine Empire (now Italy). Died 524, Pavia, Gothic Empire (now Italy).
