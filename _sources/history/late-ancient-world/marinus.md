**Marinus** succeeded Proclus as head of the Academy at Athens. He published some work on astronomy.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Expanded the decimal system and applied it to fractions.
2. Developed and published the first trigonometric tables.
3. Constructed the first practical navigational instruments and astronomical instruments.
4. Published important work on the trigonometric functions of sines and tangents.
5. Discovered and published a relationship between the arcs and chords of circles.
6. Developed new methods for evaluating square roots, cube roots, and fourth roots.
7. Developed a method for finding the center of gravity of a solid sphere. 
8. Published a treatise on conic sections, and built a geometer's compass.

Born about 450, Neapolis, Palestine (called Shechem in Bible, now Nablus, Israel). Died about 500, possibly Athens, Greece.
