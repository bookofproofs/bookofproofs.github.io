## Chronology

### 10
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Cleomedes/">Cleomedes</a>
: A Greek astronomer who wrote an influential work: "On the Circular Motions of the Celestial Bodies". 

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Heron/">Heron of Alexandria</a>
: An important geometer and worker in mechanics who invented many machines including a steam turbine. His best known mathematical work is the formula for the area of a triangle in terms of the lengths of its sides.

### 60
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Nicomachus/">Nicomachus of Gerasa</a>
: One of the leading members of the Pythagorean School whose Introduction to Arithmetic was the standard arithmetic text for more than 1000 years.

### 70
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Menelaus/">Menelaus of Alexandria</a>
: One of the later Greek geometers who applied spherical geometry to astronomy. He is best known for the so-called Menelaus's theorem. 

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Theon_of_Smyrna/">Theon of Smyrna</a>
: A Greek philosopher who described how prime numbers, geometrical numbers such as squares, progressions, music and astronomy are interrelated. 

### 78 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Zhang_Heng/">Zhang Heng</a>
: A Chinese mathematician, astronomer and geographer. He became chief astrologer and minister under the Emperor An'ti of China. 

### 85 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Ptolemy/">Claudius Ptolemy</a>
: The most influential of Greek astronomers and geographers of his time. He propounded the geocentric theory of the solar system that prevailed for 1400 years.

### 120 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Yavanesvara/">Yavanesvara</a>
: An Indian astrologer who translated an important Greek text on astrology.

### 129 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Liu_Hong/">Liu Hong</a>
: A Chinese astronomer who was involved in calendar reform. 

### 160 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Xu_Yue/">Xu Yue</a>
: A Chinese mathematician who wrote a commentary of the "Nine Chapters on the Mathematical Art". 

### 200 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Diophantus/">Diophantus of Alexandria</a>
: A Greek mathematician, sometimes known as "the father of algebra", who is best known for his "Arithmetica". This had an enormous influence on the development of [number theory][bookofproofs$62].

### 220 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Liu_Hui/">Liu Hui</a>
: A Chinese mathematician who wrote a commentary of the "Nine Chapters on the Mathematical Art". 

### 233 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Porphyry/">Porphyry Malchus</a>
: Porphyry Malchus wrote a commentary on [Euclid's Elements][bookofproofs$621] and a Life of Pythagoras. 

### 240 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Sporus/">Sporus of Nicaea</a>
: A Greek mathematician who worked on the classical problems of squaring the circle and duplicating the cube. 

### 290 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Pappus/">Pappus of Alexandria</a>
: The last of the great Greek geometers and one of his theorems is cited as the basis of modern projective geometry. He wrote commentaries on [Euclid's Elements][bookofproofs$621] and Ptolemy's Almagest.

### 300 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Pandrosion/">Pandrosion</a>

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Serenus/">Serenus</a>
: A Greek geometer who wrote commentaries on other mathematicians' work as well as proving some geometric theorems of his own.

### 335 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Theon/">Theon of Alexandria</a>
: A Greek mathematician who worked in Alexandria as a teacher of mathematics and astronomy. He was the father of the first known female mathematician Hypatia. 

### 370 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Hypatia/">Hypatia of Alexandria</a>
: The first known woman to make a substantial contribution to the development of mathematics. She was killed by a fanatical Christian sect. 

### 400 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Sun_Zi/">Sun Zi</a>
: A Chinese mathematician who wrote "Sunzi suanjing" (Sun Zi's Mathematical Manual). 

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Xiahou_Yang/">Xiahou Yang</a>
: A Chinese mathematician who wrote the text "Xiahou Yang suanjing" (Xiahou Yang's Mathematical Manual). 

### 411 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Proclus/">Proclus Diadochus</a>
: A Greek philosopher who became head of Plato's Academy and is important mathematically for his commentaries on the work of other mathematicians.

### 420 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Domninus/">Domninus of Larissa</a>
: A Greek philosopher whose mathematical work was not appreciated until it was published in the 19th Century. 

### 429 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Zu_Chongzhi/">Zu Chongzhi</a>
: A Chinese mathematician and astronomer. He introduced the approximation 355/113 to $\pi$ which is correct to 6 decimal places. 

### 430 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Zhang_Qiujian/">Zhang Qiujian</a>
: A Chinese mathematician who wrote the text "Zhang Qiujian suanjing" (Zhang Qiujian's Mathematical Manual). 

### 450 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Marinus/">Marinus of Neapolis</a>
: Marinus succeeded Proclus as head of the Academy at Athens. He published some work on astronomy. 

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Zu_Geng/">Zu Geng</a>
: A Chinese mathematician whose greatest achievement was to compute the diameter of a sphere of a given volume. 

### 474 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Anthemius/">Anthemius of Tralles</a>
: The Byzantine architect who replaced the old church of Hagia Sophia at Constantinople. He described the construction of an ellipse with a string fixed at the two foci and he described the focal properties of the parabola. 

### 476 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Aryabhata_I/">Aryabhata the Elder</a>
: An Indian mathematician who wrote the "Aryabhatiya" which summarises Hindu mathematics up to that 6th Century. 

### 480 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Boethius/">Anicius Manlius Severinus Boethius</a>
: Boethius was a Roman mathematician and philosopher who wrote texts on geometry and arithmetic which were used for many centuries during a time when mathematical achievement in Europe was at a remarkable low.

<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Eutocius/">Eutocius of Ascalon</a>
: A Greek mathematician who wrote commentaries on works of Archimedes and Apollonius.

### 490 
<a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Simplicius/">Simplicius</a>
: A Greek mathematician who wrote commentaries on the works of other philosophers and mathematicians including Euclid and <a href="https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Aristotle/">Aristotle</a>.
