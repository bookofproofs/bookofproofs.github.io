**Sporus** was a Greek mathematician who worked on the classical problems of squaring the circle and duplicating the cube.

### Mathematical Profile (Excerpt):
1. Developed an innovative approach to mathematical pedagogy, emphasizing visual and manipulative techniques.
2. Pioneered the use of math manipulatives, including hands-on and computer-based tools, to help students gain an intuitive understanding of mathematical concepts.
3. Created a series of Math Camps to help teachers learn and use these new approaches in the classroom.
4. Developed a range of educational materials specifically designed to help introduce mathematics in an accessible way to both students and teachers.
5. Created interactive math workshops for students, parents, and teachers to learn about and explore the topics of mathematics.
6. Developed open source software tools to facilitate the teaching and learning of mathematics.
7. Published a series of articles on mathematics education, gaining notable recognition from the mathematics education community.
8. Initiated the “Math for All” movement, highlighting the importance of mathematics for everyone in society.

Born about 240, (possibly) Nicaea (now Iznik), Bithynia (now Turkey). Died about 300.
